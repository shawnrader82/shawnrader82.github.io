Jail Wedding & Inmate Marriage Services

Jail Weddings at Detention Facilities Across Southern California

Getting married when one partner is in custody involves paperwork, facility approvals, and coordination that most people have never dealt with before. We handle the logistics—from marriage license guidance and notarized affidavits to scheduling the ceremony at the facility—so you can focus on the commitment itself.

What We Coordinate

  • Marriage license guidance and Affidavit of Inability to Appear (notarized)
  • Inmate marriage application submission and facility approval coordination
  • Licensed wedding officiant for the ceremony at the jail
  • Post-ceremony marriage license filing with the County Recorder

Counties We Serve

  • Los Angeles County — Men's Central Jail, Twin Towers, Pitchess, CRDF, NCCF
  • San Bernardino County — West Valley, Glen Helen, Adelanto, Central Detention
  • Orange County — Theo Lacy, Musick, Central Jail, Santa Ana City Jail
  • Riverside County — Robert Presley, Larry D. Smith, Southwest Detention
  • Ventura County — Todd Road Jail, Ventura County Main Jail
  • Santa Barbara County — Santa Barbara County Jail

How a Jail Wedding Works

  1. Contact us with the inmate's name, facility, and booking number.
  2. We guide you through the marriage license application and required documents.
  3. We notarize the Affidavit of Inability to Appear and submit the marriage request to the facility.
  4. Once approved, we schedule and coordinate the ceremony with a licensed officiant.
  5. After the ceremony, we file the signed marriage license with the County Recorder.
