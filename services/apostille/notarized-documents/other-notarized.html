What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected other notarized documents copies.

Will be accepted

  • Drafted document with clear notarization purpose
  • Signer present with current government-issued photo ID
  • Correct California notarial block (acknowledgment or jurat) attached
  • Specific, factual content (not opinion or contingent statements)
  • Document in English (translation handled separately after apostille)

Will be rejected

  • Pre-signed documents (signer must sign in the notary’s presence)
  • Documents without notarial blocks or with the wrong act type
  • Expired or invalid government photo ID
  • Documents containing only legal advice or unauthorized practice of law content
  • Documents that should use a standard form (POA, affidavit, etc.) — we’ll redirect you

Other Notarized Documents Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

My document doesn’t fit any of the main spoke categories. Can you still help?

Yes — that’s what this page is for. As long as the document needs a California notarization and apostille for foreign use, we can help. Send us the draft and we’ll confirm the right notary act and wording.

How do I know if I need an acknowledgment or a jurat?

Generally: acknowledgments confirm you signed willingly, used for transactional documents like POAs and authorizations. Jurats are used when you swear the contents are true, like affidavits and declarations. We confirm before notarization.

Can I notarize a document that’s in a foreign language?

California notaries must understand the document being notarized. We notarize in English. If the document is in another language, get it translated to English first or include a side-by-side English version for notarization.

What if the destination requires unique wording I’ve never seen?

Send us the requirements before drafting. Many consulates and foreign agencies publish their preferred wording — we’ll confirm whether California can apply that exact wording or if a workaround is needed.

Will you give legal advice on whether my document is appropriate?

No — California notaries cannot give legal advice or recommend specific document types. For legal questions, consult an attorney. We confirm the notarization is technically correct and routable for apostille.

Can you handle documents for multiple destinations in one trip?

Yes, as long as you bring all documents to a single notarization appointment and they’re all eligible for apostille. Different destinations may require different wording, so confirm requirements for each beforehand.
