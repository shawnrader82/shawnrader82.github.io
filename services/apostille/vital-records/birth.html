What this page covers

Birth Certificate Apostille, end to end.

A birth certificate apostille is what most foreign governments require before they will accept a U.S. birth certificate. We confirm you have a current, certified, long-form copy from the correct state vital-records office, then file it with that state's apostille authority — most often the California Secretary of State. If your destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention, we walk you through the consular legalization chain.

Common reasons people need this

When a birth certificate apostille is required.

Immigration & visas

Foreign immigration officers, consulates, and visa programs routinely require an apostilled U.S. birth certificate to confirm identity, nationality, and family relationships. Common examples include long-stay visas, residency applications, work permits, and family reunification cases.

Dual citizenship

Countries that grant citizenship by descent — Italy, Ireland, Germany, Mexico, Poland, and others — almost always require an apostilled long-form birth certificate for the applicant and frequently for parents and grandparents in the lineage.

Marriage & family filings abroad

If you plan to marry, register a partnership, adopt, or establish parental rights in another country, the foreign civil registrar will normally require an apostilled birth certificate to confirm identity and eligibility to marry.

School enrollment overseas

International schools and universities — especially in Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East — frequently require an apostilled birth certificate as part of the enrollment file for minor children or undergraduate applicants.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected birth certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Recent certified long-form copies with a raised seal, color seal, or security paper from the issuing state
  • California birth certificates issued by CDPH or an authorized county recorder
  • Out-of-state birth certificates issued by that state's official vital-records office
  • Amended birth certificates that match the name and details on supporting documents
  • Adoption-related birth certificates issued by the state of finalization

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies, scans, or notarized copies of a birth certificate (apostille goes on the certified copy itself)
  • Hospital souvenir certificates, baptismal certificates, or commemorative records
  • Heavily damaged, laminated, or altered certificates
  • Certificates that no longer have a legible state seal or signature
  • Certificates older than the destination country's accepted issuance window (often 6–12 months)

Good to know

  • Long-form vs short-form: most countries that grant citizenship by descent require the long-form certificate that lists parents.
  • Recency matters: many consulates want a certificate issued within the last 6–12 months.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

Birth Certificate apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

How do I order a new certified birth certificate?

Contact the vital-records office in the state where you were born. California residents can order through the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) or their county recorder. Most other states accept online, mail, or in-person requests. We can tell you the fastest option for your case.

Long-form or short-form — which do I need?

Most countries that grant citizenship by descent and most immigration cases require the long-form (also called full, certified, or vault) version that lists both parents. Short-form abstracts are usually rejected for apostille purposes abroad.

How long does a birth certificate apostille take?

California birth certificate apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization will add several more weeks.

Can you apostille a birth certificate issued in another state?

Yes. Each U.S. state apostilles its own vital records, so a Texas or New York birth certificate must go through that state's Secretary of State. We coordinate ordering and filing directly with the issuing state.

Will the original be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached directly to the certified birth certificate and returned to you by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup. We confirm return method in writing before submission.

Do I need a translation?

The apostille itself is not translated. Whether the underlying birth certificate needs a certified translation depends on the destination country. We can recommend a certified translator after the apostille is complete.
