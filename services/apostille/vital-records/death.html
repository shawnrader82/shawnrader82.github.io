What this page covers

Death Certificate Apostille, end to end.

A death certificate apostille is required when foreign courts, banks, registries, pension funds, or insurance companies need to confirm a U.S. death overseas. We verify you have a current certified copy from the correct county or state office, then file it with that state's apostille authority — most often the California Secretary of State. For non-Hague destinations, we walk you through the consular legalization chain.

Common reasons people need this

When a death certificate apostille is required.

Estates & inheritance

Foreign probate courts and registries require an apostilled U.S. death certificate before they will open or close an estate, distribute inheritance, or transfer titled assets held abroad. For inheritance matters involving Cuba, additional consular legalization steps apply.

Insurance & pension claims

Foreign pension funds, life-insurance carriers, and government benefits offices typically require an apostilled death certificate before paying out claims to beneficiaries living overseas.

Property transfers abroad

Foreign real-estate registries, vehicle authorities, and bank-account custodians often require an apostilled death certificate as part of transferring or releasing property held outside the United States.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected death certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Recent certified copies with a raised or color seal from the issuing county or state
  • California death certificates from CDPH or the county recorder where the death was registered
  • Out-of-state death certificates issued by that state's official vital-records office
  • Amended death certificates that correct cause of death, names, or other registry details
  • Cause-of-death or informational copies when permitted by the receiving authority

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies, scans, or notarized copies of a death certificate
  • Funeral-home or memorial certificates not issued by a vital-records office
  • Damaged, laminated, or altered certificates
  • Certificates issued so long ago that the destination authority will no longer accept them
  • Confidential or sealed copies that cannot be released for apostille purposes

Realistic timelines

Death Certificate apostille processing time.

California death certificates

  • Same-day or next business day — available with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California death certificates filed in person.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical out-of-state Secretary of State apostille processing.
  • Add 5–10 business days if a new certified copy must be ordered from the issuing county or state.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is outside the Hague Apostille Convention.

Death Certificate apostille checklist

  • Country where the death certificate will be used
  • Issuing state and county of the certificate

  • Whether the receiving authority requires cause-of-death detail
  • Hard deadline (probate filing, insurance claim, registry transfer)

Good to know

  • Recency: many foreign authorities want a death certificate issued within the last 6–12 months, even when an older copy already exists.
  • Cause of death: some destinations require the full cause-of-death version, others accept the informational copy — we confirm before ordering.
  • Translation: certified translation, if required, is ordered after the apostille is attached.

Death Certificate apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need a new certified death certificate for an apostille?

In most cases yes — you must use a recent certified copy issued by the correct county or state vital-records office, not a photocopy or funeral-home keepsake. We review a photo of your certificate and confirm whether it will work or if a new copy should be ordered.

Can you apostille a death certificate from another U.S. state?

Yes. Each U.S. state apostilles its own death records through its Secretary of State. We coordinate ordering and filing with the correct out-of-state offices. Timing and fees vary by state.

What if the death certificate is missing or damaged?

We can explain how to request a new certified copy from the correct vital-records office before moving forward with the apostille. In California that is typically CDPH or the county recorder where the death was registered.

How long does a death certificate apostille take?

California death certificates can often be apostilled in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states typically take 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization will add additional weeks.

Will the apostilled death certificate be returned to us?

Yes. The apostille is attached directly to the original certified death certificate. We arrange secure return by courier, mail, or in-person pickup and confirm the return method in writing before submission.

Do I need an apostille or consular legalization?

If the destination country is part of the Hague Apostille Convention, an apostille is the correct authentication. For non-member countries, consular legalization is required and we will outline the additional steps in advance.
