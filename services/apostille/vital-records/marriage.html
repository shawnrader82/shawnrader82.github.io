What this page covers

Marriage Certificate Apostille, end to end.

A marriage certificate apostille is what foreign governments and consulates require before they will recognize a U.S. marriage abroad. We confirm you have a current certified copy from the correct county or state office, then file it with that state's apostille authority — most commonly the California Secretary of State. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate the consular legalization chain.

Common reasons people need this

When a marriage certificate apostille is required.

Spousal & family visas

Foreign consulates and immigration offices require an apostilled marriage certificate when you sponsor a spouse for residency or a work visa, file for family reunification, or apply for a long-stay visa as a married couple.

Recognition of marriage abroad

Civil registrars in many countries — including Italy, Mexico, Germany, the Philippines, and across Latin America — will not recognize a U.S. marriage until the certificate is apostilled and, in many cases, certified-translated.

Foreign IDs, benefits & pensions

Spousal name changes on foreign passports or IDs, dependent benefits, foreign pension claims, and insurance filings abroad routinely require an apostilled U.S. marriage certificate as proof of the marriage.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected marriage certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Recent certified long-form copies with a raised or color seal from the issuing county or state
  • California marriage certificates from CDPH or the county clerk-recorder where the marriage was registered
  • Out-of-state marriage certificates issued by that state's official vital-records or county office
  • Certified copies in both spouses' current legal names that match supporting IDs
  • Amended marriage certificates that correct names, spelling, or other registry details

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies, scans, or notarized copies of a marriage certificate
  • Officiant souvenir certificates, ceremonial certificates, or church-only records (in most states)
  • Confidential marriage certificates that cannot be released for apostille purposes (varies by case)
  • Damaged, laminated, or altered certificates
  • Certificates older than the destination country's accepted issuance window (often 6–12 months)

Realistic timelines

Marriage Certificate apostille processing time.

California marriage certificates

  • Same-day or next business day — possible with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush.
  • 1–3 business days — typical turnaround for California marriage certificates filed in person.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical out-of-state Secretary of State apostille processing.
  • Add 5–10 business days when a new certified copy must be ordered from the issuing county or state.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is outside the Hague Apostille Convention.

  • Long-form vs short-form: most spousal-visa and foreign-registry cases require a long-form certificate.

  • Translation: if your destination requires a certified translation, we recommend ordering the translation only after the apostille is attached.

Marriage Certificate apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need a long-form or short-form marriage certificate?

Many consulates and foreign registrars prefer or require a long-form certificate that shows full details (both spouses, parents, witnesses, officiant). Short-form certificates are sometimes accepted but more often rejected. We review your copy and confirm before submission.

Can you apostille a marriage certificate from another U.S. state?

Yes. Each state apostilles its own marriage records. We coordinate ordering and filing through the correct out-of-state vital-records and Secretary of State offices. Timing and fees vary by state.

What if our marriage certificate is missing or damaged?

We can explain how to request a new certified copy from the correct vital-records or county office before moving forward with the apostille. In California that is typically CDPH or the county clerk-recorder where the marriage was registered.

How long does a marriage certificate apostille take?

California marriage certificates can often be apostilled in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available for many cases. Other states typically take 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization will add several weeks.

Will the apostilled marriage certificate be returned to us?

Yes. The apostille is attached directly to the original certified marriage certificate. We arrange secure return by courier, mail, or in-person pickup and confirm the return method before submission.

Do I need an apostille or consular legalization?

If the destination country is part of the Hague Apostille Convention, an apostille is the right authentication. For non-member countries, consular legalization is required and we will outline the additional steps in advance.
