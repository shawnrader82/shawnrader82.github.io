Using school records abroad

Why people need academic documents apostilled.

Academic records are commonly requested for foreign school admissions, immigration files, overseas employment, and licensing applications. The correct document format depends on what the foreign institution is asking for and how the school issued the record.

1

Study abroad & admissions

Foreign schools may request apostilled transcripts, diplomas, or enrollment confirmations before final admission.

2

Immigration & visas

Academic records are often used to support education history for residency, visa, or citizenship-related applications.

3

Professional licensing

Boards and regulators may require apostilled school records to verify education for licensing abroad.

4

Overseas work

Employers and credential evaluators may ask for apostilled transcripts, degree proof, or program completion letters.

Process

How school and academic apostille service works.

We focus on getting the document format right first, then routing it through the proper state apostille process so you avoid delays and duplicate fees.

Step 1 — Review the document and destination

  • Send a photo or scan of the transcript, diploma, or school letter.
  • Include the school name, issuing state, destination country, and any written requirements you received.

Step 2 — Confirm the correct format

  • We explain whether you need a registrar-issued record, notarized copy, school certification, or replacement document.
  • We help you avoid submitting student copies, incomplete records, or documents in the wrong format.

Step 3 — Submit for apostille

  • Once the document is in the correct form, we help move it through the appropriate apostille channel.
  • For non-Hague countries, we can explain when additional authentication or consular legalization may be needed.

Step 4 — Return or delivery

  • The apostilled document can be returned to you, released for pickup, or prepared for onward delivery where appropriate.
  • We outline timing and fee expectations before you commit so you can plan around application deadlines.

School & academic FAQ

Questions about apostilling school documents.

Do transcripts need to be sealed or notarized?

It depends on the destination and the school’s issuing process. Some cases accept registrar-issued records, while others require notarization or a specific certification step before apostille.

Can you help with school records from another state?

Yes. Many clients studied in another state and now live in Los Angeles. Those documents usually must be apostilled through the state connected to the school or notarization.

Can I use electronic or scanned transcripts?

Electronic records are useful for review, but apostille processing often requires paper documents in a specific certified or notarized format. We confirm what is acceptable before you order replacements.

Do I need apostille or consular legalization?

Hague Convention countries usually accept an apostille. Non-Hague destinations often require an authentication and consular legalization chain instead.
