When you need this

Common reasons companies apostille business records.

Most business apostille orders come from banking, registration, transaction, or regulatory needs abroad. The right document package depends on what the foreign institution is asking for and whether the record is state-issued or internally generated.

1

Foreign bank accounts

International banks often request apostilled formation documents, good standing certificates, and authority records before opening accounts.

2

Company registration abroad

Foreign registries may require apostilled formation and good standing records to register a subsidiary, branch, or representative office.

3

Cross-border transactions

Mergers, acquisitions, investments, and major contracts may require apostilled authority and existence documents during due diligence.

4

Licensing & compliance

Foreign agencies may require apostilled corporate records before granting approvals, licenses, or regulatory clearance.

Business apostille FAQ

Questions about business and corporate document apostilles.

What business documents can be apostilled?

Common examples include articles of incorporation or organization, certificates of good standing, corporate resolutions, bylaws, operating agreements, and related company records.

Do I need certified copies or notarization?

State-issued records often require certified copies, while internal company documents usually need notarization. We confirm the correct format before you proceed.

Can you help with companies formed outside California?

Yes. Business records generally must be apostilled through the state connected to the filing or notarization, even if you are now located in Los Angeles.

Do foreign banks and registries usually want more than one document?

Very often, yes. It is common for foreign institutions to request a formation document, good standing certificate, and an authorizing resolution together.
