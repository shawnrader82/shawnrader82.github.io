Using company records abroad

Why companies need business documents apostilled.

Corporate records are commonly requested by foreign banks, government registries, regulators, customs authorities, and overseas counsel. The correct document format depends on whether the destination needs a certified state copy or a notarized internal company document.

1

Foreign bank onboarding

Banks abroad routinely require apostilled articles, good standing, resolutions, and incumbency before opening corporate accounts.

2

International registration

Foreign registries and Ministries of Commerce often require apostilled formation documents to register a subsidiary or branch abroad.

3

Cross-border transactions

M&A, contracts, and corporate authorizations may need apostilled resolutions and powers of attorney so signatures are recognized overseas.

4

Customs & export

Customs authorities and importers may require apostilled commercial invoices and certificates of origin to clear goods or honor international tenders.

Process

How business and corporate apostille service works.

We focus on confirming each document is in the correct certified or notarized form first, then routing it through the proper state apostille authority so you avoid delays, rejections, and missed closing dates abroad.

Step 1 — Review the document and destination

  • Send a scan of the articles, good standing, resolution, bylaws, invoice, or POA.
  • Include the entity name, issuing state, destination country, and any written requirements from the bank, registry, or counsel.

Step 2 — Confirm the correct format

  • We explain whether you need a certified Secretary of State copy, an updated good standing certificate, or notarized internal company wording.
  • We help you avoid submitting plain printouts, expired records, or unsigned drafts that will be rejected.

Step 3 — Submit for apostille

  • Once the document is in the correct form, we help move it through the appropriate apostille channel.
  • For non-Hague countries, we can explain when additional authentication or consular legalization may be needed.

Step 4 — Return or delivery

  • The apostilled document can be returned to you, released for pickup, or prepared for onward delivery where appropriate.
  • We outline timing and fee expectations before you commit so you can plan around closing dates, bank deadlines, and foreign filing windows.

Business & corporate FAQ

Questions about apostilling business and corporate documents.

Which business and corporate documents can you help apostille?

Common documents include articles of incorporation and organization, certificates of good standing, corporate resolutions and incumbency certificates, bylaws and operating agreements, commercial invoices and certificates of origin, and corporate powers of attorney used for foreign banks, registries, and international transactions.

Do business records need to be certified state copies or just notarized?

It depends on the document. Articles of incorporation, certificates of good standing, and other state-filed records typically must be certified copies from the Secretary of State. Internal documents like bylaws, resolutions, or operating agreements are usually notarized first, then apostilled.

Can you apostille company documents from another U.S. state?

Yes. Out-of-state corporate documents must be processed through the apostille authority in the state where the document was filed or notarized. We help coordinate routing and confirm the right authority before you order copies or notarize internal documents.

Are apostilles accepted by foreign banks and registries?

For Hague Apostille countries, yes — a single apostille is typically the final authentication required. For non-Hague countries, additional consular legalization may be required and we explain the right path before you begin so you don’t miss a closing date or filing deadline abroad.

How long does business document apostille take?

California state apostille typically takes a few business days when documents are in the correct certified or notarized form. Out-of-state filings and non-Hague consular legalization take longer. We give you a realistic timeline up front based on the documents and destination country.
