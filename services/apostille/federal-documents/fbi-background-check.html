What this page covers

FBI background check apostille, end to end.

An FBI Identity History Summary — commonly called an FBI background check — is a fingerprint-based report from the FBI's Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) division. Foreign governments routinely require an apostilled FBI report for visa, residency, work permit, naturalization, marriage, adoption, and dual-citizenship applications. Because the report is a federal record, it is apostilled by the U.S. Department of State, not a state Secretary of State.

Common reasons people need this

When an FBI background check apostille is required.

Visas & residency applications

Most long-stay visa programs, residency permits, and digital-nomad visas require a recent apostilled FBI background check from every adult applicant — often within 6 months of application.

Work permits & professional licensing

Foreign employers, professional boards, and licensing authorities frequently require an apostilled FBI report before issuing work authorizations or recognizing U.S.-based credentials.

Citizenship by descent & naturalization

Countries that grant citizenship by descent (Italy, Ireland, Portugal, Spain, and others) and most foreign naturalization processes require an apostilled FBI background check for every adult applicant.

Marriage & adoption abroad

Foreign civil registrars and adoption agencies routinely request an apostilled FBI report to confirm there are no disqualifying criminal records before processing the application.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected FBI reports.

Will be accepted

  • FBI Identity History Summary issued directly by the FBI with original signature or embedded digital signature
  • FBI reports issued through an FBI-approved Channeler (Accurate Biometrics, Fieldprint, IdentoGO, and others)
  • Reports issued within the destination country's recency window (typically 3–6 months)
  • Digital PDFs with verifiable embedded FBI signature where the destination country accepts electronic apostille

Will be rejected

  • State DOJ background checks (those go through the state Secretary of State, not federal — see your state's process)
  • Local police clearance letters or county court records (handled separately, not by the FBI)
  • Self-printed photocopies of an FBI report without the original signature or digital seal
  • Expired FBI reports issued outside the destination country's recency window

Realistic timelines

FBI background check apostille processing time.

Hague Apostille countries

  • FBI report itself — a few business days via Channeler, or several weeks direct from the FBI.
  • Federal apostille — typically several weeks at the U.S. Department of State; rush options sometimes available.
  • Full timeline — plan for 2–6 weeks total from request to apostilled report in hand for most cases.

Non-Hague countries

  • Federal authentication at the U.S. Department of State (same step as apostille).
  • Consular legalization — add 2–6 weeks at the destination country's embassy or consulate in the U.S.
  • We coordinate the full chain so you don't have to chase multiple offices.

Good to know

  • Channeler vs direct: Channelers are FBI-approved third parties that deliver reports in days instead of weeks. We can recommend a trusted Channeler.
  • Recency matters: most foreign authorities require the FBI report to be no older than 3–6 months at the time of submission.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

FBI background check apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Can I use a state background check instead of the FBI one?

Usually not for international purposes. Foreign governments specifically request a federal FBI Identity History Summary for visa, residency, and citizenship applications. State DOJ checks are not federal and go through a different apostille process.

Should I use a Channeler or request directly from the FBI?

Channelers are usually much faster — a few business days versus several weeks for direct FBI requests. For most clients with deadlines, we recommend a Channeler. If you have time and want to save a small fee, direct from the FBI also works.

Can a digital PDF report be apostilled?

Yes, in many cases. The FBI issues digitally signed PDF reports with embedded signatures that the U.S. Department of State will apostille. Some non-Hague countries still prefer paper originals — we confirm based on your destination.

How recent does the FBI report need to be?

Most foreign governments want the report to be no older than 3–6 months at the time of submission. We confirm the recency requirement for your specific destination country.

What if my report shows entries I want to clarify?

An apostille only certifies that the document is genuinely from the FBI — it does not edit or hide its contents. If the report has entries you need to address, we can suggest expungement or rehabilitation steps to take before or alongside the apostille process.

Do I need consular legalization in addition to the apostille?

Only if your destination country is NOT a member of the Hague Apostille Convention. For Hague countries, the apostille alone is sufficient.
