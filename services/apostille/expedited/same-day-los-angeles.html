What this page covers

Same-day apostille, end to end.

A same-day apostille is possible when the document is eligible for in-person filing at the California Secretary of State, when it reaches us before the daily cutoff, and when the destination country accepts a Hague Convention apostille. We confirm all three conditions in writing before you commit, pick the documents up at your home, office, hotel, or hospital, hand-carry them to the Secretary of State counter, and return the apostilled originals to you by courier the same business day. If same-day is not realistic, we tell you immediately and quote next-business-day instead.

Common reasons people need this

When a same-day apostille is required.

Embassy or consulate appointment

Embassies and consulates often require apostilled supporting documents at the visa or legalization appointment. If your appointment is tomorrow, same- or next-business-day apostille is usually the only way to keep it.

International real estate or business closing

Foreign notaries, escrow agents, and registries frequently require apostilled powers of attorney or corporate documents on the day of signing. Same-day filing keeps multi-party international closings on schedule.

Travel or relocation deadline

Last-minute moves, work assignments, dual-citizenship submissions, and school enrollments abroad often surface paperwork requirements only days before departure. Rush apostille service prevents canceled flights and missed start dates.

Court, adoption, or family deadline

Foreign courts, adoption agencies, and family-law matters routinely set hard filing deadlines that cannot be extended. Same- or next-day apostille keeps the case on track without a continuance.

What qualifies for same-day rush

Eligible & ineligible documents for same-day apostille.

Usually eligible for same-day

  • California-issued birth, marriage, and death certificates (certified long-form, recent issuance)
  • California court documents with original clerk certification
  • Notarized powers of attorney, affidavits, and consent letters signed in front of a California notary
  • Articles of incorporation and certificates of good standing issued by the California Secretary of State
  • Notarized translations of California documents when the destination requires apostille on the translation

Cannot be same-day

  • Out-of-state vital records, court documents, or corporate filings (must be apostilled by that state)
  • Federal documents like FBI background checks, USCIS records, or NARA records (apostilled by the U.S. Department of State in Washington, DC)
  • Documents headed to a country that is not in the Hague Apostille Convention (requires consular legalization instead)
  • Documents with damaged, missing, or altered seals or signatures
  • Document copies, scans, or photocopies — apostille goes on the original certified document

Realistic timelines

Same-day apostille processing windows.

California documents

  • Same business day — in-person filing at the California Secretary of State Los Angeles office when documents reach us before the daily cutoff.
  • Next business day — afternoon pickups, late-confirmed eligibility, or destination countries that require additional review.
  • 1–3 business days — standard in-person filing without rush mobile pickup.

Out-of-state & federal documents

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for other state Secretary of State apostille offices, even with expedited mail.
  • 2–5 weeks — U.S. Department of State apostille for FBI, USCIS, NARA, and other federal documents.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

Good to know

  • Cutoff time: for same-day filing we generally need clean documents in hand by mid-morning. Afternoon pickups roll to next business day.
  • Federal documents: FBI, USCIS, and NARA documents are never same-day — they go through the U.S. Department of State in Washington, DC.
  • Consular legalization: non-Hague destinations (UAE, China, Vietnam, Qatar, and others) require an additional consulate step that cannot be completed same-day.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

Same-day apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Can every document get a same-day apostille?

Not every document qualifies for same-day processing. Eligibility depends on the issuing authority, the document type, and what time of day we receive the paperwork. California-issued vital records and notarized documents are usually the best same-day candidates. We confirm in writing whether your case qualifies before you book.

What is the cutoff time for same-day apostille service?

For same-day filing at the California Secretary of State Los Angeles office, we generally need clean, notarized documents in hand by mid-morning. After that, next-business-day filing is usually the realistic target. We give you a hard cutoff in writing when you contact us.

Do my documents need to be notarized first?

Many personal and business documents must be notarized before an apostille can be issued. State-issued records like birth, marriage, and death certificates skip the notary step. We review your documents and tell you exactly which path applies.

Can you rush apostilles for documents issued in other states?

Yes, but most other state Secretaries of State do not offer in-person same-day service the way California does. We coordinate priority-mail filing and expedited courier return, and we give you a realistic best-case window before you commit.

How do I know if I need an apostille or consular legalization?

Apostilles are used for countries that are part of the Hague Apostille Convention. Countries outside the Convention require consular legalization, which is a longer multi-step process and cannot be completed same-day. Tell us the destination country and we will confirm the correct path.

Will the original be returned with the apostille attached?

Yes. The apostille is attached directly to your original document and returned to you by secure courier, in-person handoff, or overnight mail. We confirm the return method in writing before filing.
