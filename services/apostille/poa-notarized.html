Federal document apostille FAQ

Questions about apostilling federal documents.

Do federal documents go through the California Secretary of State?

No. True federal documents are processed through the U.S. Department of State rather than the California Secretary of State.[web:272][web:118]

How do I know if I need an apostille or an authentication certificate?

The destination country decides that. Hague Apostille Convention countries receive an apostille, while non-member countries receive an authentication certificate and may need additional consular legalization.[web:272]

What form is used for federal requests?

The U.S. Department of State instructs requesters to use Form DS-4194 when submitting documents for apostille or authentication.[web:272]

Can you help if I am not sure whether my document is truly federal?

Yes. That is one of the most important parts of the review, because many delays happen when a document looks federal but does not qualify for the federal channel as issued.[web:118][web:272]
