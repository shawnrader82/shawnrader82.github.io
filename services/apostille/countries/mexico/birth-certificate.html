What this page covers

Birth Certificate Apostille for Mexico, end to end.

U.S.-born children of Mexican parents can register as Mexican citizens at the Mexican Consulate General in Los Angeles — but the Mexican Registro Civil and the Secretaría de Relaciones Exteriores (SRE) require an apostilled U.S. birth certificate as the foundational identity document. The same apostilled birth certificate is used for Mexican residency (Residente Temporal or Permanente through the Instituto Nacional de Migración / INM), for notarized property purchase documents (escritura pública), and for inheritance proceedings before a Mexican notario público. We handle the California Secretary of State apostille and advise on whether a Spanish translation by a Mexican perito traductor is required for your specific purpose.

Common reasons people need this

When a birth certificate apostille for Mexico is required.

Dual citizenship registration

U.S.-born children of Mexican parents can obtain Mexican nationality by descent. The Mexican Consulate or Registro Civil requires an apostilled U.S. birth certificate to establish the lineage and register the child as a Mexican citizen.

Mexican residency (Residente Temporal / Permanente)

INM (Instituto Nacional de Migración) requires an apostilled birth certificate as part of the residency application package for retirees, remote workers, and family reunification residency in Mexico.

Property purchase in Mexico

When a U.S. citizen purchases real property in Mexico, the Mexican notario público requires an apostilled birth certificate (or passport-equivalent identity document) to execute the escritura pública and complete the title transfer.

Inheritance & succession in Mexico

U.S.-based heirs of Mexican estates must often submit an apostilled U.S. birth certificate to prove their identity and relationship to the deceased to the Mexican notario público or family court handling the succession.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected birth certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Long-form certified birth certificate from California CDPH or county recorder
  • Current certified copy with raised seal or security features
  • Document in final form — no corrections, no lamination

Will be rejected

  • Hospital-issued birth announcements — not certified vital records
  • Laminated originals — apostille offices reject laminated documents
  • Photocopies — apostille attaches to the original certified document
  • Short-form abstract certificates (Mexico often requires long-form with parents’ names)

Birth Certificate Apostille FAQ — Mexico Use

Frequently asked questions.

Does Mexico accept a California apostille on my U.S. birth certificate?

Yes. Mexico joined the Hague Apostille Convention in 1995. A California Secretary of State apostille is the correct and sufficient U.S.-side authentication for a California-issued birth certificate used in Mexico. No additional authentication from the Mexican Consulate or U.S. Embassy is required.

Does my apostilled birth certificate need to be translated into Spanish for Mexico?

Yes, typically. Mexico requires that foreign-language documents be accompanied by a certified Spanish translation by a Mexican perito traductor (court-certified translator) before use at Registro Civil, INM, or Mexican notary offices. We handle the apostille in Los Angeles; you arrange the Spanish translation in Mexico after receiving the apostilled original.

My child was born in California to Mexican parents. Can I register them as a Mexican citizen?

Yes. U.S.-born children of Mexican nationals can register as Mexican citizens through the Mexican Consulate General in Los Angeles or directly at the Registro Civil in Mexico. The consulate typically requires an apostilled California birth certificate, both parents’ Mexican identity documents, and may require a Spanish translation. Contact the consulate for their current document checklist.

I want to buy property in Mexico. Do I need my birth certificate apostilled?

A Mexican notario público (public notary) typically requires identity documentation — most often your passport — for a property purchase. Some notarios also accept or require an apostilled birth certificate as a supplementary identity document, especially if you are purchasing in a restricted zone (coastal or border areas). Confirm with your Mexican notario what they specifically need.

My birth certificate is from Texas. Can you still help?

If your birth certificate was issued in Texas, the apostille must come from the Texas Secretary of State, not California. We can advise on the correct out-of-state apostille office and assist you in coordinating the process by mail.

How long is the apostille valid for Mexico?

The apostille itself does not expire. However, some Mexican government offices and notarios prefer recently issued certified copies — particularly INM, which may require a certified copy issued within six months. Check with the receiving Mexican authority on their recency requirements before ordering a new certified copy.
