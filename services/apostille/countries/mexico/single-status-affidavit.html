Common reasons people need this

When a single-status affidavit apostille for Mexico is required.

Mexican Registro Civil marriage license

The Mexican civil registry requires all foreign nationals to present proof of single status before issuing a marriage license. An apostilled single-status affidavit sworn before a California notary is the standard document used by U.S. citizens and residents marrying in Mexico.

Previously married — divorce decree required

If you have been previously married, the Registro Civil requires both the apostilled single-status affidavit AND the apostilled divorce decree (or apostilled death certificate of former spouse). The affidavit references the prior marriage and its legal dissolution.

INM immigration filings

INM residency applications based on marriage to a Mexican national may require a sworn affidavit of civil status as part of the personal history and family documentation package.

Mexican civil records updates

Some Mexican civil registry offices or consular processes require an affidavit of single status to update civil records, process CURP corrections, or support other administrative legal procedures.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected single-status affidavit copies.

Will be accepted

  • Sworn affidavit with jurat block (not an acknowledgment) — you must swear under oath
  • Signed in front of the California notary — not pre-signed
  • Specific factual statements (full legal name, date and place of birth, current status)
  • Signer present with current government-issued photo ID

Will be rejected

  • Pre-signed affidavits — signer must swear and sign before the notary
  • Affidavits with acknowledgment instead of jurat (it must be a sworn statement)
  • Vague or incomplete statements (must clearly state never married, or all prior marriages dissolved with dates and decree references)
  • Expired or invalid IDs

Good to know

  • A jurat means you swear the contents are true under penalty of perjury. Providing false information on a notarized affidavit is perjury, a criminal offense in California.
  • California notaries cannot draft the affidavit for you. Use a template from your Mexican attorney, the Registro Civil, or the Mexican Consulate in Los Angeles.
  • If you have been previously married, your apostilled divorce decree (or apostilled death certificate of a deceased former spouse) must accompany the affidavit at the Registro Civil.
  • Spanish translation by a Mexican perito traductor is typically required before the Registro Civil will accept the affidavit. We do not provide translation services.

Single-Status Affidavit Apostille FAQ — Mexico Use

Frequently asked questions.

What is a single-status affidavit and why does Mexico require it?

A single-status affidavit (also called an affidavit of single status or affidavit of eligibility to marry) is a sworn statement in which you declare that you are legally free to marry — either because you have never been married or because all prior marriages have been legally dissolved. The Mexican Registro Civil requires this from foreign nationals because Mexico cannot directly verify foreign civil records, so it accepts a sworn statement as proof of eligibility.

I was married before and then divorced. Do I need the affidavit AND my divorce decree?

Yes — typically both. The single-status affidavit references your prior marriage and its legal dissolution, and the apostilled divorce decree (or apostilled death certificate if you are widowed) serves as documentary proof of the dissolution. Bring both apostilled documents to the Registro Civil. Confirm with the specific Registro Civil office for their exact requirements.

Can I get this affidavit notarized at the Mexican Consulate in Los Angeles instead?

The Mexican Consulate General in Los Angeles offers consular notarization services for some documents, including sworn statements, which Mexican authorities may accept without the California apostille chain. However, consulate appointments can be limited. Confirm with the Registro Civil office in Mexico where your marriage will be registered whether they prefer a consular notarization or a California notarization + apostille before booking either option.

Does my affidavit need to be in Spanish?

The Mexican Registro Civil typically requires a certified Spanish translation of the affidavit by a Mexican perito traductor if the affidavit is in English. Some clients bring both an English and a Spanish version; we notarize the English version and the perito handles the certified translation. Confirm with your Registro Civil or Mexican attorney which format they prefer.

I have a CENOMAR from Mexico. Does that replace the affidavit?

A CENOMAR (Constancia de No Registro de Matrimonio) is a Mexican civil registry document confirming no marriage is registered in Mexican records. Some Registro Civil offices accept it for Mexican nationals instead of an affidavit, but for a U.S. citizen or resident marrying in Mexico, the Registro Civil typically requires the sworn affidavit since U.S. civil records are not centrally stored in Mexico. Confirm with your specific Registro Civil office.

Is there a template for the affidavit I can use?

The Mexican Consulate General in Los Angeles sometimes provides a template upon request. Your Mexican attorney or the Registro Civil office where you will marry may also provide one. Some online resources have standard single-status affidavit templates for Mexico — confirm the format with the receiving Registro Civil before using any template. Once you have the draft, send it to us for review before your notarization appointment.
