What this page covers

Marriage Certificate Apostille for UK Use, end to end.

A U.S. marriage certificate is one of the core documents for a UK spouse visa (Spouse Settlement Visa or Fiancé Visa), Indefinite Leave to Remain, or family reunification. UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) requires an apostilled original or certified copy to prove the legal marriage. The same apostilled certificate may also be used to update marital status in UK pension records, change a name with HMRC or the DVLA, or as part of a dual nationality application. We ensure you have the certified copy in the right format and route it through the California Secretary of State apostille.

Common reasons people need this

When a marriage certificate apostille for the UK is required.

UK spouse & Settlement Visa

UKVI requires an apostilled U.S. marriage certificate to demonstrate the genuine and subsisting marriage relationship required for a Spouse Settlement Visa, civil partner visa, or Indefinite Leave to Remain application based on marriage.

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)

Applicants extending or applying for ILR based on a spousal or long-term partner route must submit their apostilled marriage certificate alongside other relationship evidence at each application stage.

UK pension & benefit records

The UK State Pension, NHS pension, teachers’ pension, and government benefit authorities may require an apostilled marriage certificate to update a surviving spouse’s records, change beneficiary designations, or claim pension rights.

UK name change & government records

Updating a name on a UK passport, driving licence (DVLA), HMRC tax records, or bank accounts after a U.S. marriage often requires presenting an apostilled U.S. marriage certificate to UK institutions that are unfamiliar with U.S. documentation.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected marriage certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Certified marriage certificate issued by a California county recorder or CDPH
  • Full-form certificate showing both parties’ names, date, location, and officiants
  • Raised-seal or security-paper certified copy

Will be rejected

  • Commemorative or decorative marriage certificates — not legal certified records
  • Photocopies without a recorder’s stamp and signature
  • Laminated originals — apostille offices reject laminated documents
  • Short-form abstracts (UKVI typically requires the full certificate)

Good to know

  • UKVI prefers the full-form marriage certificate (not an abstract) because it shows both parties’ full names, date, location, and officiants — confirming it is a genuine civil record.
  • If your name changed since marriage and your passport reflects the new name, UKVI may ask for the marriage certificate as a chain-of-identity document alongside other evidence.
  • If you were married in a different U.S. state, the apostille comes from that state’s Secretary of State. We advise on the correct out-of-state office and can coordinate mail-in submissions.
  • The UK does not require a certified translation because U.S. marriage certificates are issued in English.

Marriage Certificate Apostille FAQ — UK Use

Frequently asked questions.

Does UKVI require the original marriage certificate or is a certified copy acceptable?

UKVI typically requires a certified copy issued by the relevant county recorder or state vital records office — not the original decorative certificate issued by the officiant or church. The certified copy is the legal record, and the California apostille attaches to it.

My marriage certificate is from another U.S. state. Can you apostille it?

If the marriage was registered in another U.S. state, the apostille must come from that state’s apostille authority — not the California Secretary of State. We can advise on the correct state office and assist with coordinating the mail-in process.

Do I need to translate my marriage certificate into English for the UK?

No — U.S. marriage certificates are already in English, so no translation is required for UK use. If your marriage certificate is in another language (e.g., a foreign marriage registered in the U.S. by a consulate), a certified translation may be required alongside the apostille.

I’m applying for a UK spouse visa. Do I need anything else besides the apostilled marriage certificate?

The apostilled marriage certificate is a key document, but UKVI also requires financial evidence, cohabitation proof, photos, and other relationship evidence. Consult a UK immigration solicitor for the full document checklist for your specific visa category and circumstances.

Can I use the same apostilled certificate for multiple UK applications?

Generally yes — the apostille does not expire. However, UKVI may note that an older apostilled certificate should be verified, and some UK pension or benefit authorities request a recently issued certified copy. Check with each receiving authority on their recency requirements.
