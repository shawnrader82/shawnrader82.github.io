Common reasons people need this

When a degree or diploma apostille for the UK is required.

NHS & healthcare registration

The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC), General Medical Council (GMC), and other UK health regulators require an apostilled U.S. degree as part of international registration — allowing U.S.-trained nurses, doctors, therapists, and allied health professionals to practice in NHS trusts and private clinics.

UK Qualified Teacher Status

The UK Teaching Regulation Agency (TRA) requires overseas teachers to submit an apostilled degree or teaching credential when applying for Qualified Teacher Status (QTS) — a prerequisite to teaching in state schools in England.

Skilled Worker visa sponsorship

UK employers sponsoring a U.S.-based candidate on a Skilled Worker visa frequently request an apostilled degree as part of the Right to Work and eligibility verification package — especially for healthcare, engineering, IT, and finance roles.

UK professional body recognition

UK engineering councils, legal bodies, financial regulators, and academic institutions may require an apostilled U.S. degree before recognizing foreign qualifications — for membership, licensure, or academic credit.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected degree or diploma copies.

Will be accepted

  • Original diploma or degree issued by a U.S. university or college
  • Certified copy issued by the university registrar (with registrar’s stamp and signature)
  • Notarized certified true copy (copy certified by a California notary) where the UK authority accepts this
  • Document in English — as issued by U.S. institutions

Will be rejected

  • Unofficial transcripts or student copies without an official seal
  • Photocopies not notarized or certified by the issuing institution
  • Laminated diplomas — must be un-laminated for apostille attachment
  • Foreign-issued degrees (those must be apostilled in their country of origin)

Degree / Diploma Apostille FAQ — UK Use

Frequently asked questions.

Does the UK accept an apostille on a notarized copy of my degree instead of the original?

It depends on the receiving UK authority. Some — like the Teaching Regulation Agency (TRA) — may accept a notarized and apostilled certified copy; others — like NMC for nursing registration — have specific requirements. Always confirm with the relevant UK body before deciding whether to apostille the original or a certified copy.

My university is in California but I live in another state — can you still help?

Yes. If your degree was issued by a California university, the California Secretary of State is the correct apostille authority regardless of where you live now. You can mail your diploma or university-issued certified copy to us and we handle the California apostille remotely.

Do I need my transcript apostilled as well as my diploma?

Some UK employers and professional bodies require both. We can apostille both the diploma and the official transcript in the same submission if you have certified copies of each from your university registrar. Submit them together to save time.

What if my degree is from a UK university — does it need an apostille for UK use?

A UK-issued degree used within the UK does not need an apostille (apostilles are for international use). If you need to use a UK degree in the U.S. or another country, it would need to be apostilled by the UK FCDO Legalisation Office.

Will the apostille confirm my degree is genuine?

No — the apostille certifies only that the notary’s signature (on a certified copy) or the issuing official’s signature (on an original) is authentic. It does not verify the content or accreditation of the degree. UK employers and bodies often use the UK ENIC (formerly NARIC) service for degree equivalency verification, separate from the apostille.
