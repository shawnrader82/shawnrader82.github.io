The MOFA attestation chain

How UAE document attestation works.

Because the UAE is not in the Hague Convention, every U.S. document must pass through all four authentication steps before UAE government offices and employers will accept it. We manage Steps 1 and 2.

Step 1 — California Secretary of State

  • For California-issued documents (birth certificates, marriage certificates, court records), the California Secretary of State authenticates the issuing official’s signature or seal.
  • For university degrees from California institutions, we notarize a certified copy if needed before California SOS authentication.
  • Typical processing: 3–7 business days.

Step 2 — U.S. Department of State

  • The U.S. Dept of State in Washington D.C. authenticates the California Secretary of State’s seal or, for documents from other states, the relevant state’s authentication.
  • Standard: 10–20 business days. Rush: 3–5 business days.
  • After Step 2, the document is ready for the UAE Embassy step.

Step 3 — UAE Embassy Washington D.C. (client-side)

  • The UAE Embassy certifies the U.S. Dept of State’s seal. This is typically handled by your UAE employer’s PRO or a UAE attestation agent with a presence in D.C.
  • Typical processing: 1–5 business days after receiving from U.S. Dept of State.

Step 4 — UAE MOFA (client-side)

  • UAE MOFA applies the final attestation stamp in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, or another UAE emirate.
  • After MOFA attestation, the document is fully authenticated for use with GDRFA, MOHRE, DHA, schools, and UAE banks.
  • Typing centers and PROs in the UAE manage the MOFA submission.

UAE attestation FAQ

Questions about document attestation for the UAE.

Is the UAE in the Hague Apostille Convention?

No. The UAE has not joined the Hague Apostille Convention. A simple apostille from the California Secretary of State is NOT accepted by UAE MOFA, GDRFA, or UAE government authorities. U.S. documents for the UAE require a full four-step attestation chain: (1) California Secretary of State, (2) U.S. Department of State, (3) UAE Embassy in Washington D.C., and (4) UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) in the UAE.

What is MOFA attestation?

MOFA stands for the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs. MOFA attestation is the final UAE government stamp that certifies the entire chain of authentication on a foreign document — making it legally valid for use with UAE government entities, employers, courts, and banks. Without the MOFA stamp, a U.S. document is not recognized in the UAE, regardless of how many U.S. authentications it carries.

Which steps of the UAE attestation chain do you handle?

We handle the U.S.-side steps: (1) California Secretary of State authentication of the document and (2) U.S. Department of State authentication of the California Secretary of State seal. The UAE-side steps — (3) UAE Embassy in Washington D.C. and (4) UAE MOFA — are typically handled by your UAE employer’s PRO, a typing center in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, or a UAE-based attestation agent.

How long does the full UAE attestation chain take?

The U.S. side (California Secretary of State + U.S. Department of State) typically takes 2–4 weeks for standard service, or 1–2 weeks with rush. The UAE Embassy and MOFA steps add another 1–3 weeks depending on the emirate and current processing volumes. Budget 5–7 weeks total for the complete chain if not using rush services.

Do my UAE documents need a certified Arabic translation?

UAE MOFA often accepts English-language U.S. documents at the attestation stage, but individual UAE government entities — GDRFA (residence visa), MOHRE (employment), DHA/HAAD (healthcare licensing), and schools — frequently require a certified Arabic translation alongside the attested original. Confirm with your UAE sponsor, employer, or PRO before submitting.
