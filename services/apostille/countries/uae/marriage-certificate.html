What this page covers

Marriage Certificate Attestation for UAE Use, end to end.

Expatriates relocating to Dubai or Abu Dhabi with a spouse must submit an attested marriage certificate to prove the legal relationship for a family residence visa. The UAE General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) does not accept a simple U.S. apostille. The full attestation chain is required: California Secretary of State → U.S. Department of State → UAE Embassy Washington D.C. → UAE MOFA. We complete the first two steps and return the partially authenticated document ready for the UAE-side agents.

Family sponsorship for UAE residency

Whether sponsoring a spouse under an employment, investor, or golden visa, the marriage certificate must clear the full MOFA attestation chain before UAE immigration authorities will process the family residency application.

Employment relocation packages

UAE employers relocating an employee and their family often require the attested marriage certificate as part of the HR onboarding package to facilitate family residency and dependant visa processing through the free zone or mainland authority.

UAE banking & legal matters

UAE banks, notary offices, and courts may require an attested marriage certificate to update joint account records, add a spouse as a beneficiary, or support inheritance and succession matters under UAE law.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected marriage certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Certified marriage certificate issued by a California county recorder or CDPH
  • Full-form certificate showing both parties’ names, date, location, and officiants
  • Raised-seal or security-paper certified copy

Will be rejected

  • Commemorative or decorative marriage certificates — not legal certified records
  • Photocopies without a recorder’s stamp and signature
  • Laminated originals — authentication offices reject laminated documents

Marriage Certificate Attestation FAQ — UAE Use

Frequently asked questions.

Why can’t I just apostille the marriage certificate for UAE use?

Because the UAE has not signed the Hague Apostille Convention. A simple apostille from the California Secretary of State is not recognized by UAE MOFA, GDRFA, or UAE courts. The full four-step authentication chain is mandatory for personal documents used in the UAE.

What does the full UAE attestation chain look like for a marriage certificate?

Step 1: California Secretary of State authenticates the county recorder’s signature. Step 2: U.S. Department of State authenticates the California Secretary of State’s seal. Step 3: UAE Embassy in Washington D.C. certifies the U.S. Dept of State seal. Step 4: UAE MOFA in the UAE applies the final attestation stamp. Each step must follow the previous one.

My marriage certificate is from another U.S. state. Can you still handle the chain?

Yes, but the state-level step must go through that state’s Secretary of State or equivalent apostille authority. We can advise on the correct out-of-state office and coordinate the U.S. Dept of State step, which is the same regardless of which state authenticated it.

Does the marriage certificate need a certified Arabic translation for UAE MOFA?

UAE MOFA may accept English-language U.S. documents, but individual UAE government entities — such as GDRFA or your employer’s HR — often require a certified Arabic translation alongside the attested original. Confirm with your UAE sponsor or PRO before proceeding.

How long does the full UAE attestation process take for a marriage certificate?

The U.S. side (California SOS + U.S. Dept of State) typically takes 2–4 weeks standard. The UAE Embassy and MOFA steps add another 1–3 weeks. Budget at least 5–7 weeks total if you are not using rush services.

Can I submit both spouses’ documents together in one order?

Yes — if both spouses need personal documents authenticated (e.g., each has a separate birth certificate that also needs to be attested), we can include them in the same submission to the California Secretary of State and U.S. Dept of State to save time and shipping costs.
