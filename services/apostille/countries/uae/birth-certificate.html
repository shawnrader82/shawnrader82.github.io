What this page covers

Birth Certificate Attestation for UAE Use, end to end.

A U.S. birth certificate needed for a UAE residence visa, family sponsorship, or school enrollment in Dubai or Abu Dhabi must be authenticated — not just apostilled — through a multi-step chain because the UAE has not joined the Hague Apostille Convention. The process starts in California: the California Secretary of State authenticates the document, then the U.S. Department of State in Washington D.C. authenticates the California Secretary of State’s signature, then the UAE Embassy in D.C. certifies the U.S. Dept of State seal, and finally UAE MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs) applies the final stamp in the UAE. We handle the first two U.S.-side steps so your document arrives in Dubai or Abu Dhabi ready for the final UAE-side steps.

Common reasons people need this

When a birth certificate attestation for the UAE is required.

UAE residence visa applications

Expatriates applying for a UAE residence visa — whether through an employer, a spouse, or an investor route — must submit authenticated birth certificates as part of the family file or personal identity verification package submitted to GDRFA Dubai or Abu Dhabi TAMM.

Family sponsorship in Dubai & Abu Dhabi

A UAE-resident sponsor bringing a spouse, child, or parent to the UAE on a family residence visa must submit authenticated birth certificates to prove the relationship. Unauthenticated or merely apostilled U.S. documents are rejected.

School enrollment in the UAE

International schools in Dubai and Abu Dhabi frequently require MOFA-attested birth certificates for enrollment of expatriate children — especially for British curriculum, American curriculum, and IB schools requiring official identity documentation.

UAE employment & HR files

Some UAE employers and free-zone authorities (DIFC, ADGM, Jebel Ali Free Zone) require MOFA-attested personal documents as part of employee onboarding — particularly for senior roles, regulated industries, or government-linked employers.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected birth certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Long-form certified birth certificate from California CDPH or county recorder
  • Current certified copy with raised seal or security features
  • Document in final form — no corrections, no lamination

Will be rejected

  • Hospital-issued birth announcements — not certified vital records
  • Laminated originals — authentication offices reject laminated documents
  • Photocopies — the authentication chain requires the original certified document
  • Short-form abstract birth certificates (UAE typically requires long-form)

Birth Certificate Attestation FAQ — UAE Use

Frequently asked questions.

Is an apostille enough for the UAE?

No. The UAE has not joined the Hague Apostille Convention, so a standard apostille from the California Secretary of State is not accepted by UAE MOFA or government authorities. You need the full attestation chain: California Secretary of State → U.S. Department of State → UAE Embassy Washington D.C. → UAE MOFA.

What is MOFA attestation?

MOFA stands for the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs. MOFA attestation is the final UAE government stamp that certifies the entire chain of authentication on your U.S. document — making it legally valid for use with UAE government entities, employers, banks, and courts. Without the MOFA stamp, your document is not recognized in the UAE.

Which steps do you handle and which ones does my UAE sponsor handle?

We handle the U.S.-side steps: (1) California Secretary of State authentication and (2) U.S. Department of State authentication. The UAE-side steps — (3) UAE Embassy in Washington D.C. and (4) UAE MOFA — are typically handled by your UAE employer’s PRO, a typing center in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, or a UAE-based attestation agent.

My birth certificate is from another U.S. state. Can you still help?

Yes, but the state-level authentication must come from that state’s Secretary of State, not California’s. We can advise on the correct out-of-state authentication office and coordinate the U.S. Dept of State step regardless of which state issued your birth certificate.

How long does the full UAE attestation chain take?

The U.S. side (California + U.S. Dept of State) typically takes 2–4 weeks standard. The UAE Embassy and MOFA steps add another 1–3 weeks depending on the emirate and current processing times. Budget at least 4–7 weeks for the complete chain if you are not using rush services.

Does the birth certificate need to be translated into Arabic for UAE MOFA?

UAE MOFA often accepts U.S. documents in English without a mandatory Arabic translation for the initial attestation, but individual UAE government entities (GDRFA, employers, schools) may require a certified Arabic translation afterward. Confirm with your UAE sponsor or PRO before submitting.
