What this page covers

Degree / Diploma Attestation for UAE Use, end to end.

Employers in Dubai and Abu Dhabi — including free zones like DIFC, ADGM, Jebel Ali, and DHCC — require attested degree certificates for employment visa applications and Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE) work permit processing. Healthcare professionals must have degrees attested for Dubai Health Authority (DHA) or Abu Dhabi Health Authority (HAAD/DoH) licensing. Without the full MOFA attestation chain, a U.S. degree is not recognized for these purposes. We handle the U.S.-side steps: California Secretary of State authentication and U.S. Department of State authentication.

Common reasons people need this

When a degree or diploma attestation for the UAE is required.

UAE work visa & MOHRE registration

MOHRE and the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs require attested educational credentials as part of the work permit application package — confirming the employee’s qualification for the role and professional category.

DHA & HAAD / DoH healthcare licensing

Doctors, nurses, pharmacists, dentists, and allied health professionals working in Dubai require DHA licensure; in Abu Dhabi, HAAD (now DoH) licensure. Both authorities require full MOFA attestation of the U.S. degree and transcript as part of the credential verification process.

Free zone employer onboarding

DIFC, ADGM, DMCC, Jebel Ali, and other UAE free zones often require attested degree certificates for senior roles, regulated professional positions, or as part of their standard HR verification process.

UAE professional body registration

UAE engineering, legal, accounting, and other professional bodies require attested foreign degrees before granting membership, practicing certificates, or professional registration to expatriate practitioners.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected degree or diploma copies.

Will be accepted

  • Original diploma or degree from a U.S. university or college
  • Certified copy issued by the university registrar (with registrar’s stamp and signature)
  • Notarized certified true copy where UAE authority accepts this format
  • Document in English as issued by the U.S. institution

Will be rejected

  • Unofficial transcripts or student copies without an official seal
  • Photocopies not certified by the issuing institution
  • Laminated diplomas — must be un-laminated for authentication attachment

  • Healthcare professionals: DHA and HAAD/DoH often require additional steps beyond MOFA attestation, including dataflow background screening. Start early — the full licensing process can take 3–6 months.

Degree / Diploma Attestation FAQ — UAE Use

Frequently asked questions.

Does the UAE accept a simple apostille on my degree?

No. The UAE is not a member of the Hague Apostille Convention. A California Secretary of State apostille alone is not accepted by UAE MOFA, MOHRE, DHA, or other UAE authorities. You need the full four-step attestation chain.

Do I need my transcript attested as well as my diploma?

Many UAE employers and licensing authorities (especially DHA and HAAD) require both the degree and the official transcripts to be attested. We can process them together in the same submission to save time.

I’m a nurse wanting to work in Dubai. What attestation steps are required?

For DHA nursing licensure, your degree must go through the full four-step chain: California SOS → U.S. Dept of State → UAE Embassy D.C. → UAE MOFA. After MOFA attestation, DHA typically requires a Dataflow verification, passing the DHA exam (for some categories), and English language proficiency. The MOFA attestation is one early step in a longer process.

My university degree is from a different U.S. state. Can you still help?

Yes, but the state-level authentication must come from that state’s Secretary of State. We can coordinate the mail-in process with the correct out-of-state office and then handle the U.S. Dept of State step from there.

Does my degree need to be translated into Arabic for UAE use?

UAE MOFA often processes English-language documents without requiring Arabic translation at the attestation stage, but individual employers, licensing bodies, or MOHRE may request a certified Arabic translation afterward. Confirm with your UAE employer or PRO.

Can I use a notarized copy of my degree instead of sending the original?

Some UAE authorities accept a notarized and attested certified copy; others require the original. We can notarize a certified copy in California first, then submit it through the attestation chain. Confirm with your UAE employer or PRO before choosing this path.
