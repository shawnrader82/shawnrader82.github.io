Common reasons people need this

When a power of attorney attestation for the UAE is required.

Dubai & Abu Dhabi property transactions

Authorize a UAE-based attorney-in-fact to sign a sale-purchase agreement (SPA), transfer property title at Dubai Land Department (DLD) or Abu Dhabi Municipality, or deal with a real estate developer on your behalf without traveling to the UAE.

UAE bank account management

Emirates NBD, FAB, ADCB, ENBD, and other UAE banks may accept an authenticated power of attorney to allow a representative to operate your account, close accounts, or transfer funds when you are outside the UAE.

UAE legal & court proceedings

UAE courts and notary offices require a notarized and attested (MOFA-stamped) power of attorney for legal representation. A California-notarized and apostilled POA may be accepted by some private parties but not by UAE government courts.

UAE company & free zone matters

Authorize a UAE-based manager or representative to handle free zone or mainland LLC matters — renewing trade licenses, signing company documents, dealing with MOHRE, or representing the company at government offices.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected power of attorney copies.

Will be accepted

  • POA drafted to UAE legal standards (by a UAE lawyer or notary is preferred)
  • Specific powers clearly listed (sell property, operate account, sign for company, etc.)
  • Grantor (donor) present with current government-issued photo ID
  • California acknowledgment block (most POAs use acknowledgment)

Will be rejected

  • Pre-signed POAs — grantor must sign in front of the California notary
  • Vague “all matters” POAs without specified powers (often rejected by Dubai Land Dept and UAE courts)
  • Photocopies — the authentication chain requires the original notarized document

Power of Attorney Attestation FAQ — UAE Use

Frequently asked questions.

Does a California apostille work for UAE property transactions?

It depends on the UAE recipient. Dubai Land Department (DLD) and UAE courts typically require the full MOFA attestation chain. Some private UAE parties (brokers, private banks) may accept a California apostille. Always confirm with your Dubai solicitor, developer, or bank before choosing the authentication path.

Can I use an Arabic-language POA notarized in California?

California notaries operate in English and cannot administer an oath or acknowledgment in a language they don’t understand if the document is entirely in Arabic. If your UAE lawyer prepares a bilingual (English/Arabic) POA, we notarize the English-language block. A purely Arabic-language POA would need to be notarized by a UAE notary in the UAE.

My UAE property developer says they need a “special power of attorney.” What does that mean?

In UAE real estate, a “special” power of attorney refers to a limited or specific POA that grants authority only for one named transaction — such as signing the sale-purchase agreement for a specific property. This is distinct from a general POA. Your UAE lawyer or developer’s legal team can provide the correct template.

How do I authorize someone to deal with my UAE bank account from the U.S.?

Each UAE bank has its own requirements for third-party access. Some require a MOFA-attested POA; others have their own bank-form POA with specific language. Contact your UAE bank’s operations team to get their specific POA template and requirements before we draft or notarize anything.

Can you deliver the authenticated POA directly to my UAE attorney-in-fact?

Yes. Once the U.S.-side steps are complete, we can ship the authenticated document via international tracked courier directly to your UAE attorney-in-fact, PRO, or attestation agent who will handle the UAE Embassy and MOFA steps.

Is a UAE MOFA-attested power of attorney valid indefinitely?

No — powers of attorney in the UAE have validity limits, typically one to three years from the notarization date, depending on the type and the authority involved. Dubai Land Department and UAE courts may reject POAs older than one year. Confirm the validity period with your UAE lawyer or DLD when drafting.
