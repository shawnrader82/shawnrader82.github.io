Process

How a vital record apostille order works with us.

The steps are similar whether you are dealing with a California birth certificate or an out‑of‑state death certificate. We focus on getting the format right first, so the apostille office can say “yes” the first time.

Step 1 — Share the record and destination country

  • Text or email a photo of the certificate and any consulate, court, or attorney instructions.
  • We confirm whether the document is a certified copy, who issued it, and which state or federal office is responsible.

Step 2 — Confirm the right certified copy

  • We flag when the record is too old, missing security features, or issued by the wrong office.
  • We guide you through ordering new long‑form or amended certificates when a replacement is safer.

Step 3 — Submit for apostille

  • We prepare the apostille application, fees, and return instructions for the appropriate Secretary of State or other office.
  • For non‑Hague countries, we can coordinate follow‑on state authentication and consular steps where needed.

Step 4 — Return delivery

  • Apostilles are attached to the certified record and returned by secure mail, courier, or pickup.
  • You receive clear instructions for presenting the apostilled record abroad so it is recognized properly.

Before you send anything

Quick checklist for vital records.

A few details on the front end prevent most delays and rejections. We walk through this list with you when you call or text.

  • Confirm whether the record was issued in California or another U.S. state.
  • Check that the certificate is a certified copy with seals, signatures, or security features (not a plain photocopy).
  • Note the destination country and whether it is part of the Hague Apostille Convention.
  • Gather any related documents your attorney, consulate, or court requested.
  • Know your deadline so we can recommend standard versus rush routes.
  • Decide whether we should return the apostilled record to you, a lawyer, or a foreign office.

Good to know

  • Vital records usually cannot be notarized themselves. The apostille relies on the issuing authority’s signatures and seals.
  • Each state apostilles its own records. A California Secretary of State apostille cannot be used for a birth certificate issued in another state.
  • Some countries ask for both a vital record and a notarized statement or translation. We can coordinate those supporting pieces with the apostille order.
  • Mail‑in service is available when you are outside Los Angeles. We confirm the plan before you ship originals.

Vital records FAQ

Questions about vital record apostilles.

Do I have to order new vital records, or can I use what I already have?

Sometimes older certified copies are acceptable, but many destinations prefer recent, long‑form records with updated security features. We review a photo of what you have and tell you if a new copy is recommended before apostille.

Can you help if my record was issued in another state?

Yes. We regularly help clients in Los Angeles with vital records issued in other states. Each state apostilles its own records, so we help you order certified copies from the right office and route them correctly.

What if I am already outside the United States?

Many clients are overseas when they realize they need a U.S. birth, marriage, death, or divorce record apostilled. We coordinate with you and, when possible, with your representative here to obtain certified copies and handle apostille steps on your behalf.

How do I know if I need an apostille or consular legalization?

Hague countries usually accept a state or federal apostille. Non‑Hague destinations often require a state authentication and consular legalization chain. We confirm the correct path for your country before you send any originals.
