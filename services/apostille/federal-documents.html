Federal apostille FAQ

Questions about federal document apostilles.

What federal documents can be apostilled?

Common examples include FBI background checks, federal court records, USCIS records, Consular Reports of Birth Abroad, Consular Reports of Death Abroad, and some National Archives records.

Do federal documents go through the California Secretary of State?

No. True federal documents are processed through the U.S. Department of State rather than a state Secretary of State.

How do I know whether I need an apostille or authentication certificate?

The destination country determines that. Hague countries receive an apostille, while non-member countries receive an authentication certificate and may need consular legalization after that.

Do federal requests use Form DS-4194?

Yes. Form DS-4194 is used to request apostille or authentication service from the U.S. Department of State.
