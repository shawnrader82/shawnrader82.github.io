Process

How a federal apostille order works with us.

Federal documents are authenticated by the U.S. Department of State — not a Secretary of State. We focus on getting the federal record, signatures, and routing right before any envelope leaves your hands.

Step 1 — Share the record and destination country

  • Text or email a photo of the federal document and any consulate, attorney, or employer instructions.
  • We confirm whether the document is a certified federal record, who issued it, and whether the destination country is Hague or non-Hague.

Step 2 — Confirm the right certified federal copy

  • We flag when the record is too old, lacks the required federal signature, or needs to be re-issued by USCIS, the FBI, NARA, or a federal court.
  • We guide you through ordering the correct certified copy before you commit to apostille fees and shipping.

Step 3 — Submit to U.S. Department of State

  • We prepare the federal apostille application, fees, and return instructions for the U.S. Department of State Office of Authentications.
  • For non‑Hague countries, we coordinate follow-on consular legalization steps with the appropriate embassy.

Step 4 — Return delivery

  • Federal apostilles are attached to the certified record and returned by secure courier worldwide.
  • You receive clear instructions for presenting the apostilled record abroad so it is recognized properly.

Before you send anything

Quick checklist for federal documents.

A few details on the front end prevent most delays and rejections at the U.S. Department of State. We walk through this list with you when you call or text.

  • Confirm whether the document is a genuine federal record (issued by USCIS, FBI, NARA, federal court, or U.S. Department of State).
  • Check that the certificate carries the original federal signature and seal — photocopies, scans, and notarized copies are not accepted.
  • Note the destination country and whether it is part of the Hague Apostille Convention.
  • Gather any related federal documents your attorney, consulate, or employer requested.
  • Know your deadline so we can recommend standard versus rush routes.
  • Decide whether we should return the apostilled record to you, an attorney, a foreign office, or a consulate.

Good to know

  • Federal documents cannot be apostilled through any state Secretary of State. They must go through the U.S. Department of State Office of Authentications.
  • State-issued records (driver's licenses, state DOJ background checks, state court records) are not federal documents — those go through a Secretary of State instead.
  • Some countries ask for both a federal record and a notarized statement or translation. We can coordinate those supporting pieces with the apostille order.
  • Mail‑in service is available worldwide. We confirm the plan and return path before you ship originals.

Federal documents FAQ

Questions about federal document apostilles.

Which federal documents can you help apostille?

We routinely help apostille FBI Identity History Summary reports, USCIS naturalization and citizenship certificates, federal court records, Consular Reports of Birth and Death Abroad (CRBA / CRODA), and National Archives records used internationally.

Do federal documents go through the U.S. Department of State?

Yes. Genuine federal documents are authenticated by the U.S. Department of State Office of Authentications, not by any state Secretary of State. We confirm the correct routing before you mail originals.

How long does federal document apostille take?

U.S. Department of State processing times vary by document type and current backlog. We give you realistic timelines, channel options, and rush routing — when available — before you send anything.

Can you help if I am outside California or the United States?

Yes. Most federal apostille work is handled by mail. We coordinate the order with you from anywhere in the U.S. or abroad and return your apostilled federal documents by secure courier.
