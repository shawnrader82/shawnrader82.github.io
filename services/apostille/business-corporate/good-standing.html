What this page covers

Certificate of Good Standing Apostille, end to end.

A certificate of good standing apostille is what foreign banks, regulators, and corporate registries use to confirm that a U.S. company exists, is current on its filings, and is authorized to do business as of the issue date. Because it’s a state-issued document, the certificate must come directly from the Secretary of State (sometimes called a certificate of existence, certificate of status, or certificate of authorization). It is then apostilled by the same Secretary of State. Most foreign parties require a certificate issued within the last 3 to 6 months.

Common reasons people need this

When a certificate of good standing apostille is required.

Foreign bank onboarding

Banks abroad require an apostilled certificate of good standing as part of corporate KYC to confirm a U.S. company is active and authorized to enter into the banking relationship.

Regulatory & tax compliance

Foreign tax authorities and regulators may require an apostilled good standing certificate to confirm a U.S. parent’s active status before granting tax IDs, licenses, or permits.

Foreign registry filings

Foreign Ministries of Commerce and corporate registries often require an apostilled good standing certificate when registering a subsidiary, branch, or representative office.

International tenders & contracts

Government and corporate tenders abroad routinely require bidders to submit an apostilled good standing certificate as part of the qualification package.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected certificate of good standing copies.

Will be accepted

  • Secretary of State certificates of good standing, status, existence, or authorization — issued within the recency window your foreign party requires
  • Long-form (detailed) good standing certificates that show officers or registered agent when required
  • Good standing certificates from any U.S. state — apostilled by the same issuing state
  • Combined orders: articles of incorporation plus a current good standing certificate apostilled together
  • Foreign-qualified entity certificates of authority when accompanied by the home-state good standing

Will be rejected

  • Expired or outdated certificates beyond the recency window required by the foreign party
  • Downloaded PDFs or screenshots from the Secretary of State business search portal without certification
  • Certificates from a state different from the one being asked to issue the apostille
  • Internal company statements of good standing — only the Secretary of State can issue this certificate
  • Certificates for entities that are suspended, forfeited, or no longer in good standing

Realistic timelines

Certificate of Good Standing apostille processing time.

California certificate of good standings

  • Same-day or next business day — available for California good standing certificates ordered and apostilled in person.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California good standing ordered and apostilled together.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for out-of-state Secretary of State apostille offices.
  • Add 1–2 weeks if a fresh certificate must be ordered from the state filing office first.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

Good to know

  • Recency matters: most foreign banks and registries reject a good standing certificate older than 3–6 months — we recommend ordering fresh.
  • Bundle with articles: if the foreign party wants both, we apostille articles plus current good standing together as a single package.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the certificate. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

Certificate of Good Standing Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Is a certificate of existence the same as a certificate of good standing?

Functionally yes — different states use different names (certificate of good standing, certificate of existence, certificate of status, certificate of authorization). Whatever your foreign party is asking for, your home state issues the equivalent document, and we apostille it.

How recent does the certificate need to be?

Most foreign banks and registries want a certificate issued within the last 30 to 90 days. Some accept up to 6 months. We confirm the recency window with you before ordering so the apostilled certificate is still valid when the foreign party reviews it.

Can you order good standing from any state?

Yes. We can order from California directly or guide you through ordering from any other U.S. state filing office. Once the certified original is in hand, we coordinate apostille through the same state.

Do I need short-form or long-form good standing?

Short-form (status only) is enough for most banks. Long-form (which lists officers, registered agent, or business activity) is sometimes required by foreign registries. We ask the destination party which format they need before ordering.

What if my company is suspended or not in good standing?

The Secretary of State will not issue a good standing certificate to a suspended or forfeited entity. We can help you understand what filings, taxes, or fees are needed to restore good standing first — or coordinate alternative document strategies if appropriate.

Can the certificate be issued and apostilled in one trip?

In California, yes — we can typically order a same-day good standing and have it apostilled in the same trip for rush cases. Other states usually require a separate ordering step first, then apostille.
