What this page covers

Commercial Invoice & Certificate of Origin Apostille, end to end.

A commercial invoice or certificate of origin apostille is what foreign customs authorities, importers, and tender boards use to confirm that an export document genuinely originates from a U.S. company. Commercial invoices are typically notarized as a true copy by a company officer; certificates of origin are usually issued or certified by a local Chamber of Commerce and then notarized for apostille. Once notarized, the document is apostilled by the Secretary of State where the notary is commissioned. Shipping and customs deadlines often drive these jobs — we routinely run rush turnarounds.

Common reasons people need this

When a commercial invoice or certificate of origin apostille is required.

Customs clearance abroad

Foreign customs authorities often require an apostilled commercial invoice or certificate of origin to release shipments, calculate duties, and confirm the goods’ origin under trade agreements.

International tenders & bids

Government and corporate tenders abroad routinely require bidders to submit apostilled invoices and certificates of origin proving production location and pricing terms.

Export shipping & freight

Freight forwarders, shipping lines, and importers in destination countries may require apostilled trade documents before releasing or accepting a shipment.

Letters of credit & trade finance

Banks issuing or confirming letters of credit may require apostilled invoices or certificates of origin to release payment under documentary credit terms.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected commercial invoice or certificate of origin copies.

Will be accepted

  • Commercial invoices signed by a company officer with notary acknowledgment or jurat
  • Certificates of origin issued or stamped by a local Chamber of Commerce, then notarized for apostille
  • Packing lists, bills of lading, and inspection certificates signed and notarized as part of an export package
  • Sworn statements of origin or declarations signed by a company officer in front of a notary
  • Combined export packages: invoice + certificate of origin + sworn statement apostilled together

Will be rejected

  • Unsigned invoices or certificates — the document must bear an original signature in front of the notary
  • Invoices notarized in a state different from where they will be apostilled
  • Photocopies of previously notarized invoices — the original notarized document is required
  • Chamber of commerce certificates with expired or missing chamber stamps
  • Invoices with incomplete or improper notarial certificates (missing venue, date, or seal)

Commercial Invoice & Certificate of Origin Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do all export documents need to be apostilled?

Not always — many destinations accept commercial invoices and certificates of origin without apostille. Apostille is typically required when the foreign customs office, importer, or tender authority specifically requests authentication, or when the country requires authentication of all trade documents.

Where do I get a certificate of origin?

Certificates of origin are typically issued by a local Chamber of Commerce in the U.S. The company files an application with the chamber, which stamps the certificate. We then notarize the company’s acknowledgment of the certificate and apostille it.

Can you apostille a shipment package with multiple documents?

Yes. We routinely apostille combined export packages — commercial invoice, certificate of origin, packing list, bill of lading, and sworn statements — all notarized and apostilled together so they travel as a complete bundle to customs or the importer.

Can the invoice be notarized at our warehouse or freight forwarder?

Yes — we run mobile notary appointments at offices, warehouses, freight forwarder locations, and ports across the greater Los Angeles area. We can also accept mail-in jobs from out-of-state companies for California Secretary of State apostille.

What about non-Hague destinations like Saudi Arabia or the UAE?

For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate the full authentication and consular legalization chain so the trade documents are recognized by the foreign customs authority. Note: the UAE joined the Apostille Convention in 2025, so apostille is now sufficient for the Emirates.

How fast can you turn an export package around?

California documents can typically be notarized and apostilled within 1–3 business days — and same-day rush is available for most cases. We routinely handle vessel-deadline jobs same-day or next-day in the Los Angeles area.
