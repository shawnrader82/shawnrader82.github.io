What this page covers

Enrollment & Graduation Letter Apostille, end to end.

Enrollment, attendance, and graduation letters need an apostille before most foreign schools, scholarship programs, employers, and consulates will treat them as valid abroad. The letter must be issued by the school registrar — on official letterhead, with a signature and (when available) the school seal — and sometimes notarized before the Secretary of State can apostille it. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate consular legalization.

Common reasons people need this

When a enrollment or graduation letter apostille is required.

Proof of current enrollment abroad

Foreign consulates, immigration offices, and student-visa programs frequently require an apostilled enrollment verification letter to confirm a student's active status before issuing a long-stay visa, renewing residency, or approving a study-abroad program.

Graduation confirmation

When a diploma has not yet been issued — common right after commencement — an apostilled graduation confirmation letter from the registrar serves as proof of degree completion for foreign employers, licensing boards, and graduate-program admissions deadlines.

Scholarships & exchange programs

Foreign government scholarships (Fulbright, Erasmus, DAAD, Chevening), exchange programs, and assistantship placements (like Spain's Auxiliares de Conversación) frequently require an apostilled enrollment or graduation letter as part of the application package.

Dependent & family visas

When a student is included on a parent's or spouse's foreign visa (or vice versa), the foreign consulate often requires an apostilled enrollment letter confirming the student's continuous study and active academic status during the visa period.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected enrollment or graduation letter copies.

Will be accepted

  • Letters on official school letterhead with the registrar's signature and school seal
  • Enrollment verifications, attendance letters, and good-standing letters
  • Graduation confirmation letters (degree-awarded statements) before diploma issuance
  • Letters issued by accredited public, private, charter, online, and graduate-level schools
  • Notarized true copies of original signed-and-sealed letters (state-dependent)

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies or scans without notarization or registrar certification
  • Letters from teachers, professors, or counselors instead of the registrar
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing notarized letter
  • Letters without an issue date or older than the destination's recency window
  • Foreign-issued letters (those must be apostilled by their issuing country)

Good to know

  • Recency matters: most consulates and scholarship programs want a letter dated within the last 30–90 days.
  • Letterhead is essential: the apostille verifies the signature — a letter must be on official school letterhead with a registrar's signature for the notary or apostille to be valid.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

Enrollment & Graduation Letter Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Does the letter need to come from the registrar?

Yes, in almost all cases. Letters from individual teachers, professors, or counselors are not accepted for apostille — the letter must come from the school registrar on official letterhead with a signature and (when available) the school seal.

Does the letter need to be notarized before the apostille?

It depends on the destination and the state where the letter is being apostilled. Some destinations accept the registrar's signature directly via apostille; others require the registrar's signature to be notarized first. We confirm the correct path before processing.

Can you apostille a letter from a school in another state?

Yes. The letter is apostilled in the state where the school is located, so an out-of-state school letter must go through that state's apostille authority. We coordinate notarization and filing with the correct out-of-state Secretary of State.

How recent does the letter need to be?

Most consulates, scholarship programs, and employers want a letter issued within the last 30–90 days. If your letter is older than the destination's recency window, we help you request a fresh one from the registrar.

How long does a school letter apostille take?

California school letter apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization add several more weeks.

Will the original letter be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached to the original signed-and-sealed letter (or to the notarized copy) and returned to you by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup. We confirm return method in writing before submission.
