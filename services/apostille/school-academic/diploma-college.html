What this page covers

College Diploma Apostille, end to end.

A college diploma apostille is what most foreign graduate programs, licensing boards, employers, and immigration offices require before they will accept a U.S. degree. Most college diplomas are notarized as true copies in front of a notary, then apostilled by the Secretary of State where the notary is commissioned. Some universities also offer registrar-certified copies that can be apostilled directly. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate consular legalization.

Common reasons people need this

When a college diploma apostille is required.

Graduate school admissions abroad

Foreign master's, doctoral, and professional programs routinely require an apostilled bachelor's or master's diploma to confirm completion of the prerequisite degree. The apostilled diploma is usually requested alongside an apostilled transcript and course descriptions.

Professional licensing abroad

Foreign licensing boards — for medicine, nursing, engineering, accounting, architecture, and law — require an apostilled degree to verify education credentials before granting license reciprocity, sitting for board exams, or registering as a foreign-qualified professional.

Overseas employment

Foreign employers, especially for skilled and visa-sponsored roles, frequently require an apostilled diploma to confirm education before issuing a contract or sponsoring a work permit. This is standard in tech, finance, healthcare, and academia.

Immigration & skilled-worker visas

Skilled-worker visas, residency programs (like Spain's NLV or Portugal's D7/D8), and immigration files routinely require an apostilled college diploma to confirm education credentials for points-based scoring or visa eligibility.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected college diploma copies.

Will be accepted

  • Original college and university diplomas notarized as a true copy in front of a notary public
  • Registrar-certified copies with the university seal and registrar's signature
  • Diplomas from accredited public and private universities, community colleges, and graduate schools
  • Associate's, bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional degree diplomas
  • Replacement diplomas issued by the university when the original is lost or damaged

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies or scans without notarization or registrar certification
  • Diplomas notarized in a state different from where they will be apostilled
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing notarized copy
  • Diplomas where the university's name, signatures, or seal are no longer legible
  • Foreign-issued diplomas (those must be apostilled by their issuing country)

Realistic timelines

College Diploma apostille processing time.

California college diplomas

  • Same-day or next business day — available with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush processing.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California-notarized diplomas submitted in person.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for out-of-state Secretary of State apostille offices.
  • Add 1–4 weeks if a registrar-certified copy must be ordered from the university first.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

Good to know

  • Notarize where you'll apostille: the diploma must be notarized in the same state that will issue the apostille — we coordinate this for California or out-of-state cases.
  • Transcript pairing: most foreign graduate programs and licensing boards want both the apostilled diploma and the apostilled transcript together.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

College Diploma apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Can a college diploma be apostilled?

Yes. The most common path is to notarize the original diploma as a true copy in front of a notary, then apostille the notary's signature through the Secretary of State. Some universities also issue registrar-certified copies that can be apostilled directly. We confirm which path your destination accepts.

Do I need the original diploma?

Usually yes — the original diploma is brought to a notary who attests that the copy made from it is a true copy. If you no longer have the original, your university registrar can usually issue a replacement diploma or a certified copy with the university seal.

What if my university is in another state?

The diploma must be notarized in the state that will apostille it. We either coordinate notarization with a notary in the university's state, or use a California notary if the diploma will be apostilled through the California Secretary of State. Either way, the notarization and apostille state must match.

Does it matter if my diploma is bachelor's, master's, or PhD?

No — the apostille process is the same for associate's, bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional degrees. What matters is that the diploma is the original or a registrar-certified copy, and that it is properly notarized in the same state that will issue the apostille.

How long does a college diploma apostille take?

California diploma apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization add several more weeks.

Will the original diploma be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached to the notarized copy or to a registrar-certified copy — your original diploma stays with you. The apostilled document is returned by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup.
