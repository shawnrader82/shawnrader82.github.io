Diploma apostille help

Get your degree documents accepted abroad

A college diploma apostille is often requested when your degree will be used in another country for graduate school, licensing, immigration, employment, or credential evaluation.

The right starting document is not always the framed diploma itself. Depending on the school, state, and destination country, the safer route may be a notarized copy, a replacement diploma, or a registrar certification prepared for apostille.

We review your document first so you do not waste time sending the wrong version to a notary, Secretary of State, or foreign institution.

Process

How college diploma apostille usually works

1. We review the diploma and destination country.

Send a photo or scan of the diploma and any checklist from the school, employer, licensing board, or government office requesting it.

2. We confirm the correct document format.

Some cases require a notarized copy. Others require a registrar letter, school certification, or a replacement diploma before apostille.

3. We coordinate apostille submission and return delivery.

Once the document is in the right format, we help move it through the proper apostille channel and return it by pickup, courier, or mail.

FAQ

Questions about college diploma apostille

Can you apostille my original diploma?

Sometimes yes, but not always. In many situations, a notarized copy or registrar-certified version is the better route.

What if I only have one original diploma?

That is common. We can help you decide whether to protect the original and use a certified or notarized alternative instead.

Can you help if the school is in another state?

Yes. We help determine which state has authority over the apostille process and what format that state will accept.

Do I also need transcripts?

Sometimes. If the foreign institution wants grades, credits, or course detail, you may also need a college transcript apostille.
