What this page covers

TEFL / TESOL Certificate Apostille, end to end.

A TEFL or TESOL certificate apostille is what foreign schools, ministries of education, and work-visa offices in countries like China, South Korea, Spain, Vietnam, and the UAE require before issuing a teaching work permit or Z-visa. Because TEFL providers are private companies (not government agencies), the certificate is notarized as a true copy in front of a notary, then apostilled by the Secretary of State where the notary is commissioned. For non-Hague destinations like the UAE and Saudi Arabia, we coordinate consular legalization.

Common reasons people need this

When a TEFL or TESOL certificate apostille is required.

Teaching English abroad

Most foreign schools require an apostilled TEFL or TESOL certificate as part of the hiring file — alongside an apostilled degree and FBI background check. The apostille proves the certificate is authentic and lets the school submit it to immigration for the work permit.

Work visa & permit applications

Foreign immigration offices issuing teacher work permits (China's Z-visa, South Korea's E-2, Spain's residency, Saudi Arabia's iqama, etc.) usually require the TEFL or TESOL apostille as part of the visa documentation. Without it, the visa is delayed or refused.

China & South Korea programs

China (Hague member since November 2023), South Korea (EPIK, GEPIK, hagwons), and Japan (JET, eikaiwa) all require apostilled TEFL or TESOL certificates for native-English-teacher placements. We handle ordering, notarization, and apostille for all three.

Credential evaluation & recruitment

International recruiters and credential evaluators working on behalf of foreign schools often require apostilled TEFL or TESOL certificates as part of the candidate vetting process before placement, even before the work permit stage.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected TEFL or TESOL certificate copies.

Will be accepted

  • Original printed TEFL, TESOL, CELTA, and English-teaching certificates
  • 120-hour, 150-hour, 180-hour, and other accredited TEFL/TESOL course certificates
  • Certificates from in-person and reputable online TEFL/TESOL providers
  • Replacement printed certificates issued by the provider when the original is lost
  • Hard-copy printouts of digital certificates that the provider has signed or stamped

Will be rejected

  • Email confirmations, screenshots, or PDF-only certificates without a printed copy
  • Certificates notarized in a state different from where they will be apostilled
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing notarized copy
  • Certificates where the provider name or signature is no longer legible
  • Foreign-issued certificates (those must be apostilled by their issuing country — e.g., Canadian TEFLs go through Global Affairs Canada)

California TEFL or TESOL certificates

  • Same-day or next business day — available with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush processing.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California-notarized certificates submitted in person.

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for out-of-state Secretary of State apostille offices.
  • Add 5–10 business days if a printed certificate must be requested from the TEFL/TESOL provider first.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Vietnam pre-accession).

TEFL / TESOL Certificate Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do I really need to apostille my TEFL or TESOL certificate?

Yes, in most cases. China, South Korea, Spain, Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and many other countries explicitly require an apostilled or legalized TEFL/TESOL certificate as part of the teacher work-permit package. Some schools may not enforce it, but the visa office almost always will.

Can a TEFL certificate be apostilled?

Yes. The most common path is to notarize the printed original certificate as a true copy in front of a notary, then apostille the notary's signature through the Secretary of State. The TEFL/TESOL provider does not need to be involved in the apostille step.

What if I only have a digital PDF certificate?

You need a printed copy. Most TEFL/TESOL providers will mail you a printed certificate on request — sometimes for a small re-issuance fee. We can tell you exactly what to ask for so the printed copy is suitable for notarization and apostille.

Which countries require a TEFL or TESOL apostille?

Common Hague-member countries that require it include China, South Korea, Spain, Japan, Germany, and Italy. Non-Hague countries that require consular legalization include the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and (pre-2026) Thailand. Always confirm your destination's current rule, as China and Canada both joined the Hague Convention recently.

How long does a TEFL/TESOL apostille take?

California TEFL apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization add 2–6 weeks.

Can you handle the FBI background check and diploma apostille at the same time?

Yes. Teaching abroad almost always requires three apostilled documents — TEFL/TESOL certificate, college diploma, and FBI background check. The FBI check is a federal document apostilled through the U.S. State Department, while the TEFL and diploma are state-level. We coordinate all three so they're ready together.
