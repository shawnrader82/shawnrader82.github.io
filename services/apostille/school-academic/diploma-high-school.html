What this page covers

High School Diploma Apostille, end to end.

A high school diploma apostille is what most foreign schools, immigration officers, and employers require before they will accept a U.S. diploma. Because high schools do not issue diplomas with raised state seals, the diploma is almost always notarized as a true copy first, then apostilled by the Secretary of State where the notary is commissioned. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate the consular legalization chain.

Common reasons people need this

When a high school diploma apostille is required.

Study abroad & foreign admissions

Foreign secondary schools, IB programs, and undergraduate admissions offices often request an apostilled U.S. high school diploma as part of the enrollment file — especially in Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East.

Immigration & visa filings

Long-stay visas, family reunification cases, and residency applications routinely require an apostilled high school diploma to confirm education history when other academic records are not available.

Education verification abroad

Foreign credential evaluators and licensing bodies use an apostilled high school diploma to verify a minimum education requirement for vocational programs, trade licensing, or employment screening abroad.

Overseas work & entry-level employment

Some overseas employers, especially in hospitality, retail, and entry-level technical roles, require an apostilled high school diploma as part of the work permit or background check package before issuing a contract.

Three steps from photo to apostille.

2

We file with the correct Secretary of State (California or other), monitor processing, and return the apostilled diploma by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup. Same-day rush is available for most California cases.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected high school diploma copies.

Will be accepted

  • Original high school diplomas notarized as a true copy in front of a notary public
  • School-issued certified copies of a diploma bearing the registrar's signature and seal
  • Diplomas from public, private, charter, magnet, and online accredited high schools
  • GED certificates and HiSET certificates with proper state-agency certification
  • Replacement diplomas issued by the school district when the original is lost

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies or scans without notarization or registrar certification
  • Diplomas notarized in a state different from where they will be apostilled
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing notarized copy
  • Diplomas where the school's name, signatures, or seal are no longer legible
  • Foreign-issued diplomas (those must be apostilled by their issuing country)

Realistic timelines

High School Diploma apostille processing time.

California high school diplomas

  • Same-day or next business day — available with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush processing.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California-notarized diplomas submitted in person.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for out-of-state Secretary of State apostille offices.
  • Add 1–4 weeks if a school-issued certified copy must be ordered from the school district first.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

High School Diploma apostille checklist

  • Country where the diploma will be used
  • Name and address of the issuing high school or district
  • Whether you have the original diploma in hand
  • Whether the destination wants a notarized or school-issued copy
  • Hard deadline (visa appointment, school start date, employer deadline)
  • Whether the destination country is in the Hague Apostille Convention

Good to know

  • Notarize where you'll apostille: the diploma must be notarized in the same state that will issue the apostille — we coordinate this for California or out-of-state cases.
  • Transcript pairing: many foreign schools ask for both the apostilled diploma and the apostilled high school transcript together.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

High School Diploma Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Does the high school diploma need to be the original?

Usually yes — the original diploma is notarized as a true copy in front of a notary, then apostilled. If you no longer have the original, your school district can issue a replacement diploma or a school-certified copy. We can guide you to the right office.

Can you apostille a diploma from a high school in another state?

Yes. The diploma must be notarized in the state that will apostille it, so we either coordinate notarization with a notary in that state or use a California notary if the diploma is being apostilled through the California Secretary of State.

Do you handle GED or HiSET certificates?

Yes. GED and HiSET certificates are apostilled the same way as a traditional high school diploma. The issuing state agency (often the Department of Education) provides certified copies which we then apostille.

What if my diploma was issued decades ago?

Older diplomas are fine as long as they are still legible — the school's name, signatures, and any seal must be visible to the notary. If the diploma is damaged or unreadable, your school district can usually issue a replacement.

How long does a high school diploma apostille take?

California diploma apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization add several more weeks.

Will the original diploma be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached to the notarized copy or to a school-issued certified copy — your original diploma stays with you. The apostilled document is returned by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup.
