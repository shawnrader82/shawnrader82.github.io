What this page covers

College Transcript Apostille, end to end.

A college transcript apostille is what most foreign graduate programs, credential evaluators, licensing boards, and immigration offices require before they will accept a U.S. college transcript. The transcript must be an official copy issued by the university registrar in a sealed envelope or with a verifiable digital signature, then certified or notarized before the Secretary of State can apostille it. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate consular legalization.

Common reasons people need this

When a college transcript apostille is required.

Foreign graduate school admissions

Foreign master's, doctoral, MBA, and professional programs almost always require an apostilled official transcript as part of the application file — for course history, GPA verification, and prerequisite confirmation. The transcript is usually requested alongside the apostilled diploma.

Foreign licensing boards

Foreign licensing boards — for medicine, nursing, engineering, accounting, architecture, and law — require an apostilled transcript to verify coursework and credit hours before granting license reciprocity or letting candidates sit for foreign board exams.

Visa & immigration support

Skilled-worker visas, residency programs, and education-based immigration files routinely require an apostilled transcript to confirm continuous education history and credit-hour requirements for visa eligibility scoring.

Credential evaluation

U.S.-based credential evaluators like WES, ECE, and IERF — working on behalf of foreign institutions — frequently require an apostilled transcript when the underlying record must be authenticated for use outside the country.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected college transcript copies.

Will be accepted

  • Sealed official transcripts issued by the university registrar
  • Transcripts bearing the registrar's signature, university seal, and issue date
  • Notarized true copies of an opened sealed transcript (state-dependent)
  • Transcripts from accredited public, private, community-college, and graduate institutions
  • Recently re-issued transcripts when the destination requires recent issuance

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies or scans of transcripts without registrar certification
  • Transcripts where the original sealed envelope was opened before notarization (some states)
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing certified copy
  • Transcripts where the registrar's name, signatures, or seal are no longer legible
  • Foreign-issued transcripts (those must be apostilled by their issuing country)

Realistic timelines

College Transcript apostille processing time.

California college transcripts

  • Same-day or next business day — available with in-person filing at the California Secretary of State when your case qualifies for rush processing.
  • 1–3 business days — standard turnaround for California transcripts submitted in person.
  • 2–4 weeks — mail-in submission to the California Secretary of State.

Other U.S. states

  • 1–6 weeks — typical processing window for out-of-state Secretary of State apostille offices.
  • Add 1–3 weeks if a new sealed transcript must be ordered from the university registrar first.
  • Add 2–6 weeks for consular legalization if the destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

Good to know

  • Do not open the envelope: many destinations require the sealed envelope to remain intact until presented — we apostille on the registrar's certification, not the inner pages.
  • Diploma pairing: most foreign graduate programs and licensing boards want both the apostilled transcript and the apostilled diploma together.
  • Translation: the apostille does not translate the document. Certified translation, if required, happens after apostille.

College Transcript Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Does the college transcript need to be sealed?

Often, yes — many foreign graduate programs and credential evaluators require the original sealed envelope to remain intact until presented. We apostille based on the registrar's certification on the envelope flap or cover sheet, so the inner transcript stays sealed. We confirm the destination's exact requirement before processing.

Can you apostille a transcript from a university in another state?

Yes. The transcript is apostilled in the state where the university issued it — so a Michigan university transcript goes through the Michigan apostille authority. We coordinate ordering and filing directly with the issuing state.

Can I use a digital or e-transcript?

Some destinations accept digitally-signed transcripts (sent through services like Parchment, Credentials Inc., or directly from the registrar), but most still require a paper transcript with a registrar's seal for apostille purposes. We confirm what is acceptable before you order replacements.

How long does a college transcript apostille take?

California transcript apostilles can often be completed in 1–3 business days with in-person filing, and same-day rush is available in most cases. Other states vary, typically 1–6 weeks. Non-Hague destinations requiring consular legalization add several more weeks.

Can I get multiple apostilled copies of my transcript?

Yes. We recommend ordering multiple sealed transcripts upfront — one per foreign institution that will receive a copy — because each must be apostilled separately. Re-issuance after the fact adds days to the timeline.

Will the original sealed transcript be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached to the registrar's certification and returned to you with the sealed transcript intact by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup. We confirm return method in writing before submission.
