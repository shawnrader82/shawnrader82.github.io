What this page covers

Course Descriptions & Syllabi Apostille, end to end.

A course descriptions and syllabi apostille is what most foreign credential evaluators, licensing boards, and graduate programs require when the underlying coursework needs to be authenticated — beyond what a transcript can show. The course descriptions are usually provided by the university registrar as a certified packet (with seal and signature), or as notarized true copies of catalog pages and instructor-issued syllabi. The packet is then apostilled by the Secretary of State where the registrar or notary is located. For non-Hague destinations, we coordinate consular legalization.

Common reasons people need this

When a course descriptions or syllabi apostille is required.

Foreign credential evaluation

Foreign credential evaluators frequently require apostilled course descriptions and syllabi when transcripts alone do not provide enough course-content detail — for example, when a foreign licensing body needs to verify specific coursework hours in pharmacology, structural engineering, or financial accounting.

License reciprocity abroad

Foreign licensing boards — for medicine, nursing, engineering, pharmacy, architecture, accounting, and law — often require apostilled course descriptions to verify that specific coursework meets the foreign jurisdiction's licensing standard before granting reciprocity, equivalency, or board-exam eligibility.

Graduate admissions abroad

Some foreign master's, doctoral, and professional programs require apostilled course descriptions or syllabi to verify prerequisite coursework, especially in highly technical fields where the foreign program needs to confirm course-content equivalency before admission.

Skilled-worker visas & permits

Some skilled-worker visa programs (for example, in regulated professions or where the destination requires occupation-specific credentials) ask for apostilled course descriptions to verify that the applicant's degree included the specific coursework required for the visa category.

What will and won't be accepted

Accepted & rejected course descriptions or syllabi copies.

Will be accepted

  • Registrar-certified course-description packets with university seal and signature
  • Notarized true copies of university catalog pages bound and stamped together
  • Instructor-issued syllabi notarized as true copies (state-dependent)
  • Program outlines and curriculum guides certified by the registrar or department chair
  • Multi-document packets bound together and certified or notarized as a single set

Will be rejected

  • Photocopies or scans without registrar certification or notarization
  • Loose syllabi pulled from a course-management system without any certification
  • Self-certified or notarized photocopies of an existing certified or notarized set
  • Materials where the university name, page numbering, or seal are no longer legible
  • Foreign-issued course descriptions (those must be apostilled by their issuing country)

Course Descriptions & Syllabi Apostille FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

What are course descriptions and syllabi used for abroad?

Foreign credential evaluators, licensing boards, and graduate programs use course descriptions and syllabi to verify that your U.S. coursework matches their specific content requirements — especially for regulated professions like medicine, nursing, engineering, pharmacy, and law. The transcript alone shows course titles and credits; the descriptions and syllabi show what was actually covered.

Where do I get certified course descriptions?

Most universities provide course descriptions through the registrar's office, the academic department, or the catalog archive. Some schools offer formal certified course-description packets; others require you to print catalog pages and have them notarized as true copies. We can guide you to the right office at your university.

Do you handle just course descriptions, or syllabi too?

Both. Course descriptions (the short paragraph summary from the catalog) and syllabi (the detailed week-by-week document from the instructor) are often both required. We apostille either or both, individually or bound together as a single packet.

Can you apostille course descriptions from a university in another state?

Yes. The packet is apostilled in the state where the university issued it. If catalog pages or syllabi need to be notarized as true copies, the notarization must happen in the same state as the apostille. We coordinate both steps for out-of-state cases.

How long does a course descriptions apostille take?

The apostille step is fast — California can often complete it in 1–3 business days. The slower step is usually getting the certified packet from the registrar, which can take 2–6 weeks at many universities. Start as early as possible.

Will the original packet be returned with the apostille?

Yes. The apostille is attached to the certified or notarized packet and returned to you by secure courier, mail, or in-person pickup. We confirm return method in writing before submission.
