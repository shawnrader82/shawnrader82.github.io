Before you start

A short checklist that prevents most notary delays.

We’ll walk through anything missing when you call or text, but these basics usually keep the appointment moving cleanly.

  • Each signer should have valid, government-issued photo ID available at the appointment.
  • Do not sign the documents early unless the document instructions specifically allow it.
  • Confirm whether your documents need witnesses in addition to notarization.
  • For hospital or jail visits, send the facility name and any room, ward, or booking details in advance.
  • If the documents may need apostille afterward, mention that before the appointment so the order of steps is correct.

Special note for sensitive signings

  • Hospital and care-facility visits require the signer to be alert, willing, and able to understand what they are signing.
  • Jail visits work best when we receive inmate details and document copies ahead of time.
  • Estate-planning signings often go smoother when witness planning is handled before the appointment day.

Notary FAQ

Questions clients ask before scheduling a mobile notary visit.

What ID do I need for a mobile notary visit?

Each signer must have valid, government-issued photo ID such as a driver's license, state ID, passport, or other accepted document. If someone's ID is expired or unavailable, let us know in advance so we can discuss options before scheduling.

Can you provide witnesses for my signing?

Some documents require one or more witnesses. In many cases you can provide your own witnesses; for an additional fee we may be able to help arrange witnesses depending on timing and location. We will confirm the requirements before your appointment.

Do you draft legal documents or give legal advice?

No. As notaries we can verify identity, witness signatures, and complete notarial certificates, but we do not draft documents or provide legal advice. If you are unsure what to sign, we always recommend speaking with an attorney first.

How far in advance should I schedule?

Same-day and rush appointments are often available, especially for urgent hospital or jail visits, but booking in advance gives you the widest choice of times. Evenings, weekends, and holidays fill quickly, so it helps to contact us as soon as you know your deadline.

What areas do you cover?

We provide mobile notary services across Los Angeles County and frequently travel to parts of Orange and Ventura Counties. If your documents will later need an apostille or legalization for use abroad, we can connect your notary visit with our apostille services so everything is prepared correctly from the start.

What if the signer is in a hospital or jail?

Let us know the facility name, room or booking details, timing limitations, and document type before we schedule. That helps us confirm access rules, travel planning, and any extra coordination needed before the visit.
