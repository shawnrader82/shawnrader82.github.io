What we do

What a Permit Runner Does.

A permit runner is a licensed courier who physically carries documents between your office and government agency counters. We are not permit expediters — we do not review plans, respond to plan check corrections, provide zoning analysis, or give code compliance opinions. That work belongs to your architect or engineer of record.

What we do: pick up, submit, retrieve, and deliver. We stand in line so your staff does not have to. Because we are also a Notary firm, we can notarize the Agent Authorization Letter LADBS requires for non-owner submittals — on the same pickup trip, at no additional Notary fee.

Built-in advantage

We Notarize Your Agent Authorization Letter on Pickup.

LADBS requires a notarized Agent Authorization Letter for any non-owner submittal. Most permit runners send you to a separate Notary before they will accept the trip. Because we are a licensed California Notary, we notarize your Agent Authorization Letter at the moment we pick up your plans — no separate trip, no separate Notary fee.

This matters most on urgent submittals. If you are racing to meet a plan check deadline, the last thing you need is a side errand. Call us, schedule the pickup, and we handle notarization and delivery in one stop.

Who needs the Agent Authorization Letter?

Any architect, engineer, contractor, or representative submitting plans or picking up permits on behalf of a property owner who will not be present at the counter. LADBS Form No. B-4 (or equivalent) must be notarized and presented at the window. We bring the stamp.

Common questions

Permit Running FAQ.

Do you do permit expediting?

No. We are permit runners, not permit expediters. We do not review plans, respond to plan check corrections, provide zoning analysis, or give code compliance opinions. That work belongs to your architect or engineer of record. We pick up, submit, retrieve, and deliver — physical courier work only.

How fast can you get a permit issued?

Permit issuance speed depends entirely on LADBS's plan check queue and review process — not on us. Once LADBS approves your plans and the permit is ready for pickup, we can retrieve it same-day or next business day. We have no ability to accelerate the city's review.

Do you submit through ePlanLA?

Yes. We can submit electronic plan packages via ePlanLA on your behalf. Because LADBS requires a notarized Agent Authorization Letter for non-owner submittals, we notarize that letter on pickup — saving you a separate Notary trip and getting your submittal moving faster.

Can you pick up blueprints from the LADBS Records Section?

Yes. We can retrieve plan records, permit history, and other documents from the LADBS Records Section at 201 N. Figueroa St. Contact us with the address and permit number so we can confirm availability before the trip.

What is the minimum job size?

A single trip is $95. There is no minimum document count — if you need one plan set dropped off or one permit picked up, that is a standard $95 trip.

Do you work weekends?

LADBS counters and most building department windows are closed on weekends, so plan check submittals and permit pickups are weekday-only. However, we are available for courier delivery of documents between your office and ours, or for notarizing Agent Authorization Letters, on weekends. Call us to confirm availability.
