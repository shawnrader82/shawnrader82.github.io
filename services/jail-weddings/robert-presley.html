Facility Details

What to Expect at Robert Presley Detention Center

Every facility handles inmate marriages differently. Here's what you need to know about getting married at Robert Presley Detention Center.

Approval process: Marriage request submitted through the Riverside County Sheriff’s Department. Approval timelines vary — typically 2 to 4 weeks depending on the inmate’s housing classification.
Ceremony setting: Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area. The facility does not provide a private ceremony room or chapel.
Guest policy: A maximum of two adults and two children are allowed per visit. One witness is required. All visitors must present valid government-issued photo ID.
Visiting hours: Inmates are allowed two visits per week, each approximately 45 minutes. Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 341-8888 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
Items allowed: No items may be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. No cameras, weapons, food, or drink allowed in the visiting area. All visitors are subject to search.
Good to know: RPDC opened in 1989 replacing the old Riverside County Jail. It’s in the heart of downtown Riverside, steps from the courthouse and government buildings. Parking is tight downtown — plan accordingly.

Getting There & Check‑In

Visiting Robert Presley Detention Center for a Wedding

Robert Presley Detention Center is in downtown Riverside on Orange Street, close to the Riverside County courthouse and the 91/60/215 freeway interchange. Downtown parking fills up fast — arrive early.

Directions & Parking

  • Address: 4000 Orange St, Riverside, CA 92501 — in downtown Riverside between 3rd and 4th Streets.
  • From LA: Take the 60 East to downtown Riverside. Exit at Market St or 14th St and head north on Orange St. The facility is on your right. Allow 60 to 90 minutes from downtown LA.
  • From the Inland Empire: Take the 91 or 215 to the 60. Exit at Market St and follow signs to Orange St.
  • Parking: Limited metered street parking on Orange St and surrounding blocks. The downtown parking garage on Mission Inn Ave is the most reliable option. Arrive at least 45 minutes early to park and walk over.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft can drop off on Orange St in front of the facility. Downtown Riverside has reliable rideshare coverage.

What to Know Before Check‑In

  • Scheduling: Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 341-8888 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
  • Visiting hours: Two visits per week per inmate, each approximately 45 minutes. Specific hours vary by housing assignment — confirm when scheduling.
  • Bring only: Valid government-issued photo ID and your car key. All other personal items must stay in your vehicle.
  • Leave in your car: Cell phones, purses, bags, cameras, food, drink, and all electronics. No smoking anywhere on jail property.
  • Arrive early: Plan to arrive at least 30 to 45 minutes before your scheduled visit to clear security and find parking downtown.
  • Dress code: Clothing must cover upper torso and lower/mid body. No gang-affiliated, sexual, lewd, or offensive imagery. Skirts must reach at least mid-thigh.
What the Ceremony Is Like

Your Wedding at Robert Presley Detention Center

Here's what to expect on ceremony day so there are no surprises.

The Visiting Room

  • Ceremonies take place in the regular visiting area — there is no private chapel or ceremony room.
  • Visits are approximately 45 minutes. Our officiant keeps the ceremony focused and meaningful within this window.
  • The visiting format (glass partition or contact) depends on the inmate’s housing assignment and security classification.
  • A maximum of two adults and two children are allowed per visit, including your required witness.

Plan Your Day

  • Total time on-site: Plan for 2 to 3 hours from parking to leaving. Security screening and downtown parking take the bulk of the time.
  • No items exchanged: Rings, gifts, documents, or any physical items cannot be passed to the inmate during the visit.
  • No photography: No cameras, phones, or recording devices are allowed inside the facility.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly. Clothing must cover upper torso and lower body. No gang references, offensive imagery, or revealing clothing.
  • Downtown location: RPDC is walkable from the Riverside courthouse and several restaurants on Mission Inn Ave — convenient if you need to handle other business the same day.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ — Robert Presley Detention Center

How long does a wedding at Robert Presley take to arrange?

The full process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks from the initial marriage request to the ceremony, depending on the Riverside County Sheriff’s approval timeline and scheduling.

What if the inmate gets transferred to another Riverside County facility?

We serve all Riverside County detention facilities. If your partner is transferred to Smith (Banning), Byrd (Murrieta), or Benoit (Indio), we adjust the process and coordinate with the new facility.

Can the ceremony happen on weekends?

Visiting schedules vary by housing assignment. Weekend visits may be available depending on the inmate’s unit. We confirm the exact schedule with the facility when arranging your ceremony.

Can I bring a ring to the ceremony?

No. Robert Presley Detention Center does not allow any items to be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. This is standard at all Riverside County facilities.

What ID does the inmate need?

Valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID is preferred. If the inmate does not have one, contact us — we have solutions.

How much does a jail wedding at Robert Presley cost?

Every case is different. We provide a personalized quote after learning about your situation. No hidden mileage, after-hours, or weekend surcharges.
