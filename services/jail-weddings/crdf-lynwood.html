Getting There & Check‑In

Visiting CRDF Lynwood for a Wedding

Century Regional Detention Facility is the primary women's jail in Los Angeles County, located in Lynwood — about 20 minutes south of downtown LA.

Directions & Parking

  • Address: 11705 Alameda St, Lynwood, CA 90262 — off the 105 freeway near the Alameda St exit.
  • From the 105: Exit at Alameda St, head north. The facility is on your right within half a mile.
  • From downtown LA: Take the 110 South to the 105 East. Exit Alameda. About 20 minutes without traffic.
  • Parking: Visitor parking is available on‑site. Less congested than the downtown facilities, but still arrive early on weekends.

What to Know Before Check‑In

  • Scheduling: Visits should be scheduled through the LASD online visitor system at visit.lasd.org.
  • Visiting hours: Vary by housing assignment. Check the LASD visiting schedule for CRDF before planning your trip.
  • Bring only: Valid government‑issued photo ID and one key. Everything else must be secured in a locker.
  • Leave in your car: Cell phones, purses, bags, cameras, and all electronics.
  • Women's facility: CRDF houses female inmates from across LA County. If your partner was recently arrested, she may still be in processing — confirm housing status before scheduling the visit.
What the Ceremony Is Like

Your Wedding at CRDF Lynwood

Here's what to expect on ceremony day at Century Regional Detention Facility.

The Visiting Room

  • Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area — no private ceremony room is provided.
  • The visiting format (glass or contact) depends on the inmate's housing assignment and classification level.
  • Visit length and frequency follow standard LASD guidelines and vary by housing unit.
  • Your required witness must present valid government‑issued photo ID and will go through the same security screening.

Plan Your Day

  • Total time on‑site: Plan for 2–3 hours including check‑in, screening, and the visit itself.
  • County Clerk distance: CRDF is about 20 minutes from the LA County Clerk's office downtown, where we pick up the marriage license before the ceremony.
  • No items exchanged: Rings, gifts, or documents cannot be passed during the visit.
  • No photography: No phones, cameras, or recording devices inside the facility.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly. Avoid clothing that resembles inmate uniforms. No revealing clothing, open‑toed shoes, or clothing with offensive imagery.
  • Same‑sex marriages: Fully recognized in California and performed at CRDF with the same process as any other marriage.
