What to Expect at Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF)

Every facility handles inmate marriages differently. Here's what you need to know about getting married at Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF).

Approval process: Marriage request submitted to the facility's legal office for approval. Typical approval timeline is 1–3 weeks.
Ceremony setting: Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area.
Guest policy: One witness is required with valid government-issued photo ID.
Visiting hours: Check current LASD visiting schedules for CRDF. Days and times vary by housing assignment.
Items allowed: No items may be exchanged during the ceremony. No electronics, cameras, or recording devices.
Good to know: CRDF is the primary women's facility in Los Angeles County, located in Lynwood. It serves female inmates from across the county. The facility is about 20 minutes south of downtown LA.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ — Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF)

Can two women get married at CRDF?

Yes. Same-sex marriages are legally recognized in California and can be performed at CRDF just like any other marriage.

Is the ceremony behind glass?

The visiting format depends on the inmate's housing assignment and security classification. We confirm the setup during coordination.

Can the ceremony happen on weekends?

Ceremonies are scheduled during regular visiting hours, which may include weekends depending on the housing assignment.

What if my partner is being transferred from CRDF?

If you know a transfer is coming, contact us as soon as possible. We can often expedite the process or coordinate with the receiving facility.

How far is CRDF from the County Clerk?

CRDF in Lynwood is about 20 minutes from the LA County Clerk's office in downtown LA, where we pick up the marriage license.

How much does a jail wedding at CRDF cost?

We provide a personalized quote based on your situation. No hidden mileage, after-hours, or weekend surcharges.
