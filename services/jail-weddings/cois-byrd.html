Facility Details

What to Expect at Cois M. Byrd Detention Center

Every facility handles inmate marriages differently. Here's what you need to know about getting married at Cois M. Byrd Detention Center.

Approval process: Marriage request submitted through the Riverside County Sheriff’s Department. Approval timelines vary — typically 2 to 4 weeks.
Ceremony setting: Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area. No private ceremony room or chapel is provided.
Guest policy: A maximum of two adults and two children per visit. One witness is required. All visitors must present valid government-issued photo ID.
Visiting hours: Inmates are allowed two visits per week, each approximately 45 minutes. Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 696-3060 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
Items allowed: No items may be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. No cameras, food, drink, or electronics. All visitors are subject to search.
Good to know: Byrd Detention Center opened in 1993 and serves the Temecula/Murrieta corridor in southwest Riverside County. The facility is in a semi-rural area of French Valley — the surrounding area has grown significantly, but the immediate vicinity is still quiet. If you’re coming from LA or north county, allow extra time for I-15 traffic through the Temecula corridor.

Getting There & Check‑In

Visiting Cois M. Byrd Detention Center for a Wedding

Cois M. Byrd Detention Center is in the French Valley area between Murrieta and Temecula, just off the I-15. The drive from LA is straightforward but the I-15 through Temecula can be brutal on weekends.

Directions & Parking

  • Address: 30755-B Auld Rd, Murrieta, CA 92563 — in French Valley, east of the I-15.
  • From LA: Take the I-15 South through Corona and Lake Elsinore. Exit at Clinton Keith Rd or Auld Rd and head east. The facility is approximately 1 mile from the freeway. Allow 90 minutes to 2 hours from downtown LA.
  • From Temecula: Take the I-15 North to Auld Rd. Head east. The facility is on the left.
  • Parking: Free visitor parking on-site. The lot is spacious and rarely fills up.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft can drop off at the visitor entrance. Rideshare availability in French Valley is moderate — confirm your return ride before going through security.

What to Know Before Check‑In

  • Scheduling: Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 696-3060 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
  • Visiting hours: Two visits per week per inmate, each approximately 45 minutes. Hours vary by housing assignment — confirm when scheduling.
  • Bring only: Valid government-issued photo ID and your car key. All other personal items stay in your vehicle.
  • Leave in your car: Cell phones, purses, bags, cameras, food, drink, and all electronics.
  • Arrive early: Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your scheduled visit to clear security screening.
  • Dress code: Clothing must cover upper torso and lower/mid body. No gang-affiliated, sexual, lewd, or offensive imagery. Skirts must reach at least mid-thigh.
What the Ceremony Is Like

Your Wedding at Cois M. Byrd Detention Center

Here's what to expect on ceremony day so there are no surprises.

The Visiting Room

  • Ceremonies take place in the regular visiting area — there is no private chapel or ceremony room.
  • Visits are approximately 45 minutes. Our officiant keeps the ceremony focused and meaningful within this window.
  • The visiting format depends on the inmate’s housing assignment and classification.
  • A maximum of two adults and two children are allowed per visit, including your required witness.

Plan Your Day

  • Total time on-site: Plan for 2 to 3 hours from parking to leaving. Security screening takes the bulk of the time beyond the ceremony itself.
  • No items exchanged: Rings, gifts, documents, or any physical items cannot be passed to the inmate during the visit.
  • No photography: No cameras, phones, or recording devices are allowed inside the facility.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly. Clothing must cover upper torso and lower body. No gang references, offensive imagery, or revealing clothing.
  • I-15 weekend traffic: The I-15 through Corona and Temecula is one of the most congested weekend corridors in Southern California. If your ceremony is on a Saturday or Sunday, leave early or consider taking the 15 to the 215 bypass through Perris to avoid the worst of it.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ — Cois M. Byrd Detention Center

How long does a wedding at Byrd Detention Center take to arrange?

The full process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks from the initial marriage request to the ceremony, depending on the Riverside County Sheriff’s approval timeline.

What if the inmate gets transferred to another Riverside County facility?

We serve all Riverside County detention facilities. If your partner is transferred to Robert Presley, Smith, or Benoit, we adjust the process and coordinate with the new facility.

Can the ceremony happen on weekends?

Visiting schedules vary by housing assignment. Weekend visits may be available. We confirm the exact schedule with the facility when arranging your ceremony.

Can I bring a ring to the ceremony?

No. Byrd Detention Center does not allow any items to be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. This is standard at all Riverside County facilities.

What ID does the inmate need?

Valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID is preferred. If the inmate does not have one, contact us — we have solutions.

How much does a jail wedding at Byrd cost?

Every case is different. We provide a personalized quote after learning about your situation. No hidden mileage, after-hours, or weekend surcharges.
