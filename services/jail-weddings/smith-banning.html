Facility Details

What to Expect at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility

Every facility handles inmate marriages differently. Here's what you need to know about getting married at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility.

Approval process: Marriage request submitted through the Riverside County Sheriff's Department. Approval typically takes 2 to 4 weeks, though program-enrolled inmates may have additional coordination requirements.
Ceremony setting: Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area. The facility does not provide a private ceremony room.
Guest policy: A maximum of two adults and two children per visit. One witness is required. All visitors must present valid government-issued photo ID.
Visiting hours: Inmates are allowed two visits per week, each approximately 45 minutes. Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 922-7540 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
Items allowed: No items may be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. No cameras, food, drink, or electronics in the visiting area. All visitors are subject to search.
Good to know: Smith Correctional is the Sheriff's largest facility in Riverside County. It houses several programs including Work Release, Supervised Electronic Confinement, substance abuse treatment, and vocational/educational programs. Inmates in specialty programs may have different visiting arrangements — we'll help you navigate that.

Getting There & Check‑In

Visiting Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility for a Wedding

Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility is in Banning, about 25 miles east of Riverside along the I-10 at the base of the San Gorgonio Pass. Plan for freeway traffic through the pass, especially on weekends.

Directions & Parking

  • Address: 1627 S Hargrave St, Banning, CA 92220 — south of the I-10, off Hargrave Street.
  • From LA: Take the I-10 East through Beaumont and Banning. Exit at Hargrave St and head south. The facility is approximately half a mile on the left. Allow 90 minutes to 2 hours from downtown LA.
  • From Riverside: Take the 60 East to the I-10 East. Exit at Hargrave St. Allow approximately 30 minutes.
  • Parking: Free visitor parking lot on-site. The lot is large and rarely fills up.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft can drop off at the visitor entrance. Rideshare availability in Banning is more limited than in Riverside — arrange your return ride in advance.

What to Know Before Check‑In

  • Scheduling: Visits must be scheduled one day in advance by calling (951) 922-7540 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.
  • Visiting hours: Two visits per week per inmate, each approximately 45 minutes. Hours vary by housing unit — confirm when scheduling.
  • Bring only: Valid government-issued photo ID and your car key. Everything else stays in your vehicle.
  • Leave in your car: Cell phones, purses, bags, cameras, food, drink, and all electronics.
  • Arrive early: Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your scheduled visit to clear security screening.
  • Dress code: Clothing must cover upper torso and lower/mid body. No gang-affiliated, sexual, lewd, or offensive imagery. Skirts must reach at least mid-thigh.
What the Ceremony Is Like

Your Wedding at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility

Here's what to expect on ceremony day so there are no surprises.

The Visiting Room

  • Ceremonies take place in the regular visiting area — there is no private chapel or ceremony room.
  • Visits are approximately 45 minutes. Our officiant keeps the ceremony focused and meaningful within this window.
  • The visiting format depends on the inmate's housing assignment and program enrollment.
  • A maximum of two adults and two children are allowed per visit, including your required witness.

Plan Your Day

  • Total time on-site: Plan for 2 to 3 hours at the facility. Add 3 to 4 hours of round-trip driving from LA.
  • No items exchanged: Rings, gifts, documents, or any physical items cannot be passed to the inmate during the visit.
  • No photography: No cameras, phones, or recording devices are allowed inside the facility.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly. Clothing must cover upper torso and lower body. No gang references, offensive imagery, or revealing clothing.
  • Pass traffic: The I-10 through San Gorgonio Pass can be windy and congested, especially on Friday afternoons and weekends. Check traffic before you leave and allow extra time.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ — Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility

How long does a wedding at Smith Correctional take to arrange?

The full process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks from the initial marriage request to the ceremony. Inmates enrolled in special programs may require additional coordination.

What if the inmate gets transferred to another Riverside County facility?

We serve all Riverside County detention facilities. If your partner is transferred to Robert Presley, Byrd, or Benoit, we adjust the process and coordinate with the new facility.

My partner is in a program at Smith. Can we still get married?

Yes. Program-enrolled inmates (Work Release, substance abuse treatment, vocational programs) may have different visiting arrangements. We'll work with the facility to accommodate your ceremony.

Can I bring a ring to the ceremony?

No. Smith Correctional does not allow any items to be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. This is standard at all Riverside County facilities.

What ID does the inmate need?

Valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID is preferred. If the inmate does not have one, contact us — we have solutions.

How much does a jail wedding at Smith Correctional cost?

Every case is different. We provide a personalized quote after learning about your situation. No hidden mileage, after-hours, or weekend surcharges — even for the drive to Banning.
