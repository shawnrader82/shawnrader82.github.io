Facility Details

What to Expect at Theo Lacy Facility

Every facility handles inmate marriages differently. Here's what you need to know about getting married at Theo Lacy Facility.

Approval process: Marriage request submitted through the Orange County Sheriff's Department. Approval timelines vary — typically 2 to 4 weeks depending on the inmate's housing classification.
Ceremony setting: Ceremonies take place during regular visiting hours in the visiting area. The facility does not provide a private ceremony room or chapel.
Guest policy: One witness is required. Both the witness and the non-incarcerated spouse must present valid government-issued photo ID. Visits are limited to two adults and two minors.
Visiting hours: Friday through Monday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Visits must be scheduled at least 24 hours in advance by calling the Theo Lacy scheduling number. Hours may vary by housing module.
Items allowed: No items may be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. Only your car key and ID are allowed past security. All other belongings must stay in your vehicle.
Good to know: Theo Lacy is the largest jail in Orange County, housing over 3,000 inmates. It's located near The Block shopping center and the Honda Center in the city of Orange — easy to find off the 5 and 22 freeways.

Getting There & Check‑In

Visiting Theo Lacy Facility for a Wedding

Theo Lacy Facility is in the city of Orange, right off the 5 and 22 freeways near The Block at Orange shopping center. It's the most accessible OC jail from both LA County and south Orange County.

Directions & Parking

  • Address: 501 The City Dr S, Orange, CA 92868 — near the intersection of The City Drive and Chapman Ave.
  • From LA: Take the 5 South to the 22 East. Exit at City Drive South. The facility is on your left. Allow 30 to 60 minutes from downtown LA depending on traffic.
  • From South OC: Take the 5 North to The City Drive exit. The facility is approximately half a mile from the freeway.
  • Parking: Free visitor parking lot on-site. The lot is generally manageable, but arrive early on weekends when visiting traffic peaks.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft can drop off at the visitor entrance on The City Drive South.

What to Know Before Check‑In

  • Scheduling: Visits must be scheduled at least 24 hours in advance by calling (714) 935-6202. Walk-in visits are not accepted.
  • Visiting hours: Friday through Monday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Specific housing modules may have restricted hours — confirm when scheduling.
  • Bring only: Valid government-issued photo ID and your car key. Everything else must remain in your vehicle.
  • Leave in your car: Cell phones, purses, bags, cameras, food, and all electronics. There are no lockers — leave everything locked in your vehicle.
  • Arrive early: Plan to arrive at least 30 minutes before your scheduled visit to clear security screening.
What the Ceremony Is Like

Your Wedding at Theo Lacy Facility

Here's what to expect on ceremony day so there are no surprises.

The Visiting Room

  • Ceremonies take place in the regular visiting area — there is no private chapel or ceremony room.
  • Visits are typically 30 minutes. Our officiant keeps the ceremony focused and meaningful within this window.
  • The visiting format (glass partition or face-to-face) depends on the inmate's housing assignment and classification.
  • Visits are limited to two adults and two minors, including your required witness.

Plan Your Day

  • Total time on-site: Plan for 2 to 3 hours from parking to leaving. Security screening and processing take the bulk of the time, even though the ceremony itself fits within a 30-minute visit.
  • No items exchanged: Rings, gifts, documents, or any physical items cannot be passed to the inmate during the visit.
  • No photography: No cameras, phones, or recording devices are allowed inside the facility.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly. Avoid clothing resembling inmate uniforms (solid orange). No revealing clothing, open-toed shoes, or clothing with offensive imagery.
  • Nearby amenities: The Block at Orange shopping center is across the street — a convenient spot to wait or grab food before or after your visit.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ — Theo Lacy Facility

How long does a wedding at Theo Lacy take to arrange?

The full process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks from the initial marriage request to the ceremony, depending on the Orange County Sheriff's approval timeline and scheduling.

What if the inmate gets transferred to Central Jail or Musick?

We serve all Orange County detention facilities. If your partner is transferred to Central Men's Jail, Central Women's Jail, or James A. Musick, we adjust the process and coordinate with the new facility.

Can the ceremony happen on weekends?

Yes. Visiting hours at Theo Lacy include Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. We schedule the ceremony during regular visiting hours.

Can I bring a ring to the ceremony?

No. Theo Lacy does not allow any items to be exchanged during the ceremony, including rings. This is standard at all OC Sheriff facilities.

What ID does the inmate need?

Valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID is preferred. If the inmate does not have one, contact us — we have solutions.

How much does a jail wedding at Theo Lacy cost?

Every case is different. We provide a personalized quote after learning about your situation. No hidden mileage, after-hours, or weekend surcharges.
