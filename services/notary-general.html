Mobile Office at the Airport

On-site laptop, printer, and scanner

If you don’t have your forms ready, we can create them together on-site. I bring a laptop, secure internet connection, and portable printer so we can generate, print, sign, and notarize your documents before you head to security.

Whether you need a minor travel consent letter, a power of attorney before a long trip, or a last‑minute affidavit, we can pull up templates, customize them for your situation, and print clean copies right at the terminal or hotel.

Important: I’m a commissioned California notary public, not an attorney. I can’t tell you which document to use or give legal advice, but I can help you access and print the forms you choose and make sure the notarization itself is completed correctly.

What We Usually Notarize

Common notarizations at airports and hotels

Many travelers discover at the last minute that they need something notarized. These are some of the documents I’m most often called to handle at LAX and nearby airport hotels.

Minor travel consent letters

Parental consent letters for children traveling with one parent, relatives, school groups, or other caregivers, often required by airlines and border officials.

Powers of attorney before departure

General and financial powers of attorney signed right before an extended trip, so someone you trust can handle banking, real estate, or legal matters while you’re away.

Affidavits and sworn statements

Sworn statements for immigration, employment, or personal matters that must be signed and notarized before you arrive at your destination.

Business & courier documents

Time‑sensitive contracts, courier releases, and other business documents that need a notary’s seal before they can be shipped or handed off to a third party.

If you’re not sure whether your document can be notarized at the airport, call or text (213) 933‑2507 with a photo of the first page and I’ll confirm what’s possible before we schedule.
