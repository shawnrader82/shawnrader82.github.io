Realistic expectations

How long does this really take?

Timelines depend on which government office processes your document and whether the destination country needs consular legalization on top.

State apostille (CA Secretary of State)

  • California walk-in counter: often same-day if submitted before cutoff.
  • Mail-in: typically 3-10 business days.
  • Rush: same-day state apostille routes for qualifying California documents.

Federal apostille (U.S. Department of State)

  • Standard mail: 4-8 weeks.
  • Expedited: available in limited cases for additional fees.

What slows things down

  • Wrong document format or missing notarization.
  • Submitting to the wrong office (state vs. federal).
  • Outdated records that need to be reissued.
  • Consular backlogs at non-Hague embassies.

What speeds things up

  • Having certified copies ready before contact.
  • Telling us your deadline up front.
  • Using same-day California walk-in options for urgent state apostilles.

Apostille FAQ

Common questions about apostille and legalization.

Do I need an apostille or consular legalization?

It depends on the destination country. Hague Apostille Convention countries usually accept a single apostille from the correct state or federal office. Non-Hague countries typically require state or federal authentication followed by consular legalization at the destination country’s embassy or consulate.

Can I use a photocopy of my document?

Usually no. Most apostille offices require certified copies of vital records, original government-issued documents, or properly notarized originals. Plain photocopies are typically rejected.

What is the fastest way to know what I need?

Call or text (213) 933-2507 with three things: your document type, the destination country, and your deadline. We’ll confirm whether you need a state apostille, federal apostille, or consular legalization before you book.

How long does an apostille take?

California Secretary of State apostilles can often be completed same-day at the California walk-in counter or 3-10 business days by mail. U.S. Department of State federal apostilles typically take 4-8 weeks by mail, with limited expedited options. Consular legalization timelines vary by country. For background on our service and apostille experience, visit our about page.

Do you handle translations too?

When the destination country requires a certified or sworn translation alongside the apostille, we can coordinate it in the right sequence so everything arrives in the format the receiving authority expects. Translation is supporting work for an apostille order rather than a standalone service.

Do I need to be in Los Angeles to work with you?

No. We help clients across California and from other U.S. states by mail. You can ship documents to us and we return them by secure courier after apostille or legalization.
