Joint Tenancy With Right of Survivorship

When two people hold property as joint tenants, the survivor automatically inherits 100% of the property when the other dies. The Affidavit of Death is the mechanism that clears the deceased person's name from the public record.

Common questions

Affidavit of Death of Joint Tenant FAQ.

What is an Affidavit of Death of Joint Tenant?

It is a document signed under penalty of perjury by the surviving joint tenant (or authorized person) confirming that the other joint tenant has died. When recorded with a certified copy of the death certificate, it removes the deceased person's name from the title without a court probate proceeding.

What documents do I need to file this?

You need the completed and notarized Affidavit of Death of Joint Tenant and a certified copy of the death certificate. The death certificate must be an official certified copy issued by the county or state — a photocopy or informational copy will not be accepted by the recorder.

Does this avoid probate?

For property held in joint tenancy with right of survivorship, yes — this affidavit bypasses the probate process entirely. The surviving joint tenant automatically inherits the property; the affidavit simply clears the public record.

What is the difference between joint tenancy and community property?

Joint tenancy includes a right of survivorship — when one owner dies, the other automatically takes 100% of the property. Community property with right of survivorship works similarly for married couples. A separate Affidavit of Death may be used for community property situations — consult your attorney on which form applies.

Will recording this trigger property tax reassessment?

Generally no, for a surviving joint tenant. However, Proposition 19 changed some parent-child and grandparent-grandchild exclusions. Consult a property tax attorney or the county assessor's office for your specific situation.

How long does recording take?

Walk-in recording at the county recorder's office is typically same-day. We bring the affidavit and certified death certificate, pay the fees, and return the recorder-stamped conformed copy to you.

Can you notarize the affidavit and then record it in the same trip?

Yes. We notarize the affidavit at your location — home, office, or wherever is convenient — and then drive to the recorder's office to file it in the same trip.

What if the property is held in a trust, not joint tenancy?

If the property was in a revocable trust, the process is different — an Affidavit of Death of Trustee or Successor Trustee affidavit may be used instead. We record those as well. Contact us with your specific situation and we will confirm the correct document type.
