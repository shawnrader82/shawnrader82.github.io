When to use a quitclaim deed

Common Quitclaim Deed Situations.

Divorce Property Settlement

When a divorce judgment or marital settlement agreement awards property to one spouse, the other executes a quitclaim deed to transfer their interest.

  • Grantor must be available to sign in front of a notary
  • Inter-spousal Transfer Grant Deed is an alternative
  • Consult your family law attorney on which form to use

Inter-Spousal Transfer

Moving property between spouses for refinancing, estate planning, or lender requirements — without triggering property tax reassessment under Proposition 19.

  • Common when one spouse is removing themselves from title before a refinance
  • PCOR marks as non-reassessable inter-spousal transfer
  • Does not remove the grantor from the existing mortgage

Gift or Family Transfer

Adding a child, sibling, or other family member to title — or gifting the property entirely. No money changes hands but the deed must still be recorded.

  • Gift deed — PCOR required, mark as gift
  • Adding a co-owner creates a new tenancy structure
  • Prop 19 may limit reassessment exclusions — consult an attorney

Common questions

Quitclaim Deed Recording FAQ.

What is a quitclaim deed?

A quitclaim deed transfers whatever interest the grantor holds in a property — with no warranty that the title is clear or that the grantor actually owns anything. It is commonly used for family transfers, divorce settlements, inter-spousal transfers, and fixing errors in how a name appears on title.

Does a quitclaim deed need to be notarized?

Yes. A quitclaim deed must be notarized before the county recorder will accept it for recording. Each grantor must sign in front of a notary.

What is an inter-spousal transfer deed?

An inter-spousal transfer deed is a type of grant or quitclaim deed used between spouses to transfer property without triggering property tax reassessment. It is common when refinancing, separating, or removing a non-borrowing spouse from title.

Do I need a quitclaim deed for a divorce property settlement?

If one spouse is awarded the property in a divorce, a quitclaim deed from the transferring spouse to the receiving spouse is typically prepared by the divorce attorney and must be notarized and recorded to update the public record.

Does a quitclaim deed eliminate my mortgage liability?

No. Recording a quitclaim deed only changes who holds title — it does not remove your name from the loan. Lenders do not release you from the note because a deed was recorded. Consult your lender or attorney about refinancing or assumption.

What county fees apply to a quitclaim deed?

The same fees as other deeds: first page recording fee + additional pages + SB2 ($75) + Real Estate Fraud Prevention Fee ($10) + AB 1466 ($2). Use our calculator for exact amounts by county.

Can you record a quitclaim deed the same day?

In most cases, yes. Walk-in recording is available at the main county offices. Call or text us and we can confirm current wait times and availability.

Do I need a PCOR with a quitclaim deed?

Usually yes. A Preliminary Change of Ownership Report must accompany most deeds. For inter-spousal transfers and certain family transfers, special exemption codes apply. Your attorney or escrow officer should advise on which boxes to check.
