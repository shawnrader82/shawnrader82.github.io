The Process

How a Jail Wedding Works

Most jail weddings follow these five steps. The full process typically takes 1–4 weeks depending on the facility, approval timelines, and scheduling availability.

Step 1 — Facility Approval

When required, we help you obtain approval to marry at the facility where the inmate is housed. Some facilities, such as LA County jails, require prior approval from the Sheriff's legal department.

Step 2 — Notary Visit to the Inmate

Our notary visits the inmate at the facility to sign the required marriage paperwork, including the Affidavit of Inability to Appear at the County Clerk's office.

Step 3 — Marriage License Pickup

Our minister accompanies the non‑incarcerated spouse to the County Clerk to purchase the marriage license. By regulation, the minister must be present when an inmate is involved.

Step 4 — Wedding Ceremony

We meet both parties at the facility during visiting hours and officiate the marriage ceremony on site.

Step 5 — Filing & Certificate

After the ceremony, the minister files the signed marriage license with the County Recorder. Your official marriage certificate arrives within 3–10 weeks depending on the county.

Common Questions

Jail Wedding FAQ

Answers to the questions we hear most from families coordinating a jail or prison wedding.

How long does a jail wedding take from start to finish?

The full process typically takes 1 to 4 weeks depending on the facility, approval timelines, and scheduling availability.

What if the inmate does not have a valid ID?

Valid, unexpired government‑issued photo ID is preferred, but if the inmate does not have one, we have solutions and can still work with you. Contact us to discuss your situation.

Can you perform weddings at both county jails and state prisons?

Yes. We handle both county jail and state prison weddings across Southern California.

Are there extra fees for mileage, after‑hours, or weekends?

No. There are no hidden mileage, after‑hours, or weekend surcharges. Every case is different, so we provide a personalized quote after learning about your situation.

Do all facilities allow jail weddings?

Most county jails and state prisons allow marriages, but some ICE and federal facilities do not permit them. Contact us and we can advise you on whether your specific facility allows it.

Can photos be taken during the ceremony?

Most jails and prisons do not allow electronics or photography inside the facility. We recommend checking with the specific facility for their current visitor policies.
