How our visits at Providence Saint Joseph work

What to expect when you book

We coordinate with you by phone or text to confirm the patient’s legal name, room number, and the documents that need notarization. When we arrive, we check in at the main entrance or information desk, follow hospital visitor procedures, and then meet you in the patient room or a designated family area.

Providence Saint Joseph allows visitors 24 hours a day. If we are arriving after 7:00 PM, we enter through the Emergency Department entrance and check in with the desk clerk on duty. All visitors must check in and check out upon arrival and departure. Once checked in, we are escorted to the nurse’s station.

Our goal is to keep the visit calm, efficient, and respectful of doctors and nurses who may need access to the patient. If the patient is in the ICU, cancer center, undergoing tests, or has visitor restrictions, let us know ahead of time so we can plan accordingly.

Travel time from Granada Hills depends on 118, 5, and 134 freeway traffic, so we will confirm an arrival window when you book that accounts for current conditions.

Preparing for your appointment

What to have ready at Providence Saint Joseph

To keep your visit smooth, please have:

  • A valid, government-issued photo ID for each signer (driver license, state ID, passport, or other accepted identification)
  • All pages of the documents printed and ready, not just the signature page (unless using our laptop/printer service)
  • Any required witnesses who are not named in the document and who meet the hospital’s visitor rules

If the patient has limited mobility or is unable to sign in their usual way, let us know beforehand so we can discuss acceptable options under California notary law.

If ID is at home, we recommend having a family member bring it to the hospital before the appointment. In some cases, credible witnesses may be an option; we will discuss this with you in advance based on California law.

What if the patient is being treated for a serious illness and may not be fully alert?

We must confirm that the signer understands what they are signing and is acting willingly. For patients dealing with serious illness, pain, or medication effects, we recommend scheduling during a time when they are typically most alert — often mid-morning after medications have stabilized. If we arrive and the patient is not able to proceed, we can wait or reschedule rather than rush through a signing that might not be valid.

Can family members from out of town be present via video call during the signing?

Family members can observe via video call for their own peace of mind, but only the people physically present can sign documents or serve as witnesses. If a family member needs to sign as well (for example, as an agent accepting a power of attorney), they would need their own notarization, which could potentially be done via remote online notarization depending on the document type and their location.

Is there anything specific about Providence hospitals I should know?

Providence facilities generally have straightforward visitor policies. Providence Saint Joseph allows 24-hour visitation, which gives us more flexibility for evening and weekend notary appointments. If the patient is in a restricted area like the ICU or a specialized unit, confirm with nursing staff that a notary visit is permitted and coordinate a time that works with their care schedule. We are experienced with Providence facilities and can adapt to their processes.
