About Northern Branch Jail

The Northern Branch Jail is a 376-bed facility located at 2301 Black Road, Santa Maria, CA 93455. Operated by the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office, it houses male and female inmates in medium and high-security housing units.

Many social visits for Santa Barbara County inmates are handled at the Northern Branch Jail through a centralized ViaPath scheduling system. This facility is separate from the Santa Barbara County Main Jail on Calle Real — the two are about 65 miles apart. Make sure you confirm which facility the inmate is housed at before scheduling.

Why inmates at Northern Branch Jail need notary services

  • Time-sensitive legal matters — Court deadlines, plea agreements, declarations, and other filings that must be signed while the inmate is in custody.
  • Powers of attorney — Allowing a spouse, family member, or attorney to manage finances, property, or legal matters on the inmate's behalf.
  • Real estate and business transactions — Deeds, escrow documents, refinance paperwork, and business authorizations that cannot wait until release.

How it works

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at Northern Branch Jail

1. Share the inmate's information and documents

Call or text with the inmate's full legal name, booking or CID number if available, and a description of the documents to be notarized. Let us know your deadline so we can prioritize scheduling.

2. We coordinate with the Northern Branch

We contact the Northern Branch Jail to confirm housing location, current visiting procedures, and how professional visits are handled. We work within the ViaPath scheduling framework and coordinate with the watch commander or designated staff to arrange the notary visit.

3. Prepare your documents

Have all documents complete and ready before the appointment. We cannot draft legal documents or provide legal advice — our role is to verify identity, witness signatures, and complete the notarization. If the inmate needs to review documents ahead of time, coordinate with their attorney or send copies through the jail's mail or email systems.

4. The notary appointment at the facility

We travel from Los Angeles to Santa Maria, check in with custody staff, and are escorted to the designated visiting area. The inmate confirms their identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarial certificate.

5. Returning the signed and notarized documents

After the visit, we return the originals to you, your attorney, a title or escrow company, or any other designated recipient. Depending on your deadlines, we can hand-deliver locally, overnight by courier, or return by mail.

Common Questions

Northern Branch Jail Notary FAQs

Where is the Northern Branch Jail?

The Northern Branch Jail is located at 2301 Black Road, Santa Maria, CA 93455. It is a 376-bed Santa Barbara County facility that houses male and female inmates in medium and high-security housing units.

Are independent mobile notaries allowed at the Northern Branch Jail?

Yes. Independent mobile notaries can provide services at the Northern Branch Jail. The notary coordinates with the watch commander, checks in with custody staff, and completes the notarization during a professional visit.

What are the visiting hours at the Northern Branch Jail?

The Northern Branch Jail offers visits Sunday through Saturday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Visits are scheduled through the ViaPath online registration system. Appointments start on the hour, and visitors should check in 15 to 30 minutes before their scheduled time.

How many visits are inmates allowed?

Inmates at the Northern Branch Jail are generally allowed up to four visits per week. All visitors must be on the inmate's approved visitor list and must check in with the same photo ID used to register.

Is this the same facility as the Santa Barbara County Main Jail?

No. The Northern Branch Jail in Santa Maria and the Main Jail on Calle Real in Santa Barbara are separate facilities about 65 miles apart. Both are operated by the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office. Many social visits for county inmates are handled here at the Northern Branch.

How long does a notary appointment at the Northern Branch take?

Plan for 5 to 7 hours total, including travel from Los Angeles to Santa Maria (approximately 2.5 hours each way), check-in with custody staff, wait time, and the signing itself. The notarization portion is quick, but the distance adds significant travel time.

Can I look up an inmate at the Northern Branch Jail?

Yes. You can search the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office inmate locator online or call the Northern Branch Jail at (805) 554‑3100 with the inmate's name and date of birth.
