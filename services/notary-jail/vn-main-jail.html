About Ventura County Main Jail (Pre‑Trial Detention Facility)

The Ventura County Main Jail, officially known as the Pre‑Trial Detention Facility (PTDF), is the county's primary booking and pre‑trial detention facility. Operated by the Ventura County Sheriff's Office, it houses inmates awaiting trial, sentencing, or transfer to other facilities.

  • Address: 800 S Victoria Ave, Ventura, CA 93009
  • Main phone: (805) 654‑3335
  • Operated by: Ventura County Sheriff’s Office
  • Capacity: Approximately 814 beds
  • Public reception lobby: 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM daily
  • Visiting days: Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, Sunday
  • Visiting sessions: Morning 8:30 AM – 11:30 AM; Afternoon 1:30 PM – 4:30 PM; Evening 7:00 PM – 9:00 PM (assigned by housing unit)
  • Check‑in: Starts 1 hour before session; closes 30 minutes before session ends
  • Location: Approximately 65 minutes from Los Angeles via US‑101

Why inmates at the Main Jail need notary services

  • Active court cases — Declarations, affidavits, and other documents needed before hearings and trial dates.
  • Family and financial planning — Powers of attorney so trusted relatives can manage finances, property, and legal matters.
  • Real estate and business issues — Deeds, contracts, and business documents that cannot wait until after release.
  • Emergency situations — Time‑sensitive paperwork for family, custody, or medical decisions while the person is in custody.

The Main Jail primarily houses pre‑trial detainees. If the inmate has already been sentenced, they may have been transferred to Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula. We confirm the correct facility before scheduling any visit.

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at Ventura County Main Jail

1. Contact us with the inmate’s information

Call or text with the inmate’s full legal name, booking number (if known), and a description of the documents that need to be notarized. Let us know your deadline so we can prioritize scheduling.

2. We confirm Main Jail procedures

Ventura County facilities have specific visiting sessions by housing unit on Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. We contact the Main Jail at (805) 654‑3335 to confirm the inmate’s availability and coordinate a professional visit, verifying that they are housed at the Main Jail and not Todd Road.

3. Prepare your documents

Have all documents ready and complete before the appointment — we cannot draft legal documents, only notarize signatures. If the inmate needs to review documents first, coordinate with their attorney or use the facility’s mail system.

4. The notary appointment in Ventura

We travel from Los Angeles to the Ventura County Main Jail (approximately 65 minutes via US‑101), clear security screening, and meet with the inmate in the designated visiting area. All visitors are subject to search, and no cell phones, wallets, or personal items are allowed in the visiting area. The inmate confirms their identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarization.

5. Document delivery

After the appointment, we return the signed and notarized documents to you, your attorney, escrow company, or other designated recipient — by hand delivery, courier, or mail depending on your timeline.

Common Questions

Ventura County Main Jail Notary FAQs

Where is Ventura County Main Jail located?

The Main Jail (Pre‑Trial Detention Facility) is located at 800 S Victoria Ave, Ventura, CA 93009. It is approximately 65 minutes from Los Angeles via US‑101.

How do I confirm someone is housed at Ventura County Main Jail?

You can search the Ventura County Sheriff’s inmate locator online or call (805) 654‑3335. Be sure to confirm whether the inmate is at the Main Jail or at Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula.

What are the visiting days and sessions at the Main Jail?

Visiting days are Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. Sessions are typically morning (8:30 AM – 11:30 AM), afternoon (1:30 PM – 4:30 PM), and evening (7:00 PM – 9:00 PM), assigned by housing unit. Check‑in starts 1 hour before each session.

What can I bring into the Main Jail during a visit?

Only your personal key ring, photo ID, and one baby bottle or blanket if bringing an infant. No cell phones, wallets, purses, backpacks, food, beverages, cameras, or reading materials are allowed. The facility does not hold items and there are no storage lockers.

What is the difference between Ventura County Main Jail and Todd Road Jail?

The Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura primarily houses pre‑trial detainees and handles initial booking. Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula primarily houses sentenced inmates and those in longer‑term custody. They are separate facilities with different addresses and visiting schedules.

How long should I plan for a Ventura Main Jail notary appointment?

Plan for 3 to 4 hours total including travel from Los Angeles (approximately 65 minutes each way via US‑101), security screening, wait time, and the signing.
