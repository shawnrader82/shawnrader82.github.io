About Santa Barbara County Main Jail

The Santa Barbara County Main Jail is located at 4436 Calle Real, Santa Barbara, CA 93110 and is operated by the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office. It houses male and female inmates who are awaiting trial, sentencing, or serving county jail sentences, and provides medical, nutritional, legal, and rehabilitative services during incarceration.

This is the county's primary detention facility. Santa Barbara County also operates the Northern Branch Jail in Santa Maria, a separate 376-bed facility about 65 miles north. Make sure you confirm which facility the inmate is housed at before scheduling.

  • Address: 4436 Calle Real, Santa Barbara, CA 93110
  • Main phone: (805) 681‑4260
  • Operated by: Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office
  • Housing: Male and female inmates
  • Location: Santa Barbara, approximately 1.5–2 hours from Los Angeles depending on traffic

Why inmates at Santa Barbara County Jail need notary services

  • Time-sensitive legal matters — Court deadlines, plea agreements, declarations, and other filings that must be signed while the inmate is in custody.
  • Powers of attorney — Allowing a spouse, family member, or attorney to manage finances, property, or legal matters on the inmate's behalf.
  • Real estate and business transactions — Deeds, escrow documents, refinance paperwork, and business authorizations that cannot wait until release.
  • Family and immigration documents — Custody agreements, support stipulations, and immigration-related affidavits that require a notarized signature.

How it works

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at Santa Barbara County Jail

1. Share the inmate's information and documents

Call or text with the inmate's full legal name, booking or CID number if available, and a description of the documents to be notarized. Let us know your deadline so we can prioritize scheduling.

2. We coordinate with custody staff

We contact the Main Jail to confirm housing location, current visiting procedures, and how professional visits are handled. We confirm the inmate is at the Calle Real facility and not the Northern Branch Jail in Santa Maria.

3. Prepare your documents

Have all documents complete and ready before the appointment. We cannot draft legal documents or provide legal advice — our role is to verify identity, witness signatures, and complete the notarization. If the inmate needs to review documents ahead of time, coordinate with their attorney or send copies through the jail's mail or email systems.

4. The notary appointment at the jail

We travel from Los Angeles to Santa Barbara, check in with custody staff, and are escorted to a non-contact visiting area. The inmate confirms their identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarial certificate.

5. Returning the signed and notarized documents

After the visit, we return the originals to you, your attorney, a title or escrow company, or any other designated recipient. Depending on your deadlines, we can hand-deliver locally, overnight by courier, or return by mail.

Common Questions

Santa Barbara County Jail Notary FAQs

Where is the Santa Barbara County Main Jail?

The Santa Barbara County Main Jail is located at 4436 Calle Real, Santa Barbara, CA 93110. It is the county's primary jail and houses male and female inmates awaiting trial or serving county sentences.

Are independent mobile notaries allowed at Santa Barbara County Jail?

Yes. Independent mobile notaries regularly provide services at the Santa Barbara County Main Jail. The notary checks in with custody staff, is escorted to a non-contact visiting area, and completes the notarization with the inmate.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization?

Custody staff maintain booking records with photos and identifying information that can be used to confirm the inmate's identity. If the inmate has a valid government-issued ID on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options with the facility before the appointment.

How long does a notary appointment at the Main Jail take?

Plan for 3 to 5 hours total, including travel from Los Angeles, check-in with custody staff, escort to and from the visiting area, wait time, and the signing itself. The notarization portion is quick, but facility procedures and distance add time.

Is this the same facility as the Northern Branch Jail?

No. The Main Jail on Calle Real in Santa Barbara and the Northern Branch Jail on Black Road in Santa Maria are separate facilities about 65 miles apart. Both are operated by the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office. Make sure you know which facility the inmate is housed at before scheduling.

Can I look up an inmate at the Santa Barbara County Jail?

Yes. You can search the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office inmate locator online or call the Main Jail at (805) 681‑4260 with the inmate's name and date of birth.
