About Ventura County Main Jail

The Ventura County Main Jail, officially known as the Pre-Trial Detention Facility (PTDF), is located at 800 South Victoria Avenue in Ventura, California. It is operated by the Ventura County Sheriff's Office and serves as the county's primary booking and pre-trial detention facility. The facility has a capacity of approximately 814 beds and houses inmates awaiting trial, sentencing, or transfer to other facilities.

The Main Jail's Public Reception Lobby is open from 6:00 AM to 11:00 PM daily. Visiting days are Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday, with multiple sessions per day assigned by housing unit. The facility is approximately 65 minutes from Los Angeles via US-101, making it one of the closer out-of-county facilities we serve.

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at Ventura Main Jail

  1. Contact us with the inmate's information

    Call or text with the inmate's full legal name, booking number (if known), and a description of the documents that need to be notarized. Let us know your deadline so we can prioritize scheduling.

  2. We coordinate with the facility

    Ventura County facilities have specific visiting sessions by housing unit on Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. We contact the Main Jail at (805) 654-3335 to confirm the inmate's availability and coordinate a professional visit. We verify the inmate is housed at the Main Jail and not Todd Road before making the trip.

  3. Prepare your documents

    Have all documents ready and complete before the appointment — we cannot draft legal documents, only notarize signatures. If the inmate needs to review documents first, coordinate with their attorney or use the facility's mail system.

  4. The notary appointment

    We travel from Los Angeles to the Ventura County Main Jail (approximately 65 minutes via US-101), clear security screening, and meet with the inmate in the designated visiting area. All visitors are subject to search. No cell phones, wallets, or personal items are allowed in the visiting area. The inmate confirms their identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarization.

  5. Document delivery

    After the appointment, we return the signed and notarized documents to you, your attorney, escrow company, or other designated recipient — by hand delivery, courier, or mail depending on your timeline.

Tip: The Main Jail primarily houses pre-trial detainees. If the inmate has already been sentenced, they may have been transferred to Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula. Call us and we'll confirm the correct facility before scheduling. Check-in at the Public Reception Lobby opens 1 hour before each session and closes 30 minutes before the session ends — arriving late means the visit is denied.

Ventura Main Jail Notary FAQs

Where is the Ventura County Main Jail?

The Main Jail (Pre-Trial Detention Facility) is located at 800 S Victoria Ave, Ventura, CA 93009. It is approximately 65 minutes from Los Angeles via US-101.

How do I find out if someone is at the Main Jail?

You can search the Ventura County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call (805) 654-3335. Be sure to confirm whether the inmate is at the Main Jail or Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula.

What are the visiting hours at the Main Jail?

Visiting days are Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. There are three sessions: morning (8:30 AM – 11:30 AM), afternoon (1:30 PM – 4:30 PM), and evening (7:00 PM – 9:00 PM). Sessions are assigned by housing unit. Check-in starts 1 hour before each session.

What can I bring into the visiting area?

Only your personal key ring, photo ID, and one baby bottle or blanket if bringing an infant. No cell phones, wallets, purses, backpacks, food, beverages, cameras, or reading materials are allowed. The facility will not hold items for you and there are no storage lockers.

What is the difference between the Main Jail and Todd Road Jail?

The Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura primarily houses pre-trial detainees and handles initial booking. Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula primarily houses sentenced inmates in longer-term custody. They are separate facilities with different addresses and visiting schedules.

How long does a notary appointment at the Main Jail take?

Plan for 3 to 4 hours total including travel from Los Angeles (approximately 65 minutes each way via US-101), security screening, wait time, and the signing.
