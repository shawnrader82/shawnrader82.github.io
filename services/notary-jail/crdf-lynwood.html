About Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF)

Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF) in Lynwood is the primary women’s jail for Los Angeles County. It houses women awaiting trial as well as those serving sentences, with a wide range of security levels and housing units.

  • Address: 11705 South Alameda Street, Lynwood, CA 90262
  • Phone: (323) 568‑4500
  • Inmate information: (213) 473‑6100
  • Population: Women in custody for a variety of cases and sentence lengths
  • Operated by: Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department
  • Professional visiting hours: Monday–Friday, 9:15 AM – 4:00 PM
  • Location: South of the 105 freeway, between the 110 and 710 freeways

Why women at CRDF need notary services

  • Family and custody matters — Documents for child custody, guardianship, school authorizations, and visitation arrangements.
  • Financial and property decisions — Powers of attorney so trusted relatives can manage bank accounts, bills, and property.
  • Marriage, divorce, and support — Paperwork related to marriage, legal separation, divorce, and child or spousal support.
  • Real estate and vehicles — Deeds, titles, and other documents needed to sell or manage homes, cars, and other assets.

How it works

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at CRDF Lynwood

1. Contact us with the inmate’s information

Call or text with the inmate’s full legal name, booking number (if known), and a description of the documents that need to be notarized. Let us know your deadline or any upcoming court dates.

2. We confirm CRDF procedures

Century Regional Detention Facility has its own schedule and rules for professional visits. We confirm the current procedures, visiting hours, and any approvals needed for a notary to meet with the inmate.

3. Prepare your documents

Have all documents ready and complete before the appointment — we cannot draft legal documents, only notarize signatures. If the inmate needs to review documents first, coordinate with their attorney or use the facility’s mail system.

4. The notary appointment in Lynwood

We travel to CRDF, clear security screening, and meet with the inmate in the designated professional visiting area. The inmate confirms her identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarization with the appropriate certificate.

5. Document delivery

After the appointment, we return the signed and notarized documents to you, your attorney, court, or other designated recipient — by hand delivery in the LA area, courier, or mail depending on your timeline.

Common Questions

CRDF Lynwood Notary FAQs

How do I confirm someone is at CRDF Lynwood?

You can search the LA County Sheriff’s Inmate Information Center online at app5.lasd.org/iic using the inmate’s name and date of birth. The system will list Century Regional Detention Facility if that is her current location.

Can CRDF notarizations help with child custody or guardianship?

Yes. Many appointments at CRDF involve documents related to child custody, temporary guardianship, and school or medical authorizations. We work with you and, if needed, your attorney to ensure the documents are ready to sign.

Can I bring the documents to CRDF myself?

Professional notary visits are separate from regular family visitation. You will need to get documents to us before the appointment, and we will bring them into the facility and return them after notarization.

What ID does the inmate need?

CRDF maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification purposes. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver’s license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

How far in advance should I schedule a CRDF appointment?

Whenever possible, contact us several days before your deadline so we can coordinate a time that works with the facility’s schedule. For urgent situations, call or text as soon as you know you need the appointment and we will review options.

What if the inmate is moved to another jail?

Women may sometimes be transferred between CRDF and other facilities. We recommend confirming her location close to the appointment time. If she has been moved, we can usually reschedule for the new facility with an updated quote if travel distance changes.
