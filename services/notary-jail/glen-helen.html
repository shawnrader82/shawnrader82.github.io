About Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center

The Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center is located on 9 acres of land in the Devore area of San Bernardino, approximately 12 miles northwest of downtown San Bernardino. Originally opened in 1960 as a work camp with a capacity of 100 inmates, the facility has expanded significantly and now has a maximum capacity of 1,446 inmates with an average daily population of approximately 800 male and female inmates. Glen Helen is San Bernardino County's primary facility for housing both male and female sentenced county inmates.

The Male Facility includes two Minimum Security Housing Units and a Maximum Security Housing Unit. The Female Facility, opened in 1988, includes three dormitory units and an additional Maximum Security Unit added in 2003, housing both pre-trial and sentenced females with a capacity of 326.

Why inmates at Glen Helen need notary services

  • Time-sensitive legal matters — Court deadlines, plea agreements, and filings that cannot wait until release.

Scheduling a Notary Appointment at Glen Helen

  1. Contact us with the inmate's information

    Call or text with the inmate's full legal name, booking number (if known), and a description of the documents that need to be notarized. Let us know your deadline so we can prioritize scheduling.

  2. We coordinate with the facility

    San Bernardino County facilities require visits to be scheduled at least one day in advance. We confirm current procedures, attorney/professional visiting availability, and ensure the inmate is still housed at Glen Helen and not temporarily transferred.

  3. Prepare your documents

    Have all documents ready and complete before the appointment — we cannot draft legal documents, only notarize signatures. If the inmate needs to review documents first, coordinate with their attorney or use the facility's mail system.

  4. The notary appointment

    We travel from Los Angeles to Glen Helen in Devore, clear security screening, and meet with the inmate in the designated visiting area. The inmate confirms their identity, signs the documents in our presence, and we complete the notarization.

  5. Document delivery

    After the appointment, we return the signed and notarized documents to you, your attorney, escrow company, or other designated recipient — by hand delivery, courier, or mail depending on your timeline.

Tip: Glen Helen houses both male and female inmates in separate facilities on the same campus. Make sure you know whether the inmate is in the Male or Female facility so we can coordinate with the correct unit.

Glen Helen Notary FAQs

Where is Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center?

Glen Helen is located at 18000 Institution Rd, Devore, CA 92407, approximately 12 miles northwest of downtown San Bernardino. It is about 60 to 80 minutes from Los Angeles via I-15.

How do I find out if someone is at Glen Helen?

You can search the San Bernardino County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call (909) 708-8371 with the inmate's name and date of birth.

What are the visiting hours at Glen Helen?

Public visiting hours are Wednesday through Saturday, 8:30 AM to 7:00 PM, by appointment only. Visits must be scheduled at least one day in advance by calling (909) 887-0364. Attorneys may visit any day of the week with advance notice.

Does Glen Helen house women?

Yes. Glen Helen has separate Male and Female facilities on the same campus. The Female Facility has a capacity of 326 inmates and houses both pre-trial and sentenced women.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

How long does a notary appointment at Glen Helen take?

Plan for 3 to 4 hours total including travel from Los Angeles to Devore, security screening, wait time, and the signing. Glen Helen's remote location adds travel time compared to closer facilities.
