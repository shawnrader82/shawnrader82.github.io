Timing & requirements

What affects translation timing and requirements

Timing factors

  • Page count and document complexity.
  • Language pair and translator availability.
  • Whether rush service is available for that language combination.
  • Whether notarization or apostille coordination is also needed.

Requirement factors

  • Receiving agency or destination country rules.
  • Whether certified translation alone is enough.
  • Whether notarization is also required.
  • Whether apostille or legalization must follow after translation.

Before you start

A short checklist that helps most translation orders move smoothly

  • Clear scans or PDFs of the original documents.
  • Confirmation of the language pair.
  • Name of the receiving agency, consulate, school, or authority.
  • Your deadline or appointment date if timing is tight.
  • Any specific instructions or formatting requirements from the receiving authority.
  • Confirmation of whether notarization or apostille will also be needed.
  • Preferred delivery method if physical return is required.

FAQ

Translation FAQ

What is the difference between certified and notarized translation?

Certified translation includes a signed translator’s declaration of accuracy and is what most agencies require. Notarized translation adds a notary’s seal and signature to that declaration, which some destination countries or agencies also require.

How is pricing determined?

Translation is typically priced per page or per word based on the language pair, document type, and complexity. Your documents are reviewed first, then a clear written estimate is provided before any work begins.

What languages are available?

Certified translation is available for many common language pairs used for immigration, academic, legal, and business purposes. Call or text with your language combination and destination to confirm availability and turnaround.

Can translation be combined with apostille or notary services?

Yes. Many documents need both translation and apostille or notarization. These steps can be coordinated together so your final documents are translated, certified, and prepared for where they will be used.

Do I need to mail my original documents?

For most projects, clear scans or PDFs are enough to start the translation. If the receiving agency requires a notarized or apostilled translation, original documents or certified copies may be needed for the notarization or apostille step.

How long does translation take?

Standard turnaround is typically 2 to 5 business days for common language pairs and shorter documents. Rush service may be available depending on the language combination and page count. If notarization or apostille coordination is also needed, those steps add additional time after the translation is completed.
