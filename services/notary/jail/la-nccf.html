About NCCF Castaic

Jail notary service at North County Correctional Facility (NCCF).

Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — NCCF Castaic

Frequently asked questions.

How do I confirm someone is at North County Correctional Facility?

You can search the LA County Sheriff's Inmate Information Center online at app5.lasd.org/iic using the inmate's name and date of birth. The system will list the current facility, including North County if that is where they are housed.

Can you visit both North County and another Pitchess unit on the same day?

In some situations, yes. If you have documents for inmates at both North County and another Pitchess unit, let us know. We can discuss whether it is practical to coordinate both visits on the same trip, subject to facility schedules and approval.

Are visiting rules different at North County?

North County is a maximum-security facility with its own visiting rules and procedures for professional visits. We confirm current requirements with the facility to ensure your appointment follows their guidelines.

How far in advance should I schedule an NCCF notary appointment?

Because of security and travel time, we recommend contacting us at least a few days in advance when possible. For urgent matters, call or text as soon as you know you need the appointment and we will review available options.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at NCCF?

NCCF maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification purposes. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

What if the inmate is transferred to another facility?

Inmates can be transferred between Castaic and downtown facilities. We recommend confirming the inmate's location close to the appointment time. If they have been moved, we can usually reschedule for the new facility with an updated quote if travel distance changes.
