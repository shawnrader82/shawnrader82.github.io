Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — West Valley Rancho Cucamonga

Frequently asked questions.

Where is West Valley Detention Center located?

West Valley Detention Center (WVDC) is located at 9500 Etiwanda Ave, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91739, off the I-15 freeway, approximately 50 to 70 minutes from Los Angeles depending on traffic.

How large is West Valley Detention Center?

WVDC is one of the largest county jails in California with a bed capacity of 3,347, processing between 50,000 and 60,000 bookings and releases each year and housing primarily pre-trial inmates.

How do I confirm someone is at West Valley Detention Center?

You can search the San Bernardino County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call (909) 708-8371 with the inmate's name and date of birth to confirm they are housed at WVDC.

What are the visiting hours at West Valley Detention Center?

Public visiting hours at WVDC are Wednesday through Saturday, 8:30 AM to 7:00 PM, by appointment only. Visits must be scheduled at least one day in advance by calling (909) 350-2476 or using the online scheduling system. Attorneys may visit any day of the week.

How is the inmate's identity verified for notarization at WVDC?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. Identification options are confirmed with the facility before the appointment.

How long should I plan for a West Valley Detention Center notary appointment?

You should plan for 2 to 3 hours total including travel from Los Angeles to Rancho Cucamonga, security screening, wait time, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but travel and jail procedures add time.
