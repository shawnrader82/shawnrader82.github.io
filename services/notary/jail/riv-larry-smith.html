Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Larry D. Smith Banning

Frequently asked questions.

Where is the Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility located?

The Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility is located at 1627 S Hargrave Street, Banning, CA 92220, near the I-10 freeway in the San Gorgonio Pass area, approximately 1.5 hours from Los Angeles.

How do I confirm someone is at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility?

You can search the Riverside County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call the facility at (951) 922-7300 with the inmate's name and date of birth to confirm they are housed at Smith Correctional.

How are visits scheduled at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility?

You can call (951) 922-7540 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM daily to schedule visits, and visits must be scheduled at least one day in advance. For attorney or professional visits, the facility is contacted at (951) 922-7300 to coordinate directly.

What are the check-in rules for visiting Smith Correctional?

Visitor check-in begins 60 minutes before the scheduled visit and closes 20 minutes before the start time. Visitors who arrive during the final 20 minutes before the visit will not be allowed to check in, so arriving early is essential.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at Smith Correctional?

The facility maintains booking records that can be used for identification. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. ID options are confirmed with the facility before the appointment.

How long should I plan for a Larry D. Smith Correctional notary appointment?

Plan for 4 to 5 hours total including travel from Los Angeles (about 1.5 hours each way), security screening, wait time, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but travel and facility procedures add significant time.
