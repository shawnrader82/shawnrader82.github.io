About Men's Central Jail

Jail notary service at Men's Central Jail (MCJ).

Men’s Central Jail (MCJ) is the largest jail facility in Los Angeles County and one of the largest in the United States. Located in downtown Los Angeles near Union Station on Bauchet Street, MCJ houses thousands of male inmates awaiting trial or serving shorter sentences. It shares the complex with the Inmate Reception Center (IRC) — the intake facility where every newly arrested person in LA County is processed, medically screened, and held for up to 96 hours before being assigned to a permanent housing facility (MCJ, Twin Towers, Pitchess/Wayside, or NCCF). Families and attorneys call us when time-sensitive documents cannot wait until release — we coordinate with MCJ and IRC security procedures to complete notarizations efficiently.

Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Men's Central Jail

Frequently asked questions.

How do I find someone's booking number at Men's Central Jail?

You can search the LA County Sheriff's Inmate Information Center online at app5.lasd.org/iic using the inmate's name and date of birth. The booking number helps confirm their location and coordinate the visit.

Is the Inmate Reception Center (IRC) the same as Men's Central Jail?

The Inmate Reception Center (IRC) is co-located with Men’s Central Jail on the same downtown LA campus at Bauchet Street, but it is a separate function. IRC is the intake facility — every person newly arrested in LA County is booked, medically screened, and held at IRC for up to 96 hours before being classified and transferred to a permanent housing facility (MCJ, Twin Towers, Pitchess/Wayside, NCCF, or CRDF Lynwood for women). If your loved one was just arrested and has not yet had a housing assignment, they are almost certainly at IRC. We coordinate notary visits at both facilities.

Can you notarize documents for someone still at IRC before they are housed?

Yes — but timing is tricky. IRC holds are usually 24 to 96 hours and inmates can be moved without warning to their assigned housing facility. If a document is truly urgent (a real estate closing that day, an emergency Power of Attorney), we can attempt IRC service. For most situations we recommend waiting until the inmate is classified and housed at MCJ, Twin Towers, or Pitchess so we have a stable location. Call (213) 933‑2507 and we’ll help you decide.

Where do people get transferred to after Men's Central Jail?

After processing at IRC and classification, LA County male inmates typically go to Men’s Central Jail (MCJ) for shorter-term or higher-security housing, Twin Towers Correctional Facility for mental health or medical needs, or the Pitchess Detention Center complex in Castaic (which includes NCCF, North, South, and East facilities — many longtime LA families still call this “Wayside”). Women go to Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF) in Lynwood. We serve all these facilities.

What if the inmate was just transferred to MCJ?

After a transfer, it can take 24–48 hours for an inmate to be fully processed and available for professional visits. We recommend confirming their status before scheduling if there has been a recent transfer.

Can family members bring documents to the jail notary appointment?

No — professional notary visits are separate from regular family visitation. You will need to get documents to us before the appointment, and we will bring them into the facility.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at MCJ?

MCJ maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification purposes. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used.

How long does a Men's Central Jail notary appointment take?

Plan for 1–2 hours total including travel, security screening, wait time, and the actual signing. The notarization itself is quick, but jail procedures add time.

What if the inmate refuses to sign the documents?

A notary cannot force anyone to sign a document. If the inmate refuses or is unable to sign, the notarization cannot be completed. You may still be responsible for a trip fee in this situation.
