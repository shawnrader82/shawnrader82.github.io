Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — High Desert Adelanto

Frequently asked questions.

Where is High Desert Detention Center?

High Desert Detention Center is located at 9438 Commerce Way, Adelanto, CA 92301, in the High Desert region of San Bernardino County.

How do I find out if someone is at High Desert Detention Center?

You can search the San Bernardino County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call the facility with the person's name and date of birth for inmate information.

Do you notarize documents at other San Bernardino County jails?

Yes. In addition to High Desert Detention Center, we visit West Valley Detention Center, Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center, and Central Detention Center for mobile notary appointments.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at High Desert Detention Center?

The facility maintains booking and inmate records, including photos and facility-issued inmate ID, which can often be used for identification under California notary law. If a valid California ID, driver's license, passport, or other acceptable ID is on file, we may be able to rely on that as well. Identification options are confirmed with the facility before the appointment.

How long does a notary appointment at High Desert Detention Center take?

Plan for 2 to 4 hours total including travel to Adelanto, security screening, wait time, and the signing itself. The notarization is quick, but the remote location and jail procedures add time.

What if the inmate was just booked or is being transferred?

Newly booked inmates may not be available for professional visits until processing is complete. If the inmate is being transferred between San Bernardino County facilities, it is best to confirm their location and housing unit close to the appointment time.
