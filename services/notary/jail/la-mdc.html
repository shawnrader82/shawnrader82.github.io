About MDC Los Angeles

Jail notary service at Metropolitan Detention Center Los Angeles (MDC).

Metropolitan Detention Center Los Angeles (MDC) is a federal detention facility operated by the Bureau of Prisons, located in downtown Los Angeles near Union Station. Unlike county jails, MDC houses federal inmates and requires advance coordination for professional visits — including attorney sponsorship and BOP approval. Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — MDC Los Angeles

Frequently asked questions.

Is MDC Los Angeles the same as Twin Towers or Men\u2019s Central Jail?

No. Metropolitan Detention Center Los Angeles is a federal Bureau of Prisons facility that holds federal inmates, while Twin Towers and Men\u2019s Central Jail are Los Angeles County jails.

Is the process the same as visiting a county jail?

No. MDC Los Angeles is a federal Bureau of Prisons facility with a more involved approval process for professional visitors. Federal visits require advance paperwork, attorney involvement, and approval that can take several business days.

Does the inmate\u2019s attorney need to be involved?

In most cases, yes. Federal facilities typically require notary visits to be coordinated through or sponsored by the inmate\u2019s attorney of record.

How far in advance should I schedule an MDC notary appointment?

We recommend contacting us at least one week before your deadline so we can complete Bureau of Prisons approval steps and schedule your appointment without rushing.

How do I confirm someone is at MDC Los Angeles?

You can search the Federal Bureau of Prisons inmate locator at bop.gov/inmateloc using the inmate\u2019s name or register number, or contact MDC Los Angeles directly at (213) 485-0439.

Can you work with out-of-state attorneys or consulates?

Yes. We frequently coordinate MDC notary appointments with federal defense attorneys from other states and with consular officials to meet court and agency requirements.
