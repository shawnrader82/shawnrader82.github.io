About Theo Lacy — ‘The Branch’ / OC Women’s Housing

Jail notary service at Theo Lacy Facility (‘The Branch’), City of Orange.

Theo Lacy Facility — known as ‘The Branch’ to Orange County families and defense attorneys — is the largest jail operated by the Orange County Sheriff’s Department, located at 501 The City Drive South in the City of Orange, adjacent to the Honda Center and Angel Stadium. With a rated capacity of approximately 3,442, Theo Lacy holds sentenced and pre-sentenced male inmates alongside female inmates in designated modules. A December 2020 OCSD realignment moved a large portion of the female population from the Central Women’s Jail (CWJ) inside the Central Jail Complex to Theo Lacy, and it has remained a major female-housing site since. Women in OC custody may be at Theo Lacy, CWJ, or the James A. Musick Facility depending on classification — if you’re not sure where a family member is held, OC Inmate Records at (714) 647-4666 can confirm. Families and attorneys call us when notarized signatures are needed on real estate, family law, immigration, and probate documents.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Theo Lacy Orange

Frequently asked questions.

Is Theo Lacy the same jail as ‘The Branch’?

Yes. Theo Lacy Facility in the City of Orange is called ‘The Branch’ by many Orange County families, attorneys, and bail bondsmen — a reference to its position as the primary branch jail complementing the Central Jail Complex (CJX) in Santa Ana. Both nicknames refer to the same facility at 501 The City Drive South.

Is Theo Lacy where Orange County women are held?

Yes. Theo Lacy Facility houses female inmates in designated modules alongside its male population. Women in OC custody are distributed across three facilities depending on classification: the Central Women’s Jail (CWJ) inside the Central Jail Complex in Santa Ana, Theo Lacy in Orange, and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. A December 2020 OCSD realignment moved a large portion of the CWJ population to Theo Lacy, which has remained a major female-housing site since. If you aren’t sure which facility a woman is being held at, call OC Inmate Records at (714) 647-4666 with her full legal name and date of birth — they can confirm before we schedule.

Where is the Theo Lacy Facility located?

Theo Lacy Facility is at 501 The City Drive South, Orange, CA 92868, in the City of Orange between the Santa Ana Freeway (I-5) and the Garden Grove Freeway (SR-22), near the Honda Center and Angel Stadium. Public parking is available at the North parking structure across from the Lamoreaux Justice Center.

What type of inmates are housed at Theo Lacy?

Theo Lacy is a medium-security jail with a rated capacity of approximately 3,442 — the largest of Orange County’s three jails. It houses sentenced and pre-sentenced male inmates and, since a December 2020 OCSD realignment, has been a primary housing site for female inmates as well. Housing modules range from minimum through medium-security custody levels. Female inmates may also be housed at the Central Women’s Jail or portions of James A. Musick depending on classification.

How do I confirm someone is at Theo Lacy Facility?

Use the Orange County Sheriff’s Department ‘Who’s In Jail’ inmate locator online, call the OC Inmate Information Center at (714) 584-6790, or call Theo Lacy directly at (714) 935-6905. Have the inmate’s full legal name and date of birth ready. New arrestees are first processed at IRC inside the Central Jail Complex, then classified and transferred to Theo Lacy, Musick, or held at CJX.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at Theo Lacy?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. Acceptable government-issued IDs include a California driver’s license, California ID card, U.S. passport, alien registration card, or military ID, when available in the inmate’s records. If the inmate’s wallet ID was booked into property at IRC during intake, ask the family to request it be brought over for the visit.

Are professional visits different from regular visiting hours at Theo Lacy?

Yes. Professional visitors such as attorneys and notaries have broader access than public visitors, who are limited to specific days and time blocks. We confirm the current professional visiting schedule with the facility before each appointment. Because Theo Lacy houses a female population in designated modules, the women’s housing area has its own professional visiting protocols — we handle that coordination.

How long does a notary appointment at Theo Lacy take?

Plan for 1 to 3 hours total including travel from Los Angeles, security screening, wait time, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but Theo Lacy is a large campus and pulling an inmate from a specific housing module can take longer than at smaller facilities.
