Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Theo Lacy Orange

Frequently asked questions.

Where is the Theo Lacy Facility located?

The Theo Lacy Facility is located at 501 The City Drive South, Orange, CA 92868, between the Santa Ana Freeway (I-5) and the Garden Grove Freeway (SR-22). Parking is available at the North parking structure across from the Lamoreaux Justice Center.

What type of inmates are housed at Theo Lacy?

Theo Lacy is a maximum-security facility with a capacity of approximately 3,100 to 3,400 inmates. It houses both sentenced and pre-sentenced male inmates arrested for misdemeanors and felonies.

How do I confirm someone is at the Theo Lacy Facility?

You can search the Orange County Sheriff's Department inmate locator online or call the facility at (714) 935-6940 with the inmate's name and date of birth.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at Theo Lacy?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

Are professional visits different from regular visiting hours at Theo Lacy?

Yes. Professional visitors such as attorneys and notaries typically have broader access than public visitors, who are limited to Friday through Monday. We confirm the current professional visiting schedule with the facility before each appointment.

How long does a notary appointment at Theo Lacy take?

Plan for 1 to 3 hours total including travel from Los Angeles, security screening, wait time, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but facility procedures and travel from LA add time.
