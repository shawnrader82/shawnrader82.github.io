About Musick Facility Irvine

Jail notary service at James A. Musick Facility (‘The Farm’ / Honor Farm).

James A. Musick Facility — known throughout Orange County as ‘The Farm’ or the Honor Farm — is the minimum-security jail campus operated by the Orange County Sheriff’s Department at 13502 Musick Road in Irvine. The nickname dates to 1963, when the site opened as the ‘County Industrial Farm’ and inmates worked agricultural jobs on the land. The farming operations ended long ago, but bail bondsmen, defense attorneys, and OC families still refer to it as the Honor Farm. It holds low-risk male inmates: non-violent misdemeanor convictions, work-release participants, and inmates finishing longer sentences. Since December 2020, all OC female inmates are housed at Theo Lacy rather than Musick.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Musick Facility Irvine

Frequently asked questions.

Is the James A. Musick Facility the same as ‘The Farm’ or the Honor Farm?

Yes. James A. Musick Facility is widely known as ‘The Farm’ or the Honor Farm. When it opened in 1963 it was called the County Industrial Farm and inmates worked agricultural jobs on the land; the nicknames stuck even though the working farm operations ended decades ago. Attorneys, bail bondsmen, and Orange County residents still call it the Honor Farm today.

Where is the James A. Musick Facility located?

13502 Musick Road, Irvine, CA 92618, in south Orange County near the junction of the I-5 and I-405 freeways. The address sits on land that was historically unincorporated Orange County; it is now within Irvine city limits.

What type of inmates are housed at Musick?

Musick is a minimum-security facility that primarily houses low-risk male inmates: those convicted of non-violent misdemeanors, work-release participants, and inmates in the final stretch of longer sentences. Since December 2020, all Orange County female inmates have been housed at Theo Lacy Facility rather than Musick or the Central Jail Complex.

How do I confirm someone is at the Musick Facility?

Use the Orange County Sheriff’s Department ‘Who’s In Jail’ inmate locator online, or call the OC Inmate Information Center at (714) 584-6790 with the inmate’s full legal name and date of birth. Note that inmates classified for maximum or medium security will not be at Musick — they will be at the Central Jail Complex or Theo Lacy Facility.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at Musick?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. Acceptable government-issued IDs include a California driver’s license, California ID card, U.S. passport, alien registration card, or military ID, when available in the inmate’s records. If the inmate’s wallet ID was booked into property at CJX during intake, ask the family to request it be brought over.

Is the visiting environment at the Honor Farm different from CJX or Theo Lacy?

Yes. Musick is a minimum-security facility, so the visiting environment is generally more relaxed than at the Central Jail Complex (CJX) or medium-security portions of Theo Lacy. Professional visits typically happen at tables rather than behind glass partitions, and inmates are usually already in dayroom clothing rather than being pulled from housing.

How long should I plan for a Musick notary appointment?

Plan for 2 to 3 hours total including travel from Los Angeles to south Irvine, check-in, wait time, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but Musick is one of the farthest facilities we drive to from Granada Hills — travel alone adds significant time each way.
