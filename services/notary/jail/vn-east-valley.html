About East Valley Jail Thousand Oaks

Jail notary service at East Valley Jail (Ventura County).

East Valley Jail — officially the East County Jail — is a small Ventura County Sheriff's Office booking facility built in 1989 and attached to the East Valley Sheriff Station in Thousand Oaks. It is a Type I facility with only 31 BSCC-rated beds. Along with the Main Jail, it accepts arrestees from every law enforcement agency in Ventura County, but it is not designed for long-term housing — most people booked here are transferred to the Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura within hours. As the closest Ventura County jail to Los Angeles County, we are well positioned from Granada Hills to reach this facility quickly.

Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

Mobile Notary FAQ — East Valley Jail Thousand Oaks

Frequently asked questions.

Where is East Valley Jail located?

East Valley Jail is located at 2101 E Olsen Rd, Thousand Oaks, CA 91360. It is approximately 45 minutes from Los Angeles via US-101.

How do I confirm someone is housed at East Valley Jail?

Use the Ventura County Sheriff's online inmate search or call (805) 654-3335 for the 24/7 inmate information line. Because East Valley (officially the East County Jail) is a Type I booking facility with only 31 rated beds, most people booked here are transferred to the Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura within hours. For custody questions specifically at East Valley, call (805) 494-8242.

What are the visiting days and policies at East Valley Jail?

Visiting schedules at East Valley Jail are set by the Ventura County Sheriff's Office and may differ from other county facilities. Contact the facility or call us to confirm current visiting days, visit length, and visitor limits.

What can I bring into East Valley Jail during a visit?

Only your personal key ring, photo ID, and one baby bottle or blanket if bringing an infant. No cell phones, wallets, purses, backpacks, food, beverages, cameras, or reading materials are allowed. The facility will not hold items and there are no storage lockers.

How is East Valley Jail different from Ventura County Main Jail or Todd Road Jail?

East Valley Jail — officially the East County Jail — is a small Type I booking facility with 31 BSCC rated beds. Along with the Main Jail, it accepts arrestees from every law enforcement agency in Ventura County. It is NOT a long-term housing facility — most people booked here are transferred to the Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura within hours. The Main Jail is a Type II facility housing pretrial and sentenced inmates. Todd Road Jail (TRJ) in Santa Paula is a Type II/III facility housing sentenced inmates and AB 109 State returns.

How long should I plan for an East Valley Jail notary appointment?

Plan for 2 to 3 hours total including travel from Los Angeles (approximately 45 minutes each way via US-101), security screening, wait time, and the signing.
