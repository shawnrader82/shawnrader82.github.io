About OC Central Jail

Jail notary service at Orange County Central Jail Complex.

The Orange County Central Jail Complex (CJX) at 550 North Flower Street in Santa Ana is the main detention facility for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD). CJX is not one building but a set of interconnected facilities sharing corridors and tunnels: the Intake Release Center (IRC) — where every person arrested in Orange County is booked, medically screened, and initially classified — the Central Men's Jail (CMJ), a maximum-security housing facility that opened in 1968, and the Transportation Bureau. If your loved one was just arrested in Orange County, they are almost certainly at IRC on their way to a housing assignment at CMJ, Theo Lacy, or James A. Musick (“The Farm”). Note: women in OC custody are housed at Theo Lacy since December 2020, when the Central Women's Jail (CWJ) closed. Families and attorneys call us when a signature cannot wait for release.

Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — OC Central Jail

Frequently asked questions.

Is the IRC the same as the Central Men's Jail in Orange County?

They are part of the same complex but serve different functions. The Intake Release Center (IRC) is where every person arrested in Orange County is booked, medically screened, and classified. The Central Men's Jail (CMJ) is one of the housing facilities where inmates are held after classification. Both are inside the Central Jail Complex (CJX) at 550 North Flower Street in Santa Ana, connected by internal corridors. If someone was arrested within the last few days and has not been transferred, they are likely at IRC. If they have been in custody longer, check whether they are at CMJ, Theo Lacy, or Musick.

How do I find someone's booking number in Orange County?

Use the Orange County Sheriff's Department online Inmate Information locator, or call the OC Inmate Information Center at (714) 584-6790. Booking numbers help us confirm which facility (IRC, CMJ, Theo Lacy, or Musick) the inmate is currently housed at so we do not arrive at the wrong location.

Are women housed at the OC Central Jail?

No, not anymore. The Central Women's Jail (CWJ) at 550 North Flower Street closed in December 2020, and all female inmates in Orange County are now housed at the Theo Lacy Facility in the City of Orange. The only exception is a small mental-health unit for women (Module K) that remains inside IRC. If a woman was recently arrested in OC, she is booked at IRC and then transferred to Theo Lacy for housing.

Where is the OC Central Jail Complex located?

The Central Jail Complex is at 550 North Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703, in the Santa Ana civic center area. Parking is available in the structure across from the Santa Ana Police Department. Travel from Granada Hills is typically 60 to 90 minutes depending on traffic.

How long does a Central Jail notary appointment take?

Plan for 2 to 3 hours total for a professional visit at CJX including travel from the LA area, check-in, security screening, and the signing. The notarization itself is quick, but Central Jail is a maximum-security facility and screening takes time.

Can I schedule notary visits at both the OC Central Jail and Theo Lacy in one trip?

Usually not efficiently — the Central Jail Complex is in Santa Ana and Theo Lacy is in the City of Orange, about 15 minutes apart, but each requires separate check-in, security screening, and professional visit procedures. Two appointments typically means two trip fees. We can advise on cost trade-offs when you call.
