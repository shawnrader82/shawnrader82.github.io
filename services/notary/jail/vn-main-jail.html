About Ventura County Main Jail

Jail notary service at Ventura County Main Jail.

Ventura County Main Jail — officially the Pre-Trial Detention Facility, or PTDF — opened in 1980 and is the primary detention facility for the Ventura County Sheriff's Office, located in Ventura near the county courthouse. It is a Type II facility housing both pretrial detainees and sentenced inmates from across Ventura County. Families and attorneys call us when time-sensitive documents need notarized signatures completed without waiting for release.

Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Ventura County Main Jail

Frequently asked questions.

Where is Ventura County Main Jail located?

The Ventura County Main Jail, also known as the Pre-Trial Detention Facility, is located at 800 S Victoria Ave, Ventura, CA 93009. It is approximately 65 minutes from Los Angeles via US-101.

How do I confirm someone is housed at Ventura County Main Jail?

Use the Ventura County Sheriff's online inmate search or call (805) 654-3335 for the 24/7 inmate information line. The Pre-Trial Detention Facility (Main Jail) uses (805) 654-3335; the Sheriff’s Office at 800 South Victoria Avenue is (805) 654-2380. Be sure to confirm whether the inmate is at the Main Jail (PTDF) in Ventura or at Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula.

What are the visiting days and sessions at Ventura County Main Jail?

Visiting days are Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. Sessions are typically morning (8:30 AM – 11:30 AM), afternoon (1:30 PM – 4:30 PM), and evening (7:00 PM – 9:00 PM), assigned by housing unit. Check-in starts 1 hour before each session and closes 30 minutes before the session ends.

What can I bring into Ventura County Main Jail during a visit?

Only your personal key ring, photo ID, and one baby bottle or blanket if bringing an infant. No cell phones, wallets, purses, backpacks, food, beverages, cameras, or reading materials are allowed. The facility will not hold items and there are no storage lockers.

What is the difference between Ventura County Main Jail and Todd Road Jail?

The Main Jail (Pre-Trial Detention Facility) in Ventura primarily houses pre-trial detainees and handles initial booking. Todd Road Jail in Santa Paula primarily houses sentenced inmates and those in longer-term custody. They are separate facilities with different addresses and visiting schedules.

How long should I plan for a Ventura County Main Jail notary appointment?

Plan for 3 to 4 hours total including travel from Los Angeles, security screening, wait time, and the signing. The drive from Los Angeles to Ventura County Main Jail is approximately 65 minutes each way via US-101.
