Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Santa Barbara Main Jail

Frequently asked questions.

Where is the Santa Barbara County Main Jail located?

The Santa Barbara County Main Jail is located at 4436 Calle Real, Santa Barbara, CA 93110. It is the county's primary jail and houses male and female inmates awaiting trial, sentencing, or serving county jail sentences.

Do mobile notaries work at the Santa Barbara Main Jail?

Yes. Independent mobile notaries regularly provide services at the Santa Barbara County Main Jail. The notary checks in with custody staff, is escorted to a non-contact visiting area, and completes the notarization with the inmate.

How is the inmate's identity verified for notarization at the Main Jail?

Custody staff maintain booking records with photos and identifying information that can be used to confirm the inmate's identity. If the inmate has a valid government-issued ID on file, that can also be used. ID options are confirmed with the facility before the appointment.

How long should I plan for a Santa Barbara Main Jail notary appointment?

Plan for 3 to 5 hours total, including travel from Los Angeles, check-in with custody staff, escort to and from the visiting area, wait time, and the signing itself. The notarization portion is quick, but facility procedures and distance add time.

Is the Northern Branch Jail the same as the Main Jail?

No. The Main Jail on Calle Real in Santa Barbara and the Northern Branch Jail on Black Road in Santa Maria are separate facilities about 65 miles apart. Both are operated by the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office, so it is important to confirm which facility the inmate is housed at before scheduling.

How do I confirm someone is at the Santa Barbara County Main Jail?

You can search the Santa Barbara County Sheriff's Office inmate locator online or call the Main Jail at (805) 681-4260 with the inmate's name and date of birth.
