About Pitchess Castaic

Jail notary service at Pitchess Detention Center.

Pitchess Detention Center — officially the Peter J. Pitchess Detention Center, and still known to many longtime LA residents as Wayside Jail — is a 2,620-acre Los Angeles County Sheriff’s detention complex in Castaic, roughly 40 miles north of downtown Los Angeles. The complex houses four separate jails: the North County Correctional Facility (NCCF), the North Facility, the South Facility, and the East Facility. Combined, Pitchess is one of the largest county jail campuses in the United States. We serve all four units when an inmate needs documents notarized without waiting for release.

A quick note on names: the complex opened in 1938 as the Wayside Honor Rancho and was renamed the Peter J. Pitchess Detention Center in 1995 after former LA County Sheriff Peter J. Pitchess. Officially it’s Pitchess — but generations of LA families still call it Wayside, and even the Sheriff’s Department acknowledges the old name in its public information. Whichever name you were told, you’re in the right place.

Which Pitchess/Wayside facility?

The four jails inside the Pitchess (Wayside) complex.

If you were told an inmate is at “Pitchess” or “Wayside,” that could mean any one of four separate jails on the Castaic campus. Each has its own housing, security level, and professional-visit procedure. Here’s how to tell them apart:

North County Correctional Facility (NCCF)

The largest of the four, with roughly 4,000 beds. Medium-security general population. This is where most Pitchess/Wayside notary appointments end up. Has its own dedicated page: NCCF Notary →

North Facility

Minimum-security dorm housing. Inmates here are typically closer to release or serving shorter sentences. Professional visits generally scheduled through the same intake process as the rest of the complex.

South Facility

Minimum-security dorm housing, similar profile to North Facility. Often used for inmate workers and lower-classification housing.

East Facility

Formerly known as “Pitchess East,” this is a medium-security jail with dedicated housing units and protective-custody assignments. Visiting procedures can differ from the North and South facilities.

Not sure which facility? Use the LA County Sheriff’s Inmate Information Center to look up the current housing assignment by name and date of birth — or just call us at (213) 933‑2507 and we’ll help figure it out.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Pitchess Castaic

Frequently asked questions.

Is Wayside Jail the same as Pitchess Detention Center?

Yes. The Castaic complex opened in 1938 as the Wayside Honor Rancho and was renamed the Peter J. Pitchess Detention Center in 1995 after former LA County Sheriff Peter J. Pitchess. The official name today is Pitchess Detention Center, but many longtime LA residents, bail bondsmen, and defense attorneys still call it Wayside or Wayside Jail. Both names refer to the same 2,620-acre LASD complex in Castaic that houses NCCF, the North Facility, the South Facility, and the East Facility.

What is the difference between NCCF and Pitchess?

NCCF stands for North County Correctional Facility. It is one of four separate jails inside the Pitchess Detention Center complex in Castaic — the other three are the North Facility, the South Facility, and the East Facility. NCCF is the largest of the four, with roughly 4,000 beds, and holds most of the medium-security general population. When someone says an inmate is “at Pitchess,” they could be at any of the four facilities including NCCF.

How do I know if someone is at Pitchess or North County (NCCF)?

You can search the LA County Sheriff’s Inmate Information Center using the inmate’s name and date of birth. The system will list the current facility, which may be one of the Pitchess units (North Facility, South Facility, East Facility) or North County Correctional Facility (NCCF). All four are on the same Castaic campus.

Can you visit more than one Castaic facility on the same trip?

In some cases, yes. If you have documents for inmates housed at different Castaic facilities, let us know. We can discuss whether it is practical to coordinate multiple visits on the same day, subject to facility schedules.

Do Castaic facilities have different visiting rules than downtown LA jails?

Yes. Each facility has its own schedule and rules for professional visits. For example, some units may allow professional visits daily from 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM, while others may limit visits to weekdays or specific hours. We confirm the current procedures for the specific unit before your appointment.

How far in advance should I schedule a Pitchess notary appointment?

Because of the travel time and coordination involved, we recommend contacting us at least a few days in advance when possible. For urgent matters, call or text as soon as you know you need the appointment and we will review available options.

What ID does the inmate need at Pitchess?

Castaic facilities maintain booking records with photos that can be used for identification purposes. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

What if the inmate is moved back to downtown Los Angeles?

Inmates can be transferred between Castaic and downtown LA facilities. We recommend confirming the inmate's location close to the appointment time. If they have been moved, we can usually reschedule for the new facility with an updated quote if travel distance changes.
