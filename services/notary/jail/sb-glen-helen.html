Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Glen Helen Devore

Frequently asked questions.

Where is Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center?

Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center is located at 18000 Institution Rd, Devore, CA 92407, approximately 12 miles northwest of downtown San Bernardino and about 60 to 80 minutes from Los Angeles via I-15.

How do I find out if someone is at Glen Helen?

You can search the San Bernardino County Sheriff's inmate locator online or call (909) 708-8371 with the inmate's name and date of birth to confirm they are housed at Glen Helen.

What are the visiting hours at Glen Helen?

Public visiting hours at Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center are Wednesday through Saturday, 8:30 AM to 7:00 PM, by appointment only. Visits must be scheduled at least one day in advance by calling (909) 887-0364. Attorneys may visit any day of the week with advance notice.

Does Glen Helen house women?

Yes. Glen Helen has separate male and female facilities on the same campus. The female facility has a capacity of approximately 326 inmates and houses both pre-trial and sentenced women.

What ID does the inmate need for notarization at Glen Helen?

The facility maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver's license, or passport on file, that can also be used. Identification options are confirmed with the facility before the appointment.

How long does a notary appointment at Glen Helen take?

Plan for 3 to 4 hours total including travel from Los Angeles to Devore, security screening, wait time, and the signing. Glen Helen's more remote location adds travel time compared to closer facilities.
