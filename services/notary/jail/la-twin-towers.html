Before your appointment

What to have ready for a jail notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID for signers

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID. For inmates, jail-issued ID cards are generally acceptable. Confirm the inmate’s current ID status before scheduling — if their ID was booked into property, it may need to be retrieved first.

✓

Complete documents ready to sign

Bring all pages of the document — not just the signature page. Documents should be fully prepared and ready to sign. Missing pages or incomplete forms will require a return visit. We can advise on preparation before we schedule.

✓

Inmate name & booking number

We need the inmate’s full legal name and booking number to confirm their location within the facility. You can typically find the booking number through the county sheriff’s online inmate search or by calling the facility directly.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Twin Towers

Frequently asked questions.

How is Twin Towers different from Men\u2019s Central Jail?

Twin Towers and Men\u2019s Central Jail are adjacent facilities at the same downtown LA complex, but they have separate entrances, visiting procedures, and housing units. Twin Towers is the nation\u2019s largest mental health facility and also houses the Inmate Reception Center (IRC) where new bookings are processed. We confirm which facility the inmate is actually housed in before scheduling.

How do I find someone\u2019s booking number at Twin Towers?

You can search the LA County Sheriff\u2019s Inmate Information Center online at app5.lasd.org/iic using the inmate\u2019s name and date of birth. The system will show which facility they are currently housed at. You can also call the 24-hour inmate information line at (213) 473-6080.

What if the inmate is in medical or mental health housing at Twin Towers?

Inmates in medical or mental health housing may have restricted visiting access. We coordinate with the facility to determine if a professional notary visit is possible and what additional steps may be required.

Can I schedule for both Twin Towers and Men\u2019s Central in one trip?

If you have documents for inmates at both facilities, let us know. Since they are adjacent, we may be able to coordinate both visits on the same day, though each facility requires separate check-in and security procedures.

What ID does the inmate need at Twin Towers?

Twin Towers maintains booking records with photos that can be used for identification purposes. If the inmate has a valid California ID, driver\u2019s license, or passport on file, that can also be used. We confirm ID options before the appointment.

What if the inmate is transferred before the Twin Towers notary appointment?

Inmates can be transferred between facilities with little notice. We recommend confirming the inmate\u2019s location close to the appointment time. If they have been moved, we can often reschedule for the new facility with an updated quote if travel distance changes.
