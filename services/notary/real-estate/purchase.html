What we notarize

What's typically in a home purchase package.

Purchase packages run 100 to 175+ pages, with 8 to 14 documents requiring notarization. We walk through every page, flag what needs your signature, and stamp the documents that need a notary acknowledgment.

  • Deed of Trust The lien securing your loan against the property — must be notarized and recorded.
  • Grant Deed Transfers ownership from seller to you. Notarized and recorded.
  • Promissory Note Your promise to repay the loan. Signed, not notarized.
  • Closing Disclosure Final loan terms, fees, and cash to close. Signed acknowledgment only.
  • Signature/Name Affidavit Confirms variations of your legal name. Notarized.
  • Occupancy Affidavit Confirms whether the property is primary, second home, or investment. Notarized.
  • Preliminary Change of Ownership Report (PCOR) California state form recorded with the deed. Signed.
  • Errors & Omissions Agreement Authorizes lender to fix clerical errors after closing. Signed.
  • 1st Payment Letter Tells you when, where, and how much to pay. Signed acknowledgment.
  • Second Home Rider / Non-Owner-Occupied Rider Loan-program-specific addendum (if applicable). Signed.

Your exact package depends on lender, loan type, occupancy, and California-specific addenda. We adapt to whatever your escrow or loan officer sends.

Purchase types

Which purchase are you signing?

The signing process is similar across purchase types, but package size, occupancy disclosures, and the riders to expect differ. Here is what to know for each.

First-time buyer

Your first purchase — we take extra time to walk through every disclosure, the deed of trust, and the rights you're signing into.

  • Typical package size: 120–175+ pages
  • Signing time: 60–75 minutes
  • No right of rescission on a purchase
  • Bring cashier's check or confirm wire before signing

Investor / non-owner-occupied

Rental or flip property. Loan terms differ from a primary residence, and the package includes a Non-Owner-Occupied Occupancy Affidavit.

  • Typical package size: 110–160 pages
  • Signing time: 45–60 minutes
  • Non-Owner-Occupied Rider notarized
  • Larger down payment and reserves usually required

Second home / vacation property

A property you'll use yourself but not as a primary residence. The package includes a Second Home Rider with occupancy restrictions.

  • Typical package size: 110–160 pages
  • Signing time: 45–60 minutes
  • Second Home Rider signed (occupancy restrictions apply)
  • Rate usually between primary and investor

Purchase FAQ

Common questions about purchase signings.

How long does a purchase signing take?

A typical purchase signing runs 45 to 75 minutes. Packages are usually 100 to 175+ pages, with 8 to 14 documents requiring notarization. First-time buyer signings can run a bit longer because we walk through every disclosure.

What do I need to bring to my purchase signing?

A current, unexpired government-issued photo ID for each borrower on title. If you owe a cashier's check or wire confirmation at signing, bring that too. Your escrow officer will email you a wiring instructions sheet — confirm wire details by phone with your escrow officer before sending, not by email alone.

Is there a 3-day right of rescission on a purchase?

No. The 3-day right of rescission only applies to refinances on a primary residence. On a purchase, funds typically disburse the same day or next business day after the package is funded and recorded.

Do both spouses need to be at the signing?

If both spouses are on title, both must be present. If only one spouse is on title, the non-titled spouse may still need to sign certain documents (often a Quitclaim or Interspousal Transfer). Your lender or escrow officer will confirm in advance.

Can you sign at the escrow office, my new home, or somewhere else?

Yes — your current home, the escrow office, your office, a quiet coffee shop, or the new property if it has power and a flat surface. We come to you anywhere in our service area at the time you choose.

What if I'm buying an investment property or second home?

The package includes a Non-Owner-Occupied Occupancy Affidavit (investment) or Second Home Rider (second home). Loan terms, down payment requirements, and disclosures differ from a primary-residence purchase — your loan officer handles the underwriting; we handle the signing.

Can you explain my loan terms, fees, or rate?

No — we are notaries and signing agents, not loan officers, real estate agents, or attorneys. We show you where to sign, initial, and date. Any question about rate, payment, escrow account, fees, or terms goes to your loan officer, real estate agent, or escrow officer.
