What we notarize

What's typically in a refinance package.

Refinance packages run 80 to 150+ pages, with 6 to 12 documents requiring notarization. We walk through every page, flag what needs your signature, and stamp the documents that need a notary acknowledgment.

  • Deed of Trust The lien securing the new loan against your property — must be notarized and recorded.
  • Promissory Note Your promise to repay the loan. Signed, not notarized.
  • Notice of Right to Cancel Required for primary-residence refis — starts the 3-day rescission clock.
  • Closing Disclosure Final loan terms, fees, and cash to close. Signed acknowledgment only.
  • Signature/Name Affidavit Confirms variations of your legal name. Notarized.
  • Occupancy Affidavit Confirms you'll live in the property as your primary residence (if applicable). Notarized.
  • Errors & Omissions Agreement Authorizes lender to fix clerical errors after closing. Signed.
  • 1st Payment Letter Tells you when, where, and how much to pay. Signed acknowledgment.
  • Interspousal / Quitclaim Deed If the spouse is on title but not on the loan. Notarized.

Your exact package depends on lender, loan type, and California-specific addenda. We adapt to whatever your loan officer sends.

Refinance types

Which refinance are you signing?

The signing process is similar across refinance types, but package size, rescission rules, and the documents to expect differ. Here is what to know for each.

Rate-and-term refinance

You are replacing your existing mortgage with a new one to get a lower rate, a shorter term, or both — without pulling cash out. If you are planning to sell the property instead, see our seller signing page.

  • Typical package size: 80–120 pages
  • Signing time: 30–45 minutes
  • 3-day right of rescission applies (primary residence)
  • Spouse-on-title-only usually still signs Interspousal or Quitclaim

Cash-out refinance

You are taking equity out of the property as cash, often for home improvements, debt consolidation, or another property purchase. If you prefer a revolving credit line instead of a lump sum, see our HELOC signing page.

  • Typical package size: 100–150+ pages (extra cash-out disclosures)
  • Signing time: 45–60 minutes
  • 3-day right of rescission applies (primary residence) — funds disburse after rescission
  • Title affidavits and additional disclosures included

FHA streamline / VA IRRRL

A simplified refinance for existing FHA or VA loans — no full appraisal or income re-verification, lower fees, faster close.

  • Typical package size: 60–90 pages
  • Signing time: 25–40 minutes
  • 3-day right of rescission applies (primary residence)
  • FHA/VA-specific addenda; net tangible benefit certification

Refinance FAQ

Common questions about refinance signings.

How long does a refinance signing take?

A typical refinance signing runs 30 to 60 minutes once everyone is at the table. Packages are usually 80 to 150+ pages, with 6 to 12 documents requiring notarization. Cash-out refis on the longer end; streamline refis on the shorter end.

What ID do I need for a refinance signing?

California law requires a current, unexpired government-issued photo ID for each borrower — driver license, state ID, U.S. passport, or foreign passport. Expired IDs are not accepted. A thumbprint is also required in the notary journal for any document affecting real property.

Do both spouses need to be present even if only one is on the loan?

In California (a community property state), the non-borrowing spouse usually still has to sign certain documents — often a Quitclaim, Interspousal Transfer, or Spousal Acknowledgment. Your lender will tell us in advance who needs to sign which pages.

What is the 3-day right of rescission?

For refinances on a primary residence, federal law gives you 3 business days after signing to cancel the loan. Funds do not disburse and the new loan does not take effect until that window closes. We make sure your Notice of Right to Cancel is signed and dated correctly so the rescission period starts on time.

Can you sign at my home, office, or somewhere else?

Yes — your home, your office, an escrow office, a quiet coffee shop, or anywhere private and well-lit with a flat surface and good light. We come to you anywhere in our service area at the time you choose.

Can you explain my loan terms or rate?

No — we are notaries and signing agents, not loan officers or attorneys. We can show you where to sign, initial, and date, but any question about rate, payment, escrow account, or loan terms goes to your lender or broker.

What if I notice an error in the package mid-signing?

Stop and tell us. We will pause the signing and call your loan officer or escrow officer immediately. It is much easier to correct an error before the package is signed and returned than after.
