Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Olive View-UCLA

Frequently asked questions.

Is Olive View the same as Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center?

No. Olive View-UCLA Medical Center is a Los Angeles County-operated safety-net hospital in Sylmar that is affiliated with UCLA for teaching purposes. Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center is a separate facility on the main UCLA campus in Westwood.

How fast can you get to Olive View from Granada Hills?

Olive View is our closest hospital. We can typically arrive in 10 to 20 minutes from our Granada Hills office. For urgent calls during business hours, we can often be on-site within an hour.

What are visiting hours at Olive View-UCLA Medical Center?

General hospital visiting hours are 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM. All visitors arriving after 6:00 PM and on weekends must sign in using the After Hours Visitors Log. Some units such as the ICU or psychiatric wards may have additional restrictions.

Does Olive View have different visitor rules than private hospitals?

As a county-operated facility, Olive View may have security screening at the entrance and specific visitor ID requirements. All visitors arriving after 6:00 PM or on weekends must sign in using the After Hours Visitors Log.

Can you notarize for a patient who only speaks Spanish or another language?

Olive View serves a very diverse community with many Spanish-speaking and multilingual patients. The signer must be able to communicate with the notary — I can communicate in basic Spanish to assist with understanding. For complex matters, a trusted bilingual family member can help facilitate.

What if the patient was brought in by ambulance and the family does not have their ID?

Emergency admissions often mean the patient's ID is at home. We recommend having a family member retrieve the ID and bring it to the hospital before the appointment. If that is not possible, we will discuss credible witness options under California law.

Do you serve other facilities in Sylmar, Pacoima, and San Fernando?

Yes. In addition to Olive View, we serve patients and families at skilled nursing facilities, rehab centers, assisted living communities, and private homes throughout Sylmar, Pacoima, San Fernando, Arleta, and the surrounding area.
