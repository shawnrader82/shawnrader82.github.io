Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Valley Presbyterian

Frequently asked questions.

How quickly can you get to Valley Presbyterian from Granada Hills?

Valley Presbyterian is one of our closer hospitals. Travel time is typically 15 to 30 minutes via the 405 or Van Nuys Boulevard. During business hours, we can often arrive within 1 to 2 hours for urgent requests.

What are visiting hours at Valley Presbyterian Hospital?

Inpatients are allowed two visitors at a time between 5:00 AM and 9:00 PM. The NICU, Pediatrics, and PICU allow parents/guardians to visit 24/7. In Maternity/Labor and Delivery, one designated support person is allowed 24 hours.

Can you notarize for a patient who primarily speaks Spanish?

Valley Presbyterian serves a large Spanish-speaking community. The signer must be able to communicate with the notary. I can communicate in basic Spanish to assist with understanding. For complex matters, a trusted bilingual family member can help facilitate.

What if the patient needs to sign but is connected to IVs or monitors?

Medical equipment does not prevent a notarization as long as the patient can hold a pen or make a mark and is mentally alert enough to understand the documents. We work around IV lines, monitors, and other medical equipment routinely.

Can I schedule a notary visit for the day of discharge?

Discharge-day notarizations are common but require careful timing. We recommend scheduling the signing as early in the day as possible to allow for delays in the discharge process.

Does Valley Presbyterian have a busy emergency department?

Yes, Valley Presbyterian's Emergency Department treats more than 60,000 patients per year, making it one of the busiest in the central San Fernando Valley. If the patient is being treated in the ER, we recommend waiting until they are admitted to an inpatient room before scheduling.

Do you also serve Sherman Oaks, North Hollywood, and Panorama City?

Yes. Valley Presbyterian draws patients from across the central San Fernando Valley. We serve patients and families at hospitals, homes, senior communities, and medical offices throughout the area.
