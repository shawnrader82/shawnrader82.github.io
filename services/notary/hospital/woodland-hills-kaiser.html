Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Kaiser Woodland Hills

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need to be a Kaiser member to use your notary service?

No. Our mobile notary service is independent from Kaiser Permanente. We come to the hospital to notarize documents for any patient or family member, regardless of their insurance or Kaiser membership status.

What are visiting hours at Kaiser Permanente Woodland Hills?

Adult inpatient visiting hours are Monday through Friday 5:00 PM to 9:00 PM, and weekends and holidays 9:00 AM to 9:00 PM. Pediatric visiting hours are weekends and holidays 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

How quickly can you get to Kaiser Woodland Hills?

Kaiser Woodland Hills is within our Tier 1 service area. Travel time via the 101 or De Soto Avenue is typically 20 to 35 minutes from our Granada Hills base. During business hours, we can often arrive within 1 to 2 hours for urgent requests.

What if the patient is scheduled for surgery and needs documents signed beforehand?

Pre-surgery notarizations are common, especially for powers of attorney or healthcare directives. We recommend scheduling the signing at least a few hours before the procedure to avoid any last-minute rush.

Where do I park when meeting a notary at Kaiser Woodland Hills?

The main campus at 5601 De Soto Avenue has a multi-level parking structure adjacent to the Medical Office Tower, as well as surface lots near the Northside Medical Offices.

Can Kaiser social workers or case managers be present during the signing?

Yes, hospital staff are welcome to be present if the patient or family prefers. However, whether they can serve as official witnesses depends on the document. Some forms prohibit employees of the treating facility from serving as witnesses.

Can you come to the Kaiser Woodland Hills campus for outpatient situations?

Yes. While most hospital notarizations involve inpatients, we can also meet you at the Kaiser campus for outpatient situations, including medical office tower appointments.
