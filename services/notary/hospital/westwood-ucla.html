Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for UCLA Westwood

Frequently asked questions.

Is this the same as UCLA Santa Monica Medical Center or UCLA West Valley?

No. Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center is at 757 Westwood Plaza on the main UCLA campus. UCLA Santa Monica Medical Center is a separate hospital near Wilshire and 16th Street. UCLA West Valley is in West Hills. All three are part of UCLA Health but are distinct facilities.

What are visiting hours at Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center?

Visitors who are symptom-free may register at the information desk for a daily visitor badge. One overnight visitor per patient is allowed. ER allows 2 visitors in rooms and 1 visitor in hallway beds.

How long does it take to get to UCLA Westwood from Granada Hills?

Travel time to Westwood usually ranges from 35 to 60 minutes depending on traffic on the 405 and surface streets like Sunset Boulevard or Wilshire. During rush hour or when there are events on campus, it can take longer.

Can you notarize documents for patients in specialized units like transplant, neurology, or oncology?

Yes. UCLA cares for many complex transplant, neurology, and oncology patients. We can visit these units as long as hospital visitor rules allow and the patient is alert enough to understand and sign documents.

Does UCLA provide its own notary service?

Large academic medical centers sometimes have internal notary options, but availability is often limited and may not cover all document types. We operate as an independent mobile notary service that you hire directly.

Where do you park when coming to UCLA Westwood?

We typically use UCLA visitor parking structures or the valet service available on Westwood Plaza at the hospital entrance. Parking fees and walking time are built into our arrival estimate.

Can you meet family members on campus rather than in the patient room?

Yes. If a document needs notarization but the patient is not the signer, we can meet in a campus lobby, outdoor area, or another convenient spot. Each signer must bring valid ID and appear in person.
