Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Northridge Hospital

Frequently asked questions.

How quickly can you get to Northridge Hospital Medical Center?

Northridge Hospital is one of our closest facilities. During business hours, we can often arrive within 1 to 2 hours for urgent requests, or schedule same-day when availability allows.

What are visiting hours at Northridge Hospital Medical Center?

General visiting hours are typically 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM on weekdays, with slightly different hours on weekends. Specific units such as the ICU, NICU, and maternity may have their own visiting policies.

Can you visit patients in the Northridge Hospital trauma center?

Yes. Northridge Hospital is a designated Level II Trauma Center and the only Pediatric Trauma Center in the San Fernando Valley. We can visit trauma patients as long as the hospital permits visitors and the patient is alert enough to sign.

What if the patient had a stroke and has difficulty signing?

Northridge Hospital is the only Comprehensive Stroke Center in the San Fernando Valley. California law allows for signature by mark if the signer cannot write their full name, as long as witness requirements are met. We will discuss acceptable options with you before the visit.

Can I have documents notarized for a patient who does not speak English?

The signer must be able to communicate with the notary to confirm their identity and understanding of the document. If the patient speaks limited English, a trusted interpreter can help facilitate communication, though California notary law has specific requirements around interpreters.

What happens if the notarization takes longer than expected?

Hospital notarizations sometimes take longer due to interruptions from medical staff, the patient needing rest, or documents requiring additional review. Our hospital visit fee covers reasonable time at the bedside.

Do you serve nearby areas like Reseda, Chatsworth, and Canoga Park?

Yes. In addition to Northridge Hospital, we serve patients and families throughout Northridge, Reseda, Chatsworth, Canoga Park, Winnetka, and the surrounding north San Fernando Valley.
