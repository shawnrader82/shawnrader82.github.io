Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for UCLA West Valley

Frequently asked questions.

Is UCLA West Valley the same as UCLA Westwood or UCLA Santa Monica?

No. UCLA West Valley Medical Center is a 260-bed community hospital in West Hills that joined the UCLA Health system. It is a separate facility from Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center in Westwood and from UCLA Santa Monica Medical Center.

What are visiting hours at UCLA West Valley Medical Center?

Visiting hours are 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. Visitation is limited to 2 visitors per patient at a time. In the Emergency Department, 2 visitors are allowed for patients in rooms and 1 visitor for hallway beds.

How long does it take to get to UCLA West Valley from Granada Hills?

Travel time to West Hills usually ranges from 25 to 40 minutes depending on traffic on the 118, Topanga Canyon, or De Soto. Because this is within the San Fernando Valley, we treat it as a Tier 1 location.

Is the parking situation easier here than at larger Los Angeles hospitals?

Generally yes. Parking at UCLA West Valley is typically less congested than at major campuses like UCLA Westwood or Cedars-Sinai. The main hospital address is 7300 Medical Center Drive, with several convenient lots adjacent.

Can you coordinate with my attorney or financial advisor for documents?

Yes. Many West Valley patients work with outside attorneys or advisors who prepare documents for signing at the hospital. Your attorney can email documents to you or directly to us for printing with our laptop and portable printer.

Can you meet family members in the lobby instead of the patient room?

Yes. If the document involves a family member rather than the patient, we can meet in the hospital lobby, cafeteria, or another quiet spot. Each signer must appear in person with valid ID.

Do you also serve nearby areas like Calabasas and Woodland Hills?

Yes. In addition to UCLA West Valley, we serve homes, medical offices, and senior communities in West Hills, Canoga Park, Woodland Hills, Chatsworth, Porter Ranch, and Calabasas at Tier 1 rates.
