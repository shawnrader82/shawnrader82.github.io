Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Kaiser LA (Sunset)

Frequently asked questions.

Is this the same Kaiser as the one in Woodland Hills?

No. Kaiser Permanente Los Angeles Medical Center on Sunset Boulevard is a separate 492-bed facility from Kaiser Permanente Woodland Hills. The Sunset campus is Kaiser's flagship Los Angeles hospital.

The Kaiser LA campus is large — how do I direct you to the right building?

The campus spans multiple blocks along Sunset Boulevard with buildings at 4700, 4760, 4867, 4900, and 4950 W. Sunset Blvd. When you contact us, share the patient's specific building address and room or unit number so we can navigate directly to you.

What are visiting hours at Kaiser Permanente Los Angeles Medical Center?

Visiting hours are open with no time restrictions. However, hospital quiet hours are observed from 9:00 PM to 5:00 AM. All visitors must meet hospital visitor requirements and check in at registration.

Can you notarize for a patient awaiting or recovering from transplant surgery?

Yes. Kaiser LA handles complex transplant cases including liver and bone marrow transplants. Families often need powers of attorney or healthcare directives notarized before or after major procedures. We coordinate closely with family and the care team.

Do I need to be a Kaiser member for you to come to this hospital?

No. Our mobile notary service is completely independent from Kaiser Permanente. We come to the hospital to notarize documents for any patient, family member, or visitor regardless of insurance or membership status.

How long does it take to get to Kaiser LA Sunset from Granada Hills?

Travel time typically takes 35 to 55 minutes via the 101 or 170 freeways, but Hollywood traffic can push that past an hour during rush hour or event nights. We confirm conditions before departing.

What if parking is full when you arrive?

Parking at the Kaiser LA campus can be competitive, especially during weekday business hours. We build extra time into our arrival window to account for parking challenges. We keep you updated by text.
