Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Keck USC

Frequently asked questions.

Is Keck Hospital of USC the same as LA General Medical Center?

No. Keck Hospital of USC is a private 401-bed academic hospital operated by Keck Medicine of USC at 1500 San Pablo Street. LA General Medical Center is a separate county-operated hospital nearby. We serve both facilities.

Can you visit patients in transplant, oncology, or specialized units at Keck Hospital?

Yes — we can visit most inpatient units as long as hospital visitor policies allow and the patient is alert enough to understand and sign documents. We coordinate timing with family members and nursing staff.

What are Keck Hospital visiting hours?

General visiting hours at Keck Hospital of USC are 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. After-hours visits may be approved by the lead RN on the patient's unit based on medical condition and hospital operations.

How long does it take to get to Keck Hospital from Granada Hills?

Travel time typically ranges from 40 to 60 minutes depending on traffic on the 5, 10, and 101 freeways and local congestion around the campus.

Can you coordinate with my attorney or care team for documents at Keck Hospital?

Yes. Many Keck patients work with attorneys, financial advisors, or care coordinators who prepare documents in advance. Your attorney can email documents to you or directly to us for printing using our laptop and portable printer service.

What if the patient is transferred between Keck and LA General?

If a transfer is planned, it is often easier to complete notarization before the move if time allows. If the patient has already transferred, contact us and we will redirect to the new facility.

Do you serve nearby areas like East LA, Montebello, and Alhambra?

Yes. In addition to Keck Hospital, we serve homes, senior communities, and medical offices in Boyle Heights, East Los Angeles, Montebello, Alhambra, Monterey Park, and surrounding neighborhoods.
