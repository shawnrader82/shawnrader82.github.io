Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Hollywood Presbyterian

Frequently asked questions.

Is Hollywood Presbyterian the same as Kaiser LA or Cedars-Sinai?

No. CHA Hollywood Presbyterian Medical Center is a separate 434-bed community hospital at 1300 North Vermont Avenue in East Hollywood, owned by the South Korea-based CHA Medical Group. It is distinct from Kaiser Permanente Los Angeles and from Cedars-Sinai.

Can you notarize documents in the new patient tower or ICU?

Yes — we can visit patients in the new $400 million patient tower, ICU, or other specialized units as long as hospital visitor rules allow and the patient is alert enough to understand and sign documents.

What are visiting hours at Hollywood Presbyterian Medical Center?

Visiting hours are 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. Two asymptomatic visitors are allowed at a time in all areas. Visitors may leave and re-enter during visiting hours. Face masks are required in all patient care areas.

How long does it take to get to Hollywood Presbyterian from Granada Hills?

Travel time to East Hollywood typically ranges from 35 to 55 minutes depending on 170/101 freeway traffic and local congestion near Vermont and Sunset. During rush hour or event days it can take longer.

Do you serve nearby neighborhoods like Los Feliz, Silver Lake, and Koreatown?

Yes. In addition to bedside visits at Hollywood Presbyterian, we serve homes, senior communities, and medical offices in Los Feliz, Silver Lake, Echo Park, Hollywood, and Koreatown.

Can you meet family members in the lobby instead of the patient room?

Yes. If the document involves a family member rather than the patient, we can meet in the hospital lobby, cafeteria, or another quiet spot. Each signer must appear in person with valid ID.

What if parking at Hollywood Presbyterian is difficult?

Visitor parking is available on campus and street parking is accessible on Vermont Avenue. We allow extra time for parking and will text you when we are on-site.
