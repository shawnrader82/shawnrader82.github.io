Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Encino Hospital

Frequently asked questions.

Is Encino Hospital the same as Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana?

No. Encino Hospital Medical Center is a separate 148-bed community hospital on Ventura Boulevard in Encino, operated by the Prime Healthcare Foundation. Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana is a larger, separate facility about two miles east.

How quickly can you get to Encino Hospital from Granada Hills?

Travel time is typically 20 to 30 minutes via Balboa Boulevard or White Oak Avenue heading south through the Valley. During business hours, we can often arrive within 1 to 2 hours for urgent requests.

What are visiting hours at Encino Hospital Medical Center?

General visiting hours are 9:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily, with two visitors allowed per patient. Critical care areas (ICU, CCU) allow 2 visitors at a time for 10 minutes every hour. Children under 12 are not permitted as visitors.

Is Encino Hospital smaller than other Valley hospitals?

Yes. With 148 licensed beds, Encino Hospital is one of the smaller acute-care facilities in the San Fernando Valley. This is an advantage for notary visits — the campus is compact, parking is easier, and we can reach the bedside quickly.

Can you also visit patients at nearby assisted living or skilled nursing facilities?

Yes. Encino and the surrounding Ventura Boulevard corridor have a number of assisted living communities and skilled nursing facilities. If a patient is discharged from Encino Hospital to a nearby facility, we can arrange a follow-up visit.

Do you serve Sherman Oaks, Reseda, and Tarzana from here?

Yes. We regularly serve patients and families in Encino, Sherman Oaks, Tarzana, Reseda, Lake Balboa, and Winnetka — all within our Tier 1 service area.

What documents are most commonly notarized at Encino Hospital?

Powers of attorney and advance healthcare directives are the most common. We also notarize living wills, HIPAA authorizations, MOLST/POLST forms, and financial documents for patients and families.
