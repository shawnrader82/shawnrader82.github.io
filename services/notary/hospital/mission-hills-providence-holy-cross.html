Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Providence Holy Cross

Frequently asked questions.

How quickly can you get to Providence Holy Cross Medical Center?

Because Holy Cross is one of our closest hospitals — just minutes from our Granada Hills base — we can often arrive within 1 to 2 hours for urgent requests during business hours, or schedule a same-day appointment when availability allows.

What are visiting hours at Providence Holy Cross Medical Center?

Providence Holy Cross welcomes visitors any time of the day, seven days a week. Nighttime quiet hours are 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM. Visitors who remain during quiet time are encouraged to stay in the patient's room.

Can you notarize in the ICU or trauma unit at Holy Cross?

Yes — we can visit patients in the ICU, trauma unit, or other restricted areas as long as the hospital allows visitors and the patient is alert enough to understand and sign documents. ICU visiting may have additional requirements — confirm with the nursing staff.

What if the patient cannot hold a pen or sign their name?

California law allows for signature by mark (such as an X) if the signer cannot write their full name, as long as certain witness requirements are met. In some cases, a signature by proxy may be possible. We will discuss the right approach with you before the visit.

Can hospital social workers or nurses witness the documents?

It depends on the document. Some forms allow hospital staff to serve as witnesses, while others require independent witnesses not named in the document. Healthcare directives, for example, have specific witness rules. We review requirements before each visit.

What if the patient is discharged before you arrive?

If the patient is discharged or moved before our scheduled arrival, contact us as soon as possible. We can often redirect to a home, rehab facility, or other location nearby.

Do you also serve Sylmar, Granada Hills, and Chatsworth?

Yes. In addition to Providence Holy Cross, we serve patients and families at homes, senior communities, and medical offices throughout Mission Hills, Granada Hills, Sylmar, Chatsworth, and the surrounding north Valley area.
