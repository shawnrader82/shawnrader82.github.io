Before your appointment

What to have ready for a bedside notary visit.

✓

Valid photo ID

Every signer needs a current government-issued photo ID — California driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, or equivalent. If the patient's ID is at home, have a family member bring it before the appointment.

✓

All document pages

Bring complete documents — not just the signature page. If you need documents generated on-site, we bring a laptop, portable printer, and internet connection to produce forms during our visit.

✓

Any required witnesses

Healthcare directives typically require two witnesses who are not named in the document and are not employees of the treating facility. Confirm witness requirements for your specific document before we arrive.

Mobile Notary FAQ for Los Robles

Frequently asked questions.

How long does it take you to get to Los Robles from Granada Hills?

Travel to Thousand Oaks via the 101 freeway typically takes 40 to 60 minutes in normal traffic. During morning or evening rush hour, the drive through Calabasas and Agoura Hills can take longer.

What are visiting hours at Los Robles Regional Medical Center?

Visiting hours are Monday through Friday 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. Extended visiting hours may apply for laboring mothers in labor and delivery, hospice or palliative care patients, and pediatric and NICU patients.

Can you visit patients in the Los Robles trauma center?

Yes. Los Robles has the only Level II Trauma Center in eastern Ventura County. We can visit trauma patients as long as the hospital permits visitors and the patient is conscious, alert, and able to understand and sign documents.

Is Los Robles in Los Angeles County or Ventura County?

Los Robles Regional Medical Center is in Thousand Oaks, which is in Ventura County. Our California notary commission covers the entire state, so county lines do not affect whether we can notarize your documents.

What if I need multiple family members to sign different documents?

We can notarize documents for multiple signers during a single hospital visit. Each person who signs will need their own valid government-issued photo ID and must personally appear before the notary.

Can you coordinate with my attorney who is sending documents from their office?

Yes. If your attorney is emailing or faxing documents and you do not have a way to print them, our laptop and portable printer service solves that problem. Your attorney can email documents directly to us for printing before the visit.

Do you also serve Westlake Village, Newbury Park, and Agoura Hills?

Yes. While Los Robles is in Thousand Oaks, we serve patients and families throughout the Conejo Valley, including Westlake Village, Newbury Park, Agoura Hills, and Oak Park at Tier 2 rates.
