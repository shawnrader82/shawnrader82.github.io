Understanding the form

What the DS-3053 Statement of Consent does.

The DS-3053 is the U.S. State Department's official form for one parent to authorize the issuance of a passport to a child under age 16 when the other parent cannot appear in person at the passport acceptance facility.

When DS-3053 is required

  • One parent cannot attend in person: the other parent is in a different city, state, or country.
  • Work, military, or travel conflicts: the non-attending parent has a commitment that prevents them from going to the passport office.
  • Divorced or separated parents: both share legal custody but only one can appear for the child's passport appointment.
  • Legal guardian situations: a guardian cannot attend but still needs to provide documented consent.
  • Both parents consent but only one applies: the appearing parent brings the signed, notarized DS-3053 from the other parent.

When DS-3053 is not used

  • Both parents appear in person: no consent form is needed when both parents are present at the passport acceptance facility.
  • Sole legal custody with documentation: the appearing parent can submit court-ordered custody documents instead of a DS-3053.
  • Other parent completely unavailable: if the other parent is deceased, incapacitated, or otherwise unable to sign, Form DS-5525 (Statement of Exigent or Special Family Circumstances) may apply instead.
  • Travel authorization only: if you need to authorize travel rather than passport issuance, a Minor Travel Consent Letter is the appropriate document.

State Department requirements

What the notarization of DS-3053 actually involves.

The DS-3053 has a built-in notarial section that the notary completes after verifying the signer's identity. The State Department also requires a notarized copy of the signer's valid photo ID to be submitted with the form — both can be handled at the same appointment.

Signing requirements

  • The non-appearing parent or guardian must sign the DS-3053 in the notary's presence — the form cannot be pre-signed.
  • The signer must present a valid, current, government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport).
  • The name on the ID must match the name of the parent or guardian listed on the form.
  • The notary completes the jurat-style section on the form, applies their seal, and records the act in the journal.
  • The form must be printed and completed in black ink following the State Department's instructions before the appointment.

Notarized ID copy requirement

  1. The State Department requires the non-appearing parent to submit a notarized copy of their valid photo ID along with the signed DS-3053.
  2. The notary certifies a photocopy of the ID by completing a copy-certification statement and applying their seal.
  3. Both the signed DS-3053 and the notarized ID copy are submitted together at the child's passport appointment.
  4. The appearing parent brings both documents — the notarized form and the notarized ID copy — to the passport acceptance facility.

Important: A notary can verify identity, notarize the DS-3053 signature, and certify a copy of the signer's ID, but cannot complete the form for you, advise on custody situations, or confirm which documents the passport agency will accept. Always verify current requirements directly with the U.S. State Department at travel.state.gov.

Before the appointment

A short DS-3053 checklist that prevents rejection at the passport office.

  • DS-3053 form printed and completed — but not yet signed.
  • Valid, current government-issued photo ID for the signer (driver's license, state ID, or passport).
  • A photocopy of that ID for the notary to certify (or the notary can make the copy on-site).
  • Child's full legal name and date of birth as they will appear on the passport application.
  • Custody or court documents if the situation involves non-standard custody arrangements — for your reference.
  • Exact appointment address, access notes, and contact phone number.

Timing note

The notarized DS-3053 is generally valid for 90 days. Schedule the notarization close to the child's passport appointment to avoid expiration before submission.

  • Confirm the passport acceptance facility's current DS-3053 requirements before the notary appointment.
  • Ask about the ID copy requirement in advance — the notarized copy is separate from presenting the original ID.
  • If both DS-3053 and a Minor Travel Consent Letter are needed, both can be handled at the same visit.

Sole custody and special circumstances

DS-3053 and DS-5525 — understanding which applies.

Most situations where one parent cannot attend call for DS-3053. When the other parent is completely unavailable — not just absent — DS-5525 addresses those special circumstances.

FAQ

DS-3053 passport consent notary FAQ

What is the DS-3053 form used for?

The DS-3053 is the U.S. State Department's Statement of Consent for Issuance of a Passport to a Minor Under Age 16. It is used when only one parent or guardian can appear in person at the passport acceptance facility. The non-appearing parent signs and notarizes the form to consent to the passport being issued.

Does the DS-3053 have to be notarized?

Yes. The DS-3053 must be signed in front of a notary public. The notary completes the jurat-style notarial section on the form, confirms the signer's identity, and applies their seal. The passport agency will not accept a DS-3053 that was not properly notarized.

Does the signer's photo ID copy also need to be notarized?

Yes. The State Department requires the non-appearing parent to submit a notarized copy of their valid photo ID together with the signed DS-3053. The notary certifies the copy at the same appointment — you do not need a separate visit for the ID copy.

How long is the DS-3053 valid after notarization?

The DS-3053 is generally valid for 90 days from the date it is signed and notarized. Schedule the notarization close to the child's passport appointment to avoid the form expiring before it can be submitted to the passport agency.

What if one parent has sole legal custody?

If one parent holds sole legal custody and can present court-issued documentation, the DS-3053 is not required. If the other parent is deceased, incapacitated, or otherwise completely unavailable and cannot sign, Form DS-5525 (Statement of Exigent or Special Family Circumstances) may apply instead. Confirm the correct procedure with the passport agency or travel.state.gov before scheduling.

Can a mobile notary help access and print the DS-3053 on-site?

Yes. If the form is not printed yet, a mobile workstation with a laptop and portable printer can be brought to the appointment. You pull up the DS-3053 from travel.state.gov, fill it in yourself, print it, and then sign and notarize it in the same visit. The notary does not complete the form for you or provide legal advice.
