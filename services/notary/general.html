Most general notary visits come down to four things.

Get these details clear and the rest of the appointment usually moves in 30–45 minutes.

1

What document do you have?

Power of attorney, affidavit, bill of sale, guardianship form, travel consent, copy certification, name-change affidavit, or something else.

2

Who is signing and where?

Number of signers, whether any are elderly, hospitalized, or in custody, and the address where everyone will meet the notary.

3

What ID will each signer use?

Drivers license, state ID, passport, or another ID that meets California notary rules, all current and in each signer’s name.

4

Is there a deadline?

Court date, flight, loan signing, or other specific cutoff. Same-day and after-hours visits are often possible when we know timing up front.

The visit

How a general mobile notary appointment usually works

1

Share the basics

You contact us with your location, document type, number of signers, and any deadlines. We confirm availability, travel fees, and per-signature notary fees up front.

2

Prepare documents & ID

You gather the unsigned documents, valid ID for each signer, and any required witnesses. Documents should be filled out but not signed until the notary is present.

3

Notary visit & signing

The notary arrives on time, verifies each signer’s identity, reviews the notary wording, and performs the appropriate acknowledgment or jurat before signatures are completed.

4

Certificates, seal, and journal

The notary completes the notarial certificate, applies the seal, records the appointment in the journal, and returns the notarized documents to you on the spot.

Important: The notary’s role is to verify identity, witness signatures, and complete required notarial certificates. For legal advice, document selection, or strategy, you should consult an attorney or the organization that requested the documents.

Before we arrive

A short checklist that keeps most general notary visits on track

  • Unsigned documents completed as much as possible (no signatures yet).
  • Valid, current government-issued photo ID for each signer.
  • Any required witnesses confirmed and available at the appointment time.
  • Exact address, parking or access instructions, and contact phone number.
  • Any special instructions from your attorney, lender, or requesting agency.
  • Awareness of any deadlines such as court dates, flights, or closings.

Helpful note

We’ll walk through anything you’re unsure about when you call or text, but having these pieces ready usually keeps the visit to about 30–45 minutes.

  • Texting a photo of the notary section can help confirm the correct wording.
  • If the requesting agency gave written instructions, send those ahead of time.
  • If a signer has mobility or capacity concerns, mention that so we can plan appropriately.

FAQ

General mobile notary FAQ

What is a mobile notary?

A mobile notary is a commissioned notary public who travels to meet you where you are — home, office, hospital, jail, or another agreed location — instead of requiring you to visit a fixed office or bank branch.

Which everyday documents can you notarize?

Most documents that use standard California acknowledgment or jurat wording can be notarized, including powers of attorney, affidavits, bills of sale, guardianship forms, travel consent letters, copy certification statements, name-change affidavits, and many more.

What identification do signers need?

Each signer must present valid, current government-issued photo ID such as a drivers license, state ID, passport, or another ID acceptable under California notary law. Expired IDs are generally not acceptable.

Can you explain my documents or tell me what to sign?

The notary can point out the notarial certificate and where signatures and initials belong, but cannot give legal advice, interpret legal language, or recommend which document you should sign. Those questions must go to your attorney, lender, or the requesting agency.

Can you bring a printer or help if I do not have the document ready?

Yes. A mobile office setup with a laptop, portable printer, and scanner can be brought so you can access templates from your attorney, bank, consulate, or a legal document platform, then print, sign, and notarize in one visit.

Do you handle apostille and international use?

Many notarized documents can be apostilled or legalized for use abroad. If your destination country or consulate requires an apostille, we can notarize your documents and coordinate the apostille or legalization process as a separate service.
