About Dorothy Kirby Center SYTF

Juvenile detention notary service at Dorothy Kirby Center SYTF.

Dorothy Kirby Center on McDonnell Avenue in East Los Angeles was historically known as LA County’s girls’ juvenile facility. Today it operates as a co‑ed Secure Youth Treatment Facility (SYTF) with a strong mental‑health treatment component, housing youth of any gender who have been committed by the court for longer-term stays. As with the county’s other SYTFs, families come to us for the paperwork that keeps life outside moving during a long commitment — vehicle titles, real estate matters, family law filings, and education or college enrollment forms for youth turning 18 while still in custody. We coordinate every visit through the youth’s attorney and the center’s professional-visit procedures.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Dorothy Kirby Center SYTF

Frequently asked questions.

Is Dorothy Kirby Center only for girls?

No, not anymore. While Dorothy Kirby Center was historically LA County’s girls’ juvenile facility, it now operates as a co‑ed Secure Youth Treatment Facility serving youth of any gender, with a particular focus on mental health treatment.

What documents come up most often at Dorothy Kirby Center?

Vehicle title transfers, real estate paperwork, family law documents, long-term financial powers of attorney, and education or college enrollment forms — the same long-stay document types common across LA County’s SYTFs.

Can a youth under 18 sign a notarized document at Kirby?

In many cases yes, but it depends on the specific document. A notary can notarize the signature of a competent signer regardless of age; the legal effect for a minor is a question for your attorney.

My child turned 18 at Kirby — do they still need parental consent?

Generally, once a young person turns 18 they can sign most documents in their own right, though some document types or facility rules may still involve the family. Confirm with your attorney before the visit.

Is Dorothy Kirby Center a Juvenile Hall or SYTF?

It is a Secure Youth Treatment Facility (SYTF) for youth already committed by the court. Pre-adjudication youth in LA County are held at Los Padrinos in Downey instead.

Do I need my attorney present for the visit?

Not necessarily in person, but involved either way — the attorney requests a court order authorizing our entry, or sponsors us into their own professional visit. Call us and we’ll coordinate with your attorney directly.

Can you handle a vehicle title transfer for a youth at Kirby?

Yes — we bring title documents into the facility, verify the youth’s identity, and notarize their signature as seller or transferor per DMV requirements.

Do you access sealed juvenile records at Kirby?

No. We do not access or handle sealed juvenile records protected under Welfare & Institutions Code § 827. We notarize signatures on documents your family or attorney provides.

How do I confirm my child is at Dorothy Kirby Center?

Call the LA County Probation central visitor information line at (562) 392-7254, or ask your attorney to confirm placement.
