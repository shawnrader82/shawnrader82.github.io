About Los Padrinos Juvenile Hall

Juvenile detention notary service at Los Padrinos Juvenile Hall.

Los Padrinos Juvenile Hall in Downey is Los Angeles County’s primary pre‑adjudication juvenile facility — since a 2023 consolidation of the county’s juvenile halls, it is currently LA County’s only operational Juvenile Hall. Youth held here are awaiting a court decision on their case; stays are typically short, measured in days to a few weeks, which means paperwork tends to be urgent. Families call us when a document cannot wait — an emergency Power of Attorney so a parent can manage the youth’s bank account, school enrollment, or medical care, a parental consent form, or an affidavit tied to an upcoming defense motion. We coordinate every visit through the youth’s defense attorney and the facility’s professional-visit procedures.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Los Padrinos Juvenile Hall

Frequently asked questions.

How fast can you get to Los Padrinos Juvenile Hall?

Same-day or next-day service is often possible once your attorney confirms access, but juvenile facility entry always requires attorney coordination first — either a court order or the attorney sponsoring us into their own visit. Call us as soon as you know documents are needed and we will start the attorney conversation immediately.

What if my child's case is still pending?

That is the normal situation at Los Padrinos — it is a pre-adjudication facility, meaning nearly every youth there has an open case. We do not need to know case details to notarize a document; we simply need your attorney to coordinate access and the document itself ready to sign.

What documents come up most often at Los Padrinos?

Emergency Powers of Attorney so a parent can manage a youth’s bank account, school records, or medical care; parental consent forms; custodial authorizations; and affidavits attached to a defense motion or bail-related filing.

Do I need a lawyer to get a notary in to see my child?

Yes. California juvenile facilities require the youth’s defense attorney to be involved — either requesting a court order authorizing our entry or bringing us in as part of their own professional visit. If your child does not yet have an attorney, the public defender or appointed counsel assigned to the case can typically make this request.

Can my child sign documents if they are under 18?

In many cases yes, but it depends on the specific document. A notary can notarize the signature of a competent signer regardless of age; whether that signature has legal effect for a particular document is a question for your attorney. We frequently notarize parental signatures on consent forms where the parent, not the minor, is the signer.

Is Los Padrinos the only juvenile hall in LA County now?

Yes, currently. Since a 2023 consolidation, Los Padrinos is LA County’s only operational Juvenile Hall for pre-adjudication youth. Youth who have already been committed by the court serve their time at one of the county’s Secure Youth Treatment Facilities instead.

Can you access my child's case file or court records?

No. Juvenile records are protected under Welfare & Institutions Code § 827 and we do not access or handle sealed records. We only notarize signatures on documents your family or attorney provides to us.

What if my child is transferred to a different facility?

If the court commits your youth following adjudication, they would move to a Secure Youth Treatment Facility such as Barry J. Nidorf, Campus Kilpatrick, or Dorothy Kirby. We serve all of these — let us know if a transfer happens and we will adjust the visit accordingly.

How do I reach LA County Probation about visitation?

Call the LA County Probation central visitor information line at (562) 392-7254 for current visitation policy and to confirm the youth’s location. We can also help you confirm details once we know the youth’s name and date of birth.
