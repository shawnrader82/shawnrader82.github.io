About Riverside Juvenile Hall

Juvenile detention notary service at Riverside Juvenile Hall.

Riverside Juvenile Hall sits at 9991 County Farm Rd in the city of Riverside — this is Riverside County’s central juvenile facility. Important correction for families searching online: the Riverside County juvenile detention facility is located in Riverside city, not Indio. Indio has only an adult jail; there is no juvenile facility there. Riverside Juvenile Hall houses pre‑adjudication youth awaiting a court decision, and the adjacent Alan M. Crogan Youth Treatment & Education Center houses youth who have already been committed by the court for longer-term treatment and education programming. We notarize documents for both populations — urgent Powers of Attorney and affidavits for pending cases, and vehicle titles, real estate, family law, and education paperwork for committed youth — coordinated through the youth’s attorney.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Riverside Juvenile Hall

Frequently asked questions.

Is the Riverside County juvenile facility located in Indio?

No — this is a common mix-up. The Riverside County juvenile detention facility is located at 9991 County Farm Rd in the city of Riverside. Indio has only an adult jail; there is no juvenile facility in Indio.

What is the Alan M. Crogan Youth Treatment & Education Center?

It is the adjacent post-adjudication facility at the same Riverside campus, housing youth who have already been committed by the court for longer-term treatment and education programming, similar to an SYTF in other counties.

Does Riverside Juvenile Hall hold both types of youth?

The main Juvenile Hall houses pre-adjudication youth awaiting a court decision, while the adjacent Alan M. Crogan Youth Treatment & Education Center houses post-adjudication, committed youth. Together the campus serves both populations.

How fast can you get there for an urgent case?

Same-day or next-day service is often possible for urgent pre-adjudication documents once your attorney confirms access, but juvenile facility entry always requires attorney coordination first.

What documents come up most for committed youth at Crogan YTEC?

Vehicle title transfers, real estate paperwork, family law documents, long-term financial powers of attorney, and education or college enrollment forms.

What documents come up most for pending cases at Riverside Juvenile Hall?

Emergency Powers of Attorney, parental consent forms, custodial authorizations, and affidavits attached to a defense motion.

Do I need a lawyer to get a notary in to see my child?

Yes. California juvenile facilities require the youth’s attorney to be involved — either requesting a court order authorizing our entry, or bringing us in as part of their own professional visit.

Do you access sealed juvenile records?

No. We do not access or handle sealed juvenile records protected under Welfare & Institutions Code § 827. We notarize signatures on documents your family or attorney provides.

How do I confirm my child's exact location at the Riverside campus?

Call the facility directly at (951) 358-4200, or ask your attorney to confirm whether the youth is at Riverside Juvenile Hall or the Alan M. Crogan Youth Treatment & Education Center.
