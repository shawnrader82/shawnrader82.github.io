About Barry J. Nidorf SYTF

Juvenile detention notary service at Barry J. Nidorf SYTF.

Barry J. Nidorf in Sylmar is one of the best-known juvenile facility names in Los Angeles County, having held both pre- and post-adjudication youth for decades. Since the county’s 2023 juvenile hall consolidation, Nidorf now operates as a Secure Youth Treatment Facility (SYTF) — housing youth who have already been committed by the court, often for multi-year terms that can extend to age 25 under the framework that replaced the state’s Division of Juvenile Justice. Because these are long stays, the paperwork families bring us looks a lot like adult jail work: selling or transferring a vehicle the youth still owns, real estate matters, family law filings, and college or education enrollment forms for youth who turn 18 during their commitment. We coordinate every visit through the youth’s attorney and Nidorf’s professional-visit procedures.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Barry J. Nidorf SYTF

Frequently asked questions.

What documents come up most during a long-term commitment at Nidorf?

Vehicle title transfers when a car the youth owns needs to be sold or transferred, real estate paperwork, family law documents (including custody of the youth’s own children), long-term financial powers of attorney, and education or college enrollment forms.

Can a youth under 18 sign a notarized document at Nidorf SYTF?

In many cases yes, but it depends on the document. A notary can notarize the signature of a competent signer regardless of age; the document’s legal effect for a minor is a separate legal question best confirmed with your attorney.

My child turned 18 while inside — do they still need parental consent?

Generally, once a young person turns 18 they can sign most documents in their own right without a parent’s signature, though some document types or facility rules may still involve the family. Confirm the specific requirement with your attorney before the visit.

How long do SYTF commitments typically last?

SYTF commitments vary by case but can run from many months to several years, and under the framework that replaced the state’s Division of Juvenile Justice, jurisdiction can extend until age 25. This is why long-term paperwork like real estate and family law documents comes up more often at SYTFs than at pre-adjudication juvenile halls.

Is Nidorf still a Juvenile Hall or is it fully SYTF now?

Since LA County’s 2023 consolidation, Nidorf operates as a Secure Youth Treatment Facility housing post-adjudication, committed youth. The county’s pre-adjudication Juvenile Hall function moved to Los Padrinos in Downey.

Do I need my attorney present for the notary visit?

Not necessarily present in person, but involved either way — the attorney requests a court order authorizing our entry, or sponsors us into their own professional visit with the youth. Call us and we’ll coordinate with your attorney directly.

Can you handle a vehicle title transfer if the youth is the registered owner?

Yes — this is one of the most common SYTF requests. We bring the title documents into the facility, verify the youth’s identity, and notarize their signature as seller or transferor per DMV requirements.

Do you access sealed juvenile records at Nidorf?

No. We do not access or handle sealed juvenile records protected under Welfare & Institutions Code § 827. We notarize signatures on documents your family or attorney provides.

How do I confirm my child is at Nidorf and not another facility?

Call the LA County Probation central visitor information line at (562) 392-7254, or ask your attorney to confirm placement. We can help coordinate once we know the youth’s name and date of birth.
