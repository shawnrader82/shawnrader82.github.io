About Campus Kilpatrick SYTF

Juvenile detention notary service at Campus Kilpatrick SYTF.

Campus Kilpatrick sits on Encinal Canyon Road in the Santa Monica Mountains above Malibu — a deliberately remote, smaller-scale setting designed around a therapeutic treatment model rather than a traditional detention environment. It operates as one of LA County’s Secure Youth Treatment Facilities (SYTF), housing youth who have already been committed by the court for longer-term stays. Because the location is farther from central LA than the county’s other juvenile facilities, families sometimes find scheduling logistics take a bit more planning — we handle the drive and coordinate arrival times around the facility’s professional-visit windows. Documents we notarize here mirror other SYTFs: vehicle titles, real estate matters, family law paperwork, and education forms for youth turning 18 inside.

Mobile Notary FAQ — Campus Kilpatrick SYTF

Frequently asked questions.

Why is Campus Kilpatrick located so far from the city?

Campus Kilpatrick uses a therapeutic treatment model that intentionally places youth in a quieter, more remote canyon setting away from the environments tied to their prior circumstances. It is one of LA County’s smaller Secure Youth Treatment Facilities.

Does the remote location affect notary scheduling?

It can add drive time and, because the facility is smaller, professional-visit windows may be more limited than at larger facilities. We recommend reaching out with a few days’ notice when the situation allows, though we do handle urgent requests.

What documents come up most often at Campus Kilpatrick?

Vehicle title transfers, real estate paperwork, family law documents, long-term financial powers of attorney, and education or college enrollment forms — the same long-stay document types common across LA County’s SYTFs.

Can a youth under 18 sign a notarized document at Kilpatrick?

In many cases yes, but it depends on the specific document. A notary can notarize the signature of a competent signer regardless of age; the legal effect for a minor is a question for your attorney.

Is Campus Kilpatrick the same as a regular Juvenile Hall?

No. Campus Kilpatrick is a Secure Youth Treatment Facility (SYTF) for youth already committed by the court, using a therapeutic model rather than a traditional detention setting. Pre-adjudication youth in LA County are held at Los Padrinos in Downey instead.

Do I need my attorney present for the visit?

Not necessarily in person, but involved either way — the attorney requests a court order authorizing our entry, or sponsors us into their own professional visit. Call us and we’ll coordinate with your attorney directly.

Can you handle a vehicle title transfer if the youth is inside?

Yes — we bring title documents into the facility, verify the youth’s identity, and notarize their signature as seller or transferor per DMV requirements.

Do you access sealed juvenile records at Kilpatrick?

No. We do not access or handle sealed juvenile records protected under Welfare & Institutions Code § 827. We notarize signatures on documents your family or attorney provides.

How do I confirm my child is at Campus Kilpatrick?

Call the LA County Probation central visitor information line at (562) 392-7254, or ask your attorney to confirm placement.
