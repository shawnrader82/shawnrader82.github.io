The Consular Legalization Process

Every non-Hague destination follows the same basic sequence, but the details vary by country. Here's the universal framework:

Step 1

Notarize

Sign before a California Notary. The Notary attaches an acknowledgment or jurat certificate. Some documents (birth certs, court records) already have certified copies and skip this step.

Step 2

State certification

California Secretary of State certifies the Notary's authority — or, for federally-issued documents (FBI reports, USDA certificates), the US Department of State authenticates first.

Step 3

Consular legalization

The destination country's embassy or consulate reviews and applies its own legalization stamp. Each consulate has different forms, fees, hours, and ID requirements.

Step 4

In-country attestation

Some countries — mostly Gulf states — require an additional Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation once the document arrives in-country. Confirm with the receiving party.
