Documents used outside the U.S.

Apostille help for Burbank & Glendale residents

When local documents need an apostille

In Burbank and Glendale, many families have relatives abroad and many studios and tech companies work internationally, so documents often need a California apostille or similar certification before embassies, schools, banks, or foreign partners will accept them.

Documents that commonly require an apostille

We frequently handle birth, marriage, and death certificates, dual‑citizenship and immigration records, school transcripts for students in animation or film programs, powers of attorney for property or royalties overseas, court orders, and business and production agreements used abroad.

How we guide you through the apostille process

We confirm the destination country, review the documents you already have, and explain whether they must be notarized, certified, or reissued before an apostille can be attached. Then we outline realistic fees and timelines—and any consulate or courier steps—so you can plan around court dates, closings, or travel. For urgent matters, see our same‑day apostille service.
