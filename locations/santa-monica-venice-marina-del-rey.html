Documents used outside the U.S.

Apostille help for Santa Monica, Venice & Marina del Rey

Many Westside clients have work, property, or family overseas, so documents signed here often need a California apostille before they are accepted abroad.

Documents that may require an apostille

Typical requests include powers of attorney for property or bank accounts abroad; business and contract documents for international clients; birth, marriage, and death certificates used for citizenship or family law overseas; and school transcripts and diplomas for students studying or working abroad.

How we guide you through the process

We confirm which country will receive the paperwork and what form they require, explain whether your documents must be notarized, certified, or reissued before an apostille, and outline realistic fees and timelines so you can plan around travel, school, or court dates. For urgent cases, see our same-day apostille service.

Secure delivery options

Once your apostille is issued, we can coordinate secure return delivery to you, your attorney, or an out-of-country contact so the documents arrive where they need to go without you handling logistics yourself.
