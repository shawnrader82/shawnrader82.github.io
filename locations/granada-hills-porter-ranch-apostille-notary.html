Apostille help for Granada Hills & Porter Ranch

When North Valley documents need an apostille

Many Granada Hills and Porter Ranch families have relatives, property, or business interests overseas, and professionals here often work with partners abroad. A California apostille confirms that a notarized document or government‑issued record is authentic so it can be accepted in countries that recognize the Hague Convention.

Documents that may require an apostille

Common requests include birth, marriage, and death certificates; school records and transcripts; powers of attorney for property or bank accounts overseas; corporate filings for foreign registrations; and court orders related to custody, adoption, or name changes.

How we guide you through the apostille process

We confirm the destination country, review the documents you already have, and explain whether they must be notarized first or obtained from a specific agency before an apostille can be issued. Then we outline realistic fees and timelines so you can decide how to proceed. For urgent cases, see our same‑day apostille service.
