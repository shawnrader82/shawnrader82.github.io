International documents

Apostille help for Santa Monica, Venice & Marina del Rey

Many Westside clients have work, property, or family overseas, so documents signed here often need a California apostille before they are accepted abroad.

Documents that may require an apostille

  • Powers of attorney for property or bank accounts in another country.
  • Business and contract documents for international clients or employers.
  • Birth, Marriage, and Death certificates used for citizenship, marriage, or family law abroad.
  • School transcripts and diplomas for students studying or working overseas.

How we guide you through the process

  • Confirm which country will receive the paperwork and what form they require.
  • Explain whether your documents must be notarized, certified, or reissued before an apostille.
  • Outline realistic fees and timelines so you can plan around travel, school, or court dates.
  • Coordinate secure return delivery to you, your attorney, or an out‑of‑country contact.

Jail‑related notarizations for Westside families

1. We review your documents and facility details.

You can send photos of your powers of attorney, jail marriage forms, or court documents along with your location and the facility name. We confirm what is needed, who must be present, and any rules that apply.

Questions from local clients

Santa Monica, Venice & Marina del Rey notary FAQ

Do you charge extra to come to the beach cities?

Travel fees depend on your exact location, time of day, parking, and whether a hospital or jail visit is involved. We always quote the full cost before you book so there are no surprises.

Can you meet in a coworking space, hotel, or rental?

Yes. We frequently meet clients in coworking spaces, offices, hotels, and short‑term rentals throughout Santa Monica, Venice, and Marina del Rey when that is the most practical and private place to sign.

Can you work around school and commute schedules?

We understand Westside traffic and school commitments. We will suggest appointment windows that are realistic for your location and timing.

How do we know if an apostille will be required?

If your documents will be used in another country, they may need a California apostille or consular legalization. We can review your situation and confirm the right path before you invest time and money.

What identification do signers need?

Each signer must have valid, government‑issued photo ID such as a driver's license, state ID, or passport, along with all pages of the documents that need to be signed. We are happy to answer ID questions before we schedule.
