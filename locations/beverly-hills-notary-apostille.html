International documents

Apostille help for Beverly Hills estates & businesses

Many Beverly Hills matters involve property, accounts, or family overseas, so documents signed here often need a California apostille before they are accepted abroad.

Documents that may require an apostille

  • Estate and trust documents, powers of attorney, and inheritance paperwork.
  • Corporate resolutions, board minutes, and international business contracts.
  • Birth, marriage, and death certificates used for citizenship, marriage, or probate abroad.
  • School and professional records for study, licensing, or work in another country.

How we guide you through the process

  • Confirm the destination country and whether it accepts a standard apostille or needs consular steps.
  • Review your documents to see if they must be notarized, certified, or reissued first.
  • Outline realistic fees and timelines around court dates, closings, or travel plans.
  • Coordinate secure return delivery to you, your attorney, or overseas contacts.

When a signer is in custody

Jail & detention notarizations for Beverly Hills families

1. We review your documents and facility.

You send photos of your powers of attorney, jail marriage forms, or court documents along with the inmate’s name, booking number, and facility. We confirm what is needed, who must be present, and any rules that apply.

2. We schedule a visit from Beverly Hills.

We coordinate travel to Pitchess Detention Center or Downtown Los Angeles jails such as Men’s Central or Twin Towers, explain expected timing, and quote travel and service fees before you decide to move forward.

3. We complete the notarization and any follow‑up.

At the facility we verify ID, witness signatures, and notarize your documents. If the paperwork will be used in another country, we can also arrange a California apostille and secure delivery once everything is completed.

Beverly Hills notary FAQ

Do you charge extra to come to Beverly Hills?

Travel fees depend on your exact location, time of day, parking or building access, and whether a hospital or jail visit is involved. We always quote the full cost before you book so there are no surprises.

Can you meet in our home, office, or medical suite?

Yes. We regularly meet clients in private homes, law and finance offices, medical and dental practices, and other professional suites throughout Beverly Hills and nearby Century City.

Can you work with my attorney or advisor?

We are happy to coordinate with your attorney, financial advisor, or family‑office team to make sure documents are prepared correctly and signed in the right order before we arrive.

How do we know if an apostille will be required?

If your documents will be used in another country, they may need a California apostille or consular legalization. We can review your situation and confirm the right path before you invest time and money.

What identification do signers need?

Each signer must have valid, government‑issued photo ID such as a driver's license, state ID, or passport, along with all pages of the documents that need to be signed. We are happy to answer ID questions before we schedule.
