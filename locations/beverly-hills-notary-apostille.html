Apostille help for Beverly Hills & Century City

When Beverly Hills documents need an apostille

Many Beverly Hills families, business owners, and international clients maintain ties to other countries, so it is common for documents first signed in local homes, offices, or law firms to require a California apostille so they are accepted abroad without delays.

Documents that may require an apostille

Typical requests include birth, marriage, and death certificates; school records and transcripts; powers of attorney for foreign property or investments; corporate documents for international filings; and court orders involving custody, adoption, or name changes.

How we guide you through the apostille process

We confirm the destination country, review the documents you already have, and explain whether they must be notarized first or obtained from a specific agency before an apostille can be issued. We then outline realistic fees and timelines before you decide to move forward. For urgent matters, see our same‑day apostille service.

How do we know if an apostille will be required?

If your documents will be used in another country, they may need a California apostille or consular legalization. We can review your situation and confirm the right path before you invest time and money.

Each signer must have valid, government‑issued photo ID such as a driver's license, state ID, or passport, along with all pages of the documents that need to be signed. We are happy to answer ID questions before we schedule.
