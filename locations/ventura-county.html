About this county

What "Ventura County" means for your document

Ventura County sits directly west of Los Angeles County along the 101 corridor. From our Granada Hills base, the Conejo Valley and Simi Valley are reachable in under an hour in normal traffic. California Notary and Apostille rules don't change at the county line, but travel, parking, and which county office issues your underlying records do.

Same California rules, different drive

A California-commissioned Notary follows the same statute and journal rules in Thousand Oaks as in Los Angeles. What changes is travel time, the 23 / 101 corridor, and where we stage between appointments.

County-issued records come from Ventura

Ventura County birth, death, and marriage certificates are issued by the Ventura County Clerk-Recorder. For most apostille purposes, you need a certified copy with a wet-ink clerk signature before the California Secretary of State step.

County records & apostille

Ventura County vital records and Apostille

Many Apostille requests start with a Ventura County document — a birth certificate, a death certificate, or a marriage record. Here's how the pieces fit together so you don't order the wrong copy.

Birth, death, marriage

Ventura County issues certified copies through the Ventura County Clerk-Recorder in the city of Ventura. For Apostille purposes, you generally need a certified copy with a wet-ink signature from the county clerk — not a copy you notarize yourself.

More: vital records Apostille overview.

Divorce decrees

Divorce judgments come from the Ventura County Superior Court, not the Clerk-Recorder. You'll typically need a certified court copy with a wet-ink clerk's signature before the California Secretary of State can apostille it.

More: divorce decree Apostille.

State-level vital records

For births, deaths, or marriages issued outside Ventura County, the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) issues state-level certified copies that also work for Apostille — usually with longer processing times than county copies.

What we handle

Once you have the right certified copy, we coordinate the California Secretary of State Apostille step. For documents that aren't government-issued (powers of attorney, affidavits, corporate records, school records), we handle the Notary step first and then the Apostille.
