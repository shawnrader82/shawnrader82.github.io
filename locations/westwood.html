Apostille help for Westwood

When documents may need an apostille

Westwood clients often handle international family, education, business, and property matters, so documents signed at local homes, offices, or medical settings may later need a California apostille before they can be accepted abroad.

Documents that commonly require an apostille

Typical requests include birth, marriage, and death certificates, powers of attorney for overseas use, court and family documents, school or academic records, and business paperwork that will be presented in another country.

How we guide you through the apostille process

We confirm the destination country, review the documents you already have, and explain whether they must be notarized first, reissued, or certified through another agency before an apostille can be attached.
