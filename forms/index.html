Available Form Templates

Philippine Special Power of Attorney (SPA) →

Who uses this: Filipino nationals and dual citizens in Los Angeles authorizing an agent in the Philippines.

For real estate transactions, bank matters, and legal filings in the Philippines. Uses the official template from the Philippine Consulate General Los Angeles.

California Statutory Power of Attorney →

Who uses this: California residents authorizing an agent to act on their behalf.

The statutory financial POA form recognized under California Probate Code §4401. Used for finances, real estate, and general business affairs.

California Advance Health Care Directive →

Who uses this: California residents planning ahead for medical incapacity, including hospital and hospice patients.

The official directive for medical decisions when you cannot speak for yourself. Includes power of attorney for health care and end-of-life instructions.

How to Use This Library

  1. Pick your form. Choose the template that matches your situation. Every form links to the official government or consular source so you always work from the authoritative version.
  2. Fill it out yourself (or with an attorney if the situation is complex). We cannot draft, edit, or recommend content on any form — California law prohibits notaries from acting as attorneys.
  3. Call or text us when you are ready to sign. We come to your home, office, hospital, or facility. If you need the form printed on the spot, we can bring a laptop and printer.
  4. Sign in front of us. We verify your identity, witness the signature, and complete the notary certificate. Done.
