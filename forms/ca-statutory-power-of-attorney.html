Frequently Asked Questions

The answers below describe our notary service and cite publicly available statutory or agency information. They are not legal advice. For questions about which form to use, what to write in it, or how the statute applies to your situation, please consult a licensed California attorney.

Do you notarize California Statutory Powers of Attorney?
Yes. Once the form is completed and you are ready to sign, we can notarize your acknowledgment as a California Notary Public across Los Angeles, Orange, and Ventura Counties.
Can you help me decide whether to use the statutory form or a custom POA?
No. Choosing which form of power of attorney fits your situation is a legal question. Please consult a licensed California attorney. We can notarize whichever form you and your attorney decide to use.
Can you help me fill out the form?
No. Under California law, a notary may not draft, complete, or advise on the content of a document. If you have questions about which categories to initial, whom to name as agent, or what special instructions to include, please consult a licensed California attorney.
If you need the POA recorded at a county recorder, can you handle recording?
Yes. Once the POA is notarized, we can take it to the appropriate county recorder through our Document Recording service. Whether recording is required or advisable in your particular situation is a legal and title-practice question we cannot answer — please direct that to your attorney or the title company involved.
Can you notarize a POA at a hospital, care facility, or private home?
Yes. Mobile bedside notarization is a routine part of our service across Los Angeles, Orange, and Ventura Counties. The signer must be able to communicate directly with the notary, present valid ID, and sign without assistance.
Can I revoke a POA once it is notarized?
Revocation of a power of attorney is governed by California Probate Code Division 4.5 and by any specific revocation language in the POA itself. Please consult a licensed California attorney about how to revoke effectively in your specific situation. We can notarize a written revocation once you have prepared it.
