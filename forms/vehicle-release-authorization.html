Frequently Asked Questions

The answers below describe our notary service. They are not legal advice. For questions about which form to use, what to write in it, or whether a specific facility will accept this template, please consult a licensed California attorney or contact the facility directly.

Do you notarize vehicle release authorization letters at LA County jails?
Yes. Notarization of vehicle release authorization letters is a routine part of our jail inmate notary service across Los Angeles County jails including Men's Central Jail, Twin Towers, and Pitchess Detention Center. Once your form is completed and the signer is ready to sign in front of a Notary Public, we can notarize the signature.
Will every tow yard accept this template?
No. Different impound facilities have different requirements. Some LAPD Official Police Garages, LASD contract tow yards, and CHP impound lots accept a simple notarized letter identifying the vehicle and the pickup person. Others require their own form, a full power of attorney, or additional documents such as proof of insurance, valid registration, or a driver's license matching the pickup person. Before your appointment, please confirm with the specific tow yard or Official Police Garage holding the vehicle what documents they require and whether a notarized authorization letter is sufficient in their policy. This is not a question a Notary can answer for you.
Can you help me fill out the form?
No. Under California law, a Notary may not draft, complete, or advise on the content of a document. We can notarize the signature once the form is completed and the signer is ready to sign. If you have questions about what to write in a specific blank, please consult a licensed California attorney or contact the tow yard or Official Police Garage directly for their guidance.
How is this different from a power of attorney?
A power of attorney is a broader legal instrument covering many possible categories of authority. A vehicle release authorization letter is narrower in scope and is typically used to authorize one specific person to retrieve one specific vehicle. Whether either instrument is the right choice for a given situation is a legal question. Please consult a licensed California attorney or the receiving tow yard or Official Police Garage.
Do you provide the notary certificate wording?
We attach the California acknowledgment or California jurat wording you specify. Which wording is required for your document is a legal question — please confirm with your attorney or the receiving tow yard before your appointment.
Do you travel to jails for these signings?
Yes. Jail inmate notary service is one of our regular offerings across Los Angeles, Orange, and Ventura Counties. Vehicle release authorization letters are one of the most common documents we notarize at jails. See our jail inmate notary page for how in-custody signings work.
