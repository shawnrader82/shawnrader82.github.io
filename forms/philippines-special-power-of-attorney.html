Frequently Asked Questions

The answers below describe our notary service and cite publicly available statutory or agency information. They are not legal advice. For questions about which form to use, what to write in it, or how the statute applies to your situation, please consult a licensed California attorney.

Do you notarize Philippine SPAs?
Yes. Once your SPA form is filled out and you are ready to sign, we can notarize your signature as a California Notary Public. We come to you across Los Angeles, Orange, and Ventura Counties, including hospital bedside signings.
Can you Apostille the notarized SPA afterward?
Yes. Once we notarize the SPA, we can route it to the California Secretary of State for an Apostille. Turnaround varies by workload at the LA Regional Office and the Sacramento office. See our Apostille Los Angeles page for current turnaround.
Can you help me fill out the SPA?
No. Under California law, a notary may not draft, complete, or advise on the content of a document. If you have questions about which acts to authorize, how to describe the property, or which options apply to your situation, please consult a licensed attorney or the receiving institution in the Philippines.
Do multiple family members sign in one visit?
Yes, we frequently notarize signatures for multiple family members in a single visit (for example, siblings authorizing an SPA together). Each signer must present a valid, unexpired, government-issued photo ID and must sign in front of the notary.
Do you notarize Philippine SPAs at hospitals or care facilities?
Yes. Hospital bedside notary service is one of our regular offerings across Los Angeles, Orange, and Ventura Counties. See our hospital notary page for how bedside signings work.
Do you provide the notary certificate wording?
We attach the California acknowledgment or California jurat wording you specify. Which wording is required for your document is a legal question — please confirm with your attorney or the receiving institution before your appointment.
